The [WPC introduced the Qi2.2 standard](https://www.wirelesspowerconsortium.com/knowledge-base/specifications/history-of-the-qi-specifications/) in April 2025, but the [rollout of faster wireless chargers](https://www.theverge.com/news/708060/qi-2-2-certification-25w-wpc-belkin-anker-ugreen-aukey) has been relatively slow. Following companies like [Nomad](https://www.theverge.com/news/813769/nomad-qi2-stand-one-max-wireless-charging-25w-magsafe-pixelsnap), [Belkin](https://www.theverge.com/news/763659/belkin-qi2-2-25w-wireless-charging-ultracharge-pro), and [Kuxiu](https://www.theverge.com/news/815808/kuxiu-k1-ultra-magnetic-magsafe-power-bank-qi2-25w-wireless-charging), Satechi has announced a new charger that supports Qi2.2. The 3-in-1 Foldable Wireless Charging Stand is an updated version of an [existing product](https://satechi.com/products/3-in-1-foldable-qi2-wireless-charging-stand/); the new incarnation boosts wireless charging speeds up to 25W for devices that support it, including iPhone 16 and 17 models and the [Pixel 10 Pro XL](https://www.theverge.com/reviews/766613/google-pixel-10-pro-review-ai-magic-cue-camera-pro-res-zoom).

Available now through [Satechi’s online store](https://satechi.com/products/3-in-1-foldable-wireless-charging-stand-with-qi2-25w) and [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/d/B0GNRSY36V?tag=theverge02-20) for $129.99, the 3-in-1 charger is the same price as the older Qi2 version but features an updated design with corners and hard edges that have all been rounded off. The main adjustable charging pad that supports 25W rates is the most notable upgrade to the charger’s functionality. The other built-in chargers — an Apple Watch fast charger on the back and an extra Qi pad on the base for charging AirPods and other wireless earbuds — have been carried over from the previous model.

[![Satechi’s 3-in-1 charger folded down and placed in a suitcase atop clothing.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/04/satechi2.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/04/satechi2.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

While it’s primarily designed as a way to charge multiple devices on your desk or nightstand from a single outlet using an included 45W USB-C power adapter, the stand can also fold down for travel. However, when flattened it makes for a fairly large travel accessory. If you want a multi-device charger that’s optimized for portability and travel, [Kuxiu’s $99.99 X40 Turbo stand](https://www.theverge.com/tech/779884/kuxius-x40-turbo-lays-claim-to-best-3-in-1-travel-charger) folds down much smaller and is still capable of charging your Apple Watch, earbuds, and a phone at 25W.
