Instagram will no longer support end-to-end encrypted messages starting May 8th. In a statement to _The Verge_, Meta spokesperson Dina El-Kassaby Luce says the platform is discontinuing the feature because “very few people” were using E2EE in their DMs.

Instagram has begun notifying impacted users about the change inside its app, and has also [posted an update to its support page](https://help.instagram.com/491565145294150/?helpref=uf_share), suggesting that users download E2EE chats and images before it discontinues the feature, [as spotted earlier by _PiunikaWeb_](https://piunikaweb.com/2026/03/13/instagram-to-stop-supporting-end-to-end-encrypted-dms-after-may-8/). “Anyone who wants to keep messaging with end-to-end encryption can easily do that on WhatsApp,” El-Kassaby Luce adds.

After bringing E2EE to WhatsApp and Messenger, [Meta began rolling out the feature to Instagram in 2023](https://www.theverge.com/2023/1/23/23567537/messenger-e2ee-chats-themes-emoji-meta-facebook), preventing third parties — including Meta — from viewing your messages. But as regulators around the globe [pressure social platforms](https://www.theverge.com/policy/890367/house-lawmakers-online-safety-bills-kids-act) to ramp up child safety features, E2EE has become one of their targets. In 2024, the Nevada Attorney General filed a [motion to ban Meta from offering E2EE to minors](https://www.zetter-zeroday.com/nevada-ag-asks-court-to-ban-meta-from-providing-end-to-end-encryption-to-minors/), while [New Mexico’s AG recently accused the company](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/20/meta-apple-child-safety-zuckerberg-cook.html) of knowing E2EE “would make its platforms less safe by preventing it from detecting and reporting child sexual exploitation.”
