Google is bringing a new desktop mode to its Pixel phones and tablets. The feature comes as part of [Google’s March Pixel drop](https://blog.google/products-and-platforms/devices/pixel/march-2026-pixel-drop/), allowing users with a Pixel 8 and newer to connect their device to an external monitor via USB-C for a “desktop-like multi-window experience.”

You can use a mouse and keyboard with the new desktop mode, which also works with the Pixel 9 Pro Fold and the Pixel 10 Pro Fold. The Pixel Tablet is also getting a desktop windowing feature that Google says will offer a “familiar interface to arrange and resize overlapping windows.”

[![Google showed off what Android’s desktop mode might look like last year.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/06/external-display-connected-to-desktop-session-material-design.gif?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/06/external-display-connected-to-desktop-session-material-design.gif?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

Though Google’s announcement doesn’t include any images of what these new experiences will look like, the company has been teasing a desktop experience for Android for months now. Last May, [Google said that it’s working with Samsung](https://www.theverge.com/news/671577/google-android-desktop-mode-samsung-dex) to build on the [company’s DeX feature](https://www.theverge.com/2017/5/2/15495036/samsung-dex-station-galaxy-s8-review-desktop-dock), which automatically changes your phone’s layout when connected to a larger display. Google also started letting users with a Pixel 8 and up [test out these features in June](https://www.theverge.com/news/684994/android-16-material-3-expressive-redesign-not-ready).
