AltStore PAL, the alternative iOS app store available in the European Union and Japan, is joining the social web. In [a post on Wednesday](https://fosstodon.org/@altstore/116211352248122697), AltStore PAL announced that users across Mastodon, Threads, and Bluesky can now interact with apps that developers choose to federate on its [explore.alt.store website](https://explore.alt.store/public/local).

Any likes from the social web will appear on AltStore PAL, according to [creator Riley Testut, who shared last October](https://www.theverge.com/news/693512/apple-eu-dma-app-store-concessions) that “you’ll be able to comment on an app on Mastodon, like a news update on Threads, then open AltStore and view all these same interactions in-app.” Users can also now sign into the AltStore PAL with their Mastodon or Bluesky accounts.

[![Three fediverse-focused apps are coming to the AltStore PAL.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/AltStorePAL2.3NewApps.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/AltStorePAL2.3NewApps.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

“Our aim with adding fediverse features to AltStore is to improve the discoverability of apps by making them shareable on the social web,” Testut tells _The Verge_. “In the future, we’d love for apps to have native support within ActivityPub and become a standard ActivityPub object like notes and articles, allowing them to appear like any other item in your feed.”

AltStore PAL launched in the EU in 2024 after Apple began complying with the region’s Digital Markets Act (DMA), which requires the company to allow users to download third-party app stores. It later arrived in Japan after the [country implemented similar laws](https://www.theverge.com/news/847149/apple-third-party-app-stores-payment-processing-ios-japan), and it’s coming to [Australia, Brazil, and the UK, too](https://www.theverge.com/news/795173/altstore-alternative-ios-iphone-app-store-more-countries-fediverse-social-web).

[![You can browse new collections of apps, too.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/AltStorePAL2.3SourceCollections.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/AltStorePAL2.3SourceCollections.png?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

Along with this update, AltStore PAL is adding three fediverse-friendly apps to its store. That includes the open-source short-form video platform [Loops](https://joinloops.org/), the video-sharing app [PeerTube](https://joinpeertube.org/), and the Mastodon client [iPhanpy](https://fantinel.dev/blog/iphanpy-mastodon-client). You can access these new apps from the store’s redesigned Sources page, which now features collections of apps in categories like “popular,” “indie gems,” and “fediverse favorites.”
