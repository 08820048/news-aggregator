Apple’s iOS 27 update will allow users to choose the AI chatbot they want to link with Siri. That’s according to a [report from _Bloomberg_’s Mark Gurman](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-26/apple-plans-to-open-up-siri-to-rival-ai-assistants-beyond-chatgpt-in-ios-27?srnd=phx-technology), who says third-party chatbots downloaded from the App Store, like Google’s Gemini or Anthropic’s Claude, will be able to fetch replies for Siri — similar to how the voice assistant already [works with OpenAI’s ChatGPT](https://www.theverge.com/2024/6/10/24174786/apple-openai-partnership-chatgpt-wwdc).

The new system, reportedly called “Extensions,” will give users the ability to enable or disable the chatbots they want to connect with Siri on the iPhone, iPad, and Mac, _Bloomberg_ reports. The upcoming integrations will also work with the [standalone app Apple reportedly plans](https://www.theverge.com/tech/899801/apple-wwdc-2026-new-siri-apple-intelligence-standalone-app) on launching for its AI-upgraded version of Siri, which is supposed to take action on your behalf across apps.

Following [several setbacks](https://www.theverge.com/news/835466/apple-ai-chief-john-giannandrea-steps-down-siri), Apple [revealed in January that it’s working](https://www.theverge.com/news/860521/apple-siri-google-gemini-ai-personalization) with Google to power its overhauled Siri. _The Information_ reported this week that the deal also includes the ability for Apple to [use Gemini to train smaller AI models](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/901014/apples-deal-with-google-lets-it-use-gemini-to-train-smaller-ai-models).

Apple is expected to reveal the newest versions of its operating systems during its [Worldwide Developers Conference set to kick off on June 8th](https://www.theverge.com/news/898997/apple-wwdc-2026-june-8-ios-27-ai-siri).
