Before [Apple announced the new MacBook Air](https://www.theverge.com/tech/888091/apple-march-2026-event-macbook-air-pro-announcement) on Tuesday, the M4-powered MacBook Air was the model we recommended for most people. Even with the introduction of the cheaper [MacBook Neo](https://www.theverge.com/tech/888712/macbook-neo-laptop-iphone-chip-apple-event-photos-price-features), the Air is still the better choice if you want more power. We haven’t tested the new M5 version yet, but the changes between the two Air models appear relatively minor on paper, so the overall experience is likely to feel very similar. No matter which MacBook Air you choose, you’ll get a thin, lightweight laptop that’s more than powerful enough to handle everyday work and play, and even some light gaming or video editing. Both also offer excellent battery life that should easily last well over a full workday, along with a 12-megapixel Center Stage webcam.
