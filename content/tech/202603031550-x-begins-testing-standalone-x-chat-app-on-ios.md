Social network X is bringing its private messaging service, dubbed X Chat, to a standalone app. The company on Monday said the [initial beta](https://t.co/x45kk97r2H) of the X Chat app was being made available to thousands of early adopters through Apple’s TestFlight testing platform.

The beta program had reached full capacity two hours after the announcement. According to an [X post](https://x.com/mjboswell/status/2028668731858284749) by xAI product designer Michael Boswell, the company will expand the beta beyond the initial 1,000 users “soon.” (After publication, Boswell [announced](https://x.com/mjboswell/status/2028873788482294218?s=20) the beta was expanding to 5,000 users.)

“For the past few months, we’ve been quietly building a standalone X Chat app for iOS,” Boswell wrote on X. “Use it. Break it. We want your feedback,” he said.

X Chat is X’s upgraded version of X’s existing DM (direct messages) feature, which the company claims is end-to-end encrypted. Security experts, however, [have warned potential users](https://techcrunch.com/2025/09/05/x-is-now-offering-me-end-to-end-encrypted-chat-you-probably-shouldnt-trust-it-yet/) that the X Chat service is less secure than other encrypted messaging apps, like Signal, and should not be trusted. It’s not clear whether the company has addressed the security community’s concerns in the standalone app.

The introduction of the app represents a decided shift away from X owner Elon Musk’s earlier plan to make X an “[everything app](https://techcrunch.com/2022/10/08/elon-musk-x-everything-wechat-tesla-app/),” that would provide access to messaging, payments, creator content, and more, centralizing all aspects of the service.

A handful of early testers have begun sharing [screenshots](https://x.com/GarrettEBorn/status/2028658214003429742) of the app’s interface. One user said the beta version of the app had [stylized](https://x.com/techdevnotes/status/2028849397065240699) the name as xChat instead of X Chat, which could suggest a potential rebranding. Others began sharing screenshots from the app, like the [login screen](https://x.com/SawyerMerritt/status/2028646997352165389?s=20), which has a starry background.

Early users describe the app as a simple, smoother interface for using X Chat compared with the X app. The current version of the app doesn’t include message requests, but Boswell [says](https://x.com/mjboswell/status/2028705694585606552) that’s being rebuilt now. Other features [still in the works](https://x.com/mjboswell/status/2028874492148093117?s=20) include Verified badges and support for making calling, among other things.

xAI’s Grok responded to questions about the new app on X, confirming other details, like the app’s [age rating](https://x.com/grok/status/2028691673568481676?s=20), which will be the same as X (17+ on the iOS App Store), and that chats [would](https://x.com/grok/status/2028690757083611233) sync to the X app and the [chat.x.com web app](https://t.co/4RxHgA2wuN), which launched in December 2025.

The AI chatbot also [noted](https://x.com/grok/status/2028669872473841926) that an Android version of the X Chat app was expected “very soon.”
