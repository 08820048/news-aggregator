Numerous robotaxis operated by Chinese tech giant Baidu froze in a major city on Tuesday, [reportedly](https://www.wired.com/story/robotaxi-outage-in-china-leaves-passengers-stuck-in-cars-on-highways/) trapping passengers inside, stranding them on highways, and causing at least one accident in snarled traffic.

Police in Wuhan [confirmed](https://www.wsj.com/business/autos/baidus-apollo-go-robotaxis-stall-in-chinas-wuhan-a9b143ad?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqdXqKfhF249d_igjLuqLB_jaDXOeS-bCd2J2YpiqfM32h2GexPQC-gvHS-S1T0%3D&gaa_ts=69cce8c4&gaa_sig=zzFxk7kh7UvuBHX6zaCAuT1WfVKyb9b5r3tHHTtsXl_SGmComAeS5mCdWQrqstZCDQFPhWznjBJxrto19n1C5A%3D%3D) receiving multiple reports of Baidu’s Apollo Go robotaxis stopping in the middle of streets and being unable to move. Police said no injuries have been reported and that preliminary investigations suggest an unspecified “system failure” is responsible for the outage.

Wuhan is a major robotaxi hub for Baidu, which has [reportedly](https://www.france24.com/en/live-news/20260401-chinese-robotaxis-stall-in-apparent-malfunction-police) deployed more than 500 driverless cars on its roads. It’s unclear how many vehicles malfunctioned. Local news reports [cited](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/baidu-robotaxi-outage-wuhan-caused-by-system-failure-police-say-2026-04-01/) by _Reuters_ suggest at least 100 robotaxis were affected. Baidu did not immediately respond to _The Verge_’s request for comment.

The incident has reignited debate over the safety of self-driving cars in China, one of the world’s most enthusiastic adopters of the technology, amid an [aggressive global expansion](https://www.theverge.com/2024/11/22/24303299/baidu-apollo-go-rt6-robotaxi-unit-economics-waymo). Baidu is a major operator, [deploying](https://ir.baidu.com/static-files/7004dcea-6dcf-4a4d-be0d-f6fa1eb2695f) robotaxis in 26 cities worldwide, including partnering with Uber in [London](https://www.theverge.com/news/707347/uber-baidu-robotaxi-deal-autonomous-ridehail) and [Dubai](https://www.theverge.com/transportation/876288/uber-to-do-baidu-robotaxis-in-dubai).
