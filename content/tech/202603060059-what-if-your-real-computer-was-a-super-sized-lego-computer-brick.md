In 1979 — nearly 50 years ago — Lego jazzed up [its very first spaceships](https://www.theverge.com/23173235/lego-galaxy-explorer-90th-anniversary-birthday-price-release-date) with an iconic sloped computer brick that was just painted plastic. In 2022, we introduced you to [the engineer who fit an actual tiny computer inside](https://www.theverge.com/2022/8/20/23308217/lego-brick-computer-james-brown).

Now, industrial designer Paul Staal has flipped the script with a working desktop PC that’s a super-sized homage to the classic computer brick (via [Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/cyberDeck/comments/1rjozwu/the_m2x2_a_3dprinted_101scale_lego_computer_brick/) and [_Hackster.io_](https://www.hackster.io/news/the-m2x2-turns-a-classic-lego-brick-into-a-real-computer-b350a57736db.amp)).

[![You can play with this 3D model at Staal’s site.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/m2x2.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/m2x2.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

It’s ten times bigger than the original:

[![Size comparison.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/the-m2x2-a-3d-printed-10-1-scale-lego-computer-brick-v0-9xv99wg3wtmg1.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/the-m2x2-a-3d-printed-10-1-scale-lego-computer-brick-v0-9xv99wg3wtmg1.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

The “M2x2” is largely a 3D-printed case for a Mac Mini — one you can [freely print at home yourself](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2469337-the-m2x2-a-mac-mini-m4-case#profileId-2710965) — but it’s not just a shell. He’s outfitted it with a 7-inch IPS touchscreen display, and a full array of additional ports and SD card reader thanks to an integrated USB-C hub.

[![The volume knob and a visualizer.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260302_111548.BrlaSEqW_1TM4R2.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260302_111548.BrlaSEqW_1TM4R2.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

The big studs on top are functional, too! The left one contains a hidden [Ikea Symfonisk remote control](https://www.theverge.com/2019/10/1/20892984/ikea-sound-controller-review-symfonisk) so he can control his Sonos speaker, while the right has a hidden wireless charger for his AirPods. Both have normal Lego-style studs on top, too, so you can connect some classic bricks or a minifig.

[![A mockup running on the screen.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260302_112030.CCEXbm1r_28SY8A.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260302_112030.CCEXbm1r_28SY8A.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

Around back, the kit has an integrated carry handle and easy-wrap cord hangers.

[![Cord wrap.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_135800.Ht1NaaJU_2iS40p.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_135800.Ht1NaaJU_2iS40p.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

[At his stunning interactive website](https://www.wattiv.nl/work/m2x2/), Staal says he’s currently using the M2x2 as his primary computer, though he’s mostly using its screen as a Home Assistant dashboard he can tap to control his home. He has a primary monitor plugged into it, an [LG DualUp](https://www.theverge.com/23273991/lg-dualup-tall-pc-windows-mac-monitor-review), to get work done.

[![](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_140809.DsQLHJoA_5Io2p_72614b.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=7.775%2C0%2C84.45%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_140809.DsQLHJoA_5Io2p_72614b.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=7.775,0,84.45,100)

[![](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_142532.DN3gkcHf_Zp6aQ.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=7.775%2C0%2C84.45%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/20260214_142532.DN3gkcHf_Zp6aQ.webp?quality=90&strip=all&crop=7.775,0,84.45,100)

If you’ve already got the Mac Mini, he estimates [the rest should cost you](https://makerworld.com/en/models/2469337-the-m2x2-a-mac-mini-m4-case#profileId-2710965) less than €100 (roughly $116), and he has guided step-by-step assembly instructions at his site with excellent photos. Do note that the Ikea/Sonos Symfonisk products [are discontinued](https://www.theverge.com/news/661491/sonos-ikea-symfonisk-discontinued), and the knob in particular is from an older generation of Ikea product. I still see some on eBay, but you might want a different knob solution.
