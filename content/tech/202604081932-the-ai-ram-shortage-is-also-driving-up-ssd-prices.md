I thought the WD Black SN850X 2TB SSD in my gaming PC was pricey when I bought it for $173 in 2024, but now [that same SSD costs $649](https://pcpartpicker.com/product/sfwypg/western-digital-wd_black-sn850x-wheatsink-2-tb-m2-2280-pcie-40-x4-nvme-solid-state-drive-wds200t2xhe?history_days=180), more than what I paid for most of the parts in my PC combined. The price on my WD Black drive nearly quadrupled since November 2025, and consumer SSDs across the board are seeing similar increases, [much like with RAM](https://www.theverge.com/report/839506/ram-shortage-price-increases-pc-gaming-smartphones):

-   The 4TB version of the popular [Samsung 990 Pro SSD](https://pcpartpicker.com/product/RKYmP6/samsung-990-pro-4-tb-m2-2280-pcie-40-x4-nvme-solid-state-drive-mz-v9p4t0bw?history_days=180) previously cost $320, but will now run you nearly $1,000.
-   External SanDisk SSDs saw [a 200 percent price hike](https://gizmodo.com/apple-store-prices-for-sandisk-ssds-are-suddenly-astronomical-2000736615) at the Apple Store in March.
-   Sony has announced that [it’s suspending orders](https://www.theverge.com/tech/902828/sony-sd-cfexpress-memory-card-shortage) for its SD and CFexpress cards.
-   Modular PC brand Framework has [warned its customers](https://www.theverge.com/tech/893038/framework-ram-storage-price-increases-march-2026) of more price hikes as it runs out of its existing storage inventory.
-   Even hard disks are feeling the squeeze — as of February, Western Digital and Seagate were [reportedly nearly sold out](https://www.rockpapershotgun.com/ssd-prices-in-yet-more-trouble-as-two-of-the-biggest-hard-drive-makers-have-already-sold-out-their-2026-stock) of all of their inventory for 2026.

According to price trends from [PC Part Picker](https://pcpartpicker.com/trends/price/internal-hard-drive/), NVMe SSD prices began ticking upward in December 2025, with prices on 256GB to 4TB SSDs now double or triple what they were just a few months ago, and continuing to climb.

Like with RAM, demand from the AI industry is swallowing up supply from a limited number of manufacturers, leading to a drastic reduction in the inventory that’s available to consumers. According to Counterpoint Research, the same few brands with the biggest [RAM market share](https://counterpointresearch.com/en/insights/global-dram-and-hbm-market-share) also [control the global NAND market](https://counterpointresearch.com/en/insights/global-nand-memory-market-share) — Samsung, SK Hynix, and Micron.

NAND flash is the type of memory used for SSDs, USB drives, and SD cards. It’s significantly faster and more energy-efficient than mechanical HDDs. As a result, NAND storage is more appealing for AI companies and consumers alike.

Even so, HDDs are more cost-effective for storing large amounts of data, like the huge datasets needed for AI, which could explain why HDD prices are [also on the rise](https://www.tomshardware.com/pc-components/hdds/hard-drive-prices-have-surged-by-an-average-of-46-percent-since-september-iconic-24tb-seagate-barracuda-now-usd500-as-ai-claims-another-victim). For instance, the [2TB Seagate Barracuda 3.5-inch HDD](https://camelcamelcamel.com/product/B07H2RR55Q?context=search) I have in my PC was $47 when I bought it a few years ago, but now costs $90.

All three leading NAND brands have been pulling in huge profits from the AI boom, profits the consumer market can’t compete with. Micron even [left the consumer RAM and SSD market](https://www.theverge.com/news/837594/crucial-ram-ssd-micron-ai) last year, which [briefly sparked rumors](https://wccftech.com/no-samsung-isnt-phasing-out-of-the-consumer-ssd-business/) that Samsung might do the same thing. As of March, Micron says it’s “seeing NAND demand significantly in excess of our available supply for the foreseeable future,” as its NAND revenue has [grown 169 percent year-over-year](http://investors.micron.com/static-files/9c0becf5-df56-4eec-bd67-453dda68b273).
