Fresh off launching the low-cost [MacBook Neo](https://www.theverge.com/tech/888712/macbook-neo-laptop-iphone-chip-apple-event-photos-price-features), Apple is reportedly preparing at least three new products that will fit into its highest-end “ultra” lineup. According to _[Bloomberg’s](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/newsletters/2026-03-08/apple-to-expand-ultra-lines-after-599-macbook-neo-3d-printed-aluminum-imacs-mmhpa12d?srnd=undefined)_ Mark Gruman, the next batch of releases may not bear the “ultra” name, like its Watch, but will all command price premiums over their mainline counterparts.

There’s the oft-rumored [foldable iPhone](https://www.theverge.com/news/850114/apples-foldable-gets-prossered), which is expected to cost around [$2,000](https://www.theverge.com/news/782366/foldable-details-iphone-air-ifixit-teardown), and a [touchscreen MacBook Pro](https://www.theverge.com/tech/884089/apple-touchscreen-macbook-pro-dynamic-island) is supposedly slated for the fall. Those are pretty straightforward plays for the higher end of the market. More interesting are the next-gen AirPods, which are rumored to [include cameras](https://www.theverge.com/tech/880293/apple-ai-hardware-smart-glasses-pin-airpods) to feed visual context to Siri. Since AirPods already use the Pro and Max branding, similar to Apple Silicon, a set of AirPods Ultra could very well be on the docket.

Between the Neo and [multiple foldables](https://www.theverge.com/tech/887202/apple-touchscreen-macbook-ipad-hybrid) in the works, it seems that Apple is simultaneously trying to go further up- and down-market.
