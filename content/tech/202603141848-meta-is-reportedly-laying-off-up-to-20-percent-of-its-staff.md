According to _Reuters_, Meta is looking to offset spending on [AI](https://www.theverge.com/news/869882/mark-zuckerberg-meta-earnings-q4-2025) and [data centers](https://www.theverge.com/news/869008/meta-data-centers-ad-campaign) with a massive round of layoffs. Sources familiar with the matter say the company could lay off as much as [20 percent](https://www.reuters.com/business/world-at-work/meta-planning-sweeping-layoffs-ai-costs-mount-2026-03-14/?utm_source=braze&utm_medium=notifications&utm_campaign=2025_engagement) of its staff, eliminating roughly 15,800 positions. That would be the largest series of layoffs at the company since it terminated 22,000 workers over just a few months between [November 2022](https://www.theverge.com/2022/11/9/23448926/meta-layoffs-2022) and [early 2023](https://about.fb.com/news/2023/03/mark-zuckerberg-meta-year-of-efficiency/).

Word of the potential downsizing comes after Meta signaled that it was all but [giving](https://www.theverge.com/news/861295/meta-reality-labs-layoffs-shift-to-wearables) up on [VR](https://www.theverge.com/tech/881647/meta-vr-mobile-metaverse-horizon-worlds) and the [Metaverse](https://www.theverge.com/tech/863209/meta-has-discontinued-its-metaverse-for-work-too), [slashing budgets](https://www.theverge.com/news/838186/meta-metaverse-budget-cuts-2026) and [closing studios](https://www.theverge.com/news/861420/meta-reality-labs-layoffs-vr-studios-twisted-pixel-sanzaru-armature). Instead, the company has been spending big to attract [AI talent](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/703929/meta-openai-anthropic-superintelligence-lab-ai-poaching-money), build [data centers](https://www.theverge.com/science/876555/meta-data-center-winter-power-outages-storm-ice), and acquire companies like [Moltbook](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/892178/meta-moltbook-acquisition-ai-agents).

Amid all this, the company has also repeatedly found itself in hot water over its [smart](https://www.theverge.com/policy/886348/meta-glasses-ice-doxxing-privacy) [glasses](https://www.theverge.com/tech/889637/meta-ai-smart-glasses-human-reviewers-kenya), [chatbots](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/892978/ai-chatbots-investigation-help-teens-plan-violence), and its [impact on teens](https://www.theverge.com/policy/893930/social-media-addiction-trial-los-angeles-zuckerberg-instagram-youtube).

When asked for comment, Meta spokesperson Andy Stone said, “This is speculative reporting about theoretical approaches.”
