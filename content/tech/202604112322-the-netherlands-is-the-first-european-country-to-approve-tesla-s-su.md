Dutch regulators, the [RDW, announced](https://www.rdw.nl/en/news/2026/rdw-explanation-of-european-type-approval-tesla-with-provisional-validity-in-the-netherlands) that after over a year and a half of testing, it has officially approved Tesla’s Full-Self Driving (FSD) Supervised. This makes the Netherlands the first European country to authorize the use of FSD on its roads. This could open the door to wider adoption throughout the EU. Tesla’s European headquarters is located in Amsterdam, so it’s only fitting that the country is the first to embrace the company’s FSD.

In a statement announcing the approval, the RDW said that, “Using driver assistance systems correctly makes a positive contribution to road safety because the driver is supported in their driving tasks; it is a supplement to the driver. Through continuous strict monitoring of the driver in the vehicle, the system is safer than other driver assistance systems.”

The update implementing FSD Supervised (version 2026.3.6) has [started rolling out](https://www.notateslaapp.com/news/3959/tesla-starts-rolling-out-fsd-supervised-in-the-netherlands) to a limited number of users. Drivers will need to watch a tutorial and take a quiz before self-driving can be enabled, which reminds people that FSD Supervised “does not make your vehicle autonomous. Do not become complacent.”

Tesla’s FSD has faced some [challenges](https://www.theverge.com/transportation/896607/ubers-former-head-of-self-driving-almost-died-using-teslas-fsd) in the US recently, including an [investigation](https://www.theverge.com/transportation/897303/tesla-full-self-driving-nhtsa-probe-march-2026) by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration that could lead to a recall.
