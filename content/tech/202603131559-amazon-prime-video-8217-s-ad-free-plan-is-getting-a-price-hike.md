Amazon Prime Video is raising the price of its ad-free plan in the US and adding several new features. Starting April 10th, [Prime Video’s ad-free tier will cost $4.99 per month](https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/entertainment/prime-video-ultra-ad-free-streaming-subscription) instead of $2.99, and include “exclusive” access to 4K/UHD streaming.

The price increase comes [two years after Amazon switched](https://www.theverge.com/2023/12/26/24015595/amazon-prime-video-ads-coming-january-29) all users on Prime Video — which comes as a perk with a standard Amazon Prime subscription — to an ad-supported plan and began charging extra for commercial-free streams. Now, the streamer is calling its ad-free tier Prime Video “Ultra,” and is adding support for up to five simultaneous streams (up from three), up to 100 downloads (up from 25), and Dolby Atmos.

It’s just the latest in a string of steady streaming price increases that have recently impacted [HBO Max](https://www.theverge.com/news/803431/hbo-max-streaming-price-increasing-bundle), [Apple TV](https://www.theverge.com/news/763486/apple-tv-plus-streaming-price-increase), [Paramount Plus](https://www.theverge.com/news/818019/paramount-plus-price-increase-us-2026), [Disney Plus](https://www.theverge.com/news/783489/disney-plus-streaming-subscription-price-increase), and [Crunchyroll](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/872468/crunchyroll-price-hike-raise-fan-mega-ultimate-tier). The hike also comes ahead of the Prime Video [debut of _Tomb Raider_](https://www.theverge.com/news/770357/amazon-lara-croft-tomb-raider-sophie-turner-series) and _[God of War](https://www.theverge.com/streaming/886008/amazon-god-of-war-kratos-image)_, two highly anticipated live-action adaptations [that follow its very good _Fallout_](https://www.theverge.com/24126474/fallout-review-amazon-prime-video) series.

In a post announcing the change, Amazon says putting premium features in ad-free streaming “requires significant investment,” adding that the structure “aligns with other major streaming services.” Prime Video notes that members on the ad-supported tier can continue to access HD/HDR streams, Dolby Vision, and an array of live sports streams.
