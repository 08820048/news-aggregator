On Wednesday, the White House posted [a video](https://x.com/WhiteHouse/status/2029307088808055083) of actual military strikes on Iran in the style usually seen in _Call of Duty_ highlight videos, and started the video with a clip from _Call of Duty_. The real-life footage of missiles and other munitions hitting targets in Iran shows clips seen in other Trump administration videos, like [this one](https://x.com/CENTCOM/status/2029219939102401017) posted to the U.S. Central Command X account.

As noted [by _The Washington Post’s_ Drew Harwell](https://bsky.app/profile/drewharwell.com/post/3mgbcif44xs2f), the animation at the start appears to be from _Call of Duty: Modern Warfare III_ when a player activates a killstreak reward that launches a nuclear strike. The administration has repeatedly used games as a basis for social media posts promoting its agenda. In September, the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) [shared](https://www.theverge.com/news/785288/pokemon-theo-von-dhs-ice) [a video montage of ICE raids](https://x.com/DHSgov/status/1970251208322621530) with the caption “Gotta Catch ‘Em All” set to the theme song from the original _Pokémon_ TV show. A DHS post recruiting ICE officers used [a _Halo_ image](https://x.com/DHSgov/status/1982819431894901043) with the text “DESTROY THE FLOOD.”

Activision and Xbox didn’t immediately reply to a request for comment.
