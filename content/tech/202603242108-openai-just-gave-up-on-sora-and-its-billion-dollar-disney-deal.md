On Tuesday afternoon, OpenAI announced “We’re saying goodbye to Sora,” the video generation tool that it launched at the end of 2024, and centered in a massive licensing deal with Disney only a few months ago. _[The Wall Street Journal](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/digital/openai-shutting-down-sora-ai-video-app-1236546187/)_ reported the move earlier, saying that OpenAI boss Sam Altman had informed staff that both the TikTok-like Sora app and API access for developers would be discontinued, with no plans to roll the feature into ChatGPT as had previously been [rumored](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/openai-plans-launch-sora-video-ai-chatgpt-strategy-shift?rc=v4bmzs).

According to _[The Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/business/digital/openai-shutting-down-sora-ai-video-app-1236546187/)_, as a result, the deal Disney announced in December, [saying it would invest $1 billion in OpenAI](https://www.theverge.com/news/842348/disney-openai-sora-chatgpt-images), license its characters for use within Sora, and send AI-generated videos into Disney Plus, is also coming to an end.

> [Sora](https://x.com/soraofficialapp/status/2036532795984715896):
> 
> We’re saying goodbye to Sora. To everyone who created with Sora, shared it, and built community around it: thank you. What you made with Sora mattered, and we know this news is disappointing.
> 
> We’ll share more soon, including timelines for the app and API and details on preserving your work. – The Sora Team

OpenAI hasn’t responded to a request for comment or otherwise explained the shift, but there have been signs that things are changing, following Altman’s [declaration of a “code red”](https://www.theverge.com/news/836212/openai-code-red-chatgpt) a few months ago over possible slippage of [ChatGPT vs. Google Gemini](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/899677/openai-google-gemini-ai-shopping-features).

Last week, the [_WSJ_ reported](https://www.wsj.com/tech/openai-plans-launch-of-desktop-superapp-to-refocus-simplify-user-experience-9e19931d) OpenAI is working on a ChatGPT [desktop “superapp”](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/897778/openai-chatgpt-codex-atlas-browser-superapp) to simplify its product lineup around Codex and its AI browser, which applications CEO Fidji Simo [commented on](https://x.com/fidjissimo/status/2034769466433913082), tweeting, “Companies go through phases of exploration and phases of refocus; both are critical. But when new bets start to work, like we’re seeing now with Codex, it’s very important to double down on them and avoid distractions. Really glad we’re seizing this moment.”
