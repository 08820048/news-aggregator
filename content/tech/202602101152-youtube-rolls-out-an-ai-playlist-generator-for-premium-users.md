YouTube has been toying around with using AI to create playlists for a while. In July 2024, the company was testing a feature to let people create [custom radio stations using prompts](https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/15/youtube-music-is-testing-an-ai-generated-radio-feature-and-adding-a-song-recognition-tool/) in the U.S.

Other rival streaming services like [Spotify](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/07/spotify-launches-personalized-ai-playlists-you-can-build-using-prompts/), [Amazon Music](https://techcrunch.com/2024/04/16/amazon-music-follows-spotify-with-an-ai-playlist-generator-of-its-own-maestro/) and [Deezer](https://techcrunch.com/2024/07/15/deezer-chases-spotify-and-amazon-music-with-its-own-ai-playlist-generator/), have also rolled out AI-powered playlist or radio creation features.

YouTube has been trying to make its Premium plan more attractive to users.
