Nintendo of America is suing the US government over President Trump’s tariffs and is demanding a “prompt refund, with interest” of any duties that it has paid, according to [a complaint](https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/27776121-nintendo-v-us-government/) filed in the US Court of International Trade, as reported previously [by _Aftermath_](https://aftermath.site/nintendo-tariffs-sue/).

The Supreme Court ruled last month that Trump’s use of the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA) to levy “reciprocal” tariffs was [illegal](https://www.theverge.com/policy/882227/scotus-trump-tariffs-ruling-imports-small-businesses-refunds), without specifying how any refunds might work.

Nintendo announced [launch details](https://www.theverge.com/news/634603/nintendo-switch-2-price-release-date-details) for the Switch 2, including a planned preorder date of April 9th, on the same day Trump [announced plans](https://www.theverge.com/news/642240/trump-tariffs-imports-apple-manufacturing) to implement broad tariffs on imports from many countries last year. It pushed back [Switch 2 preorders](https://www.theverge.com/news/643483/nintendo-switch-2-preorders-delayed-tariffs) in the US to “assess the potential impact of tariffs,” before [eventually launching as planned](https://www.theverge.com/news/643616/nintendo-switch-2-us-preorders-date), but with hiked prices for several accessories.

FedEx has also filed a lawsuit in the US Court of International Trade, asking for [a “full refund” of tariff payments](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/fedex-sues-trump-administration-for-full-refund-of-tariff-payments/). The company said that, if refunds are issued to the company, it will [refund customers](https://www.theverge.com/policy/886216/fedex-trump-tariff-refunds) for Trump’s tariffs to “the shippers and consumers who originally bore those charges.”
