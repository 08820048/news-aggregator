Netflix’s party-focused lineup of TV games just got three excellent new additions: Jackbox’s _Drawful 2_, _Fibbage 4_, and _Quiplash 3_, available as the [_Jackbox Party Essentials_ pack](https://go.skimresources.com/?id=1025X1701640&xs=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.netflix.com%2Ftudum%2Farticles%2Fjackbox-games-party-essentials-news) on the platform. Subscribers can access _Jackbox Party Essentials_ at no additional cost and use their phones as controllers to play.

The _Jackbox_ bundle seems like a natural fit for the platform; Netflix’s other TV games include multiplayer party versions of _Boggle_ and _Pictionary_, and people who have already played games in Jackbox’s other Party Packs will be familiar with using their phone as a controller. If you get some friends together to check out _Jackbox Party Essentials_, I recommend starting with _Quiplash 3_, which tasks players with making up answers to ridiculous prompts and voting on the best ones. The _Quiplash_ series has become a long-running staple in my family for holidays and big get-togethers because of how accessible and silly it is.

Since launching its TV games [last year](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/820289/netflix-casual-party-game-multiplayer-tetris-pictionary), Netflix has slowly been adding to [its lineup](https://go.skimresources.com/?id=1025X1701640&xs=1&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.netflix.com%2Ftudum%2Farticles%2Fnetflix-party-games-play-on-tv), including with [a Netflix version of _Overcooked_](https://www.theverge.com/streaming/890925/overcooked-now-with-a-demogorgon). Netflix still offers mobile titles for subscribers, too, and including [a collection of daily puzzle games](https://www.theverge.com/news/820124/netflix-puzzled-daily-puzzle-game-app-ios-android-tudum-web) and an app [dedicated just to kids games](https://www.theverge.com/entertainment/907293/netflix-playground-kids-games-app).
