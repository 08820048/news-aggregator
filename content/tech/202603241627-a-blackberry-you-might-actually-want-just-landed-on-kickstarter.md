When _The Verge_’s [Allison Johnson](https://www.theverge.com/authors/allison-johnson) went [hands-on with the company’s Titan 2 Elite phone](https://www.theverge.com/tech/888101/unihertz-titan-2-elite-qwerty-keyboard) at MWC 2026, Unihertz didn’t divulge pricing info. But the compact Android phone with a BlackBerry-inspired keyboard is now available for preorder through a [Kickstarter crowdfunding campaign](https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/jellyphone/titan-2-elite-the-5g-keyboard-phone-youve-been-waiting-for) and starts at $489, with a discount for early backers bringing it down to $389. It’s expected to ship as early as June 2026.

The Titan 2 Elite is a smaller and lighter version of [Unihertz’s Titan 2](https://www.theverge.com/news/691938/titan-2-smartphone-keyboard-blackberry-5g-android-15) that debuted last June as a modernized version of the [BlackBerry Passport](https://www.theverge.com/2014/9/24/6837943/blackberry-passport-review). The Titan 2 Elite’s design is more reminiscent of the [BlackBerry Bold](https://www.theverge.com/2011/11/15/2563080/blackberry-bold-9790-curve-9380-available-coming-weeks) lineup, but with a slightly boxier design to accommodate a 4.03-inch 120Hz AMOLED display. The Elite also features Android 16 with OS upgrades promised to Android 20, 5G connectivity with dual nano SIMs and eSIM, fingerprint and face unlock, a 4,050mAh battery, and a full QWERTY keyboard that can be used as a trackpad.

[![A photo of the edge of the Unihertz Titan 2 Elite showing how thick the phone is.](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/unihertz2.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0%2C0%2C100%2C100&w=2400)](https://platform.theverge.com/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/03/unihertz2.jpg?quality=90&strip=all&crop=0,0,100,100)

On the back of the Titan 2 Elite you’ll also find a pair of 50MP cameras with telephoto capabilities, and a front-facing 32MP holepunch camera in the upper left corner of the display.

Unihertz is offering two versions of its modernized BlackBerry. The standard Titan 2 Elite features a Dimensity 7400 processor with 12GB of RAM and 256GB of storage. The slightly more expensive Titan 2 Elite Pro, which instead starts at $579 (but is temporarily discounted to $479), has a faster Dimensity 8400 processor, a bump to 512GB of storage, and a 50MP telephoto camera upgraded with optical image stabilization capabilities.
