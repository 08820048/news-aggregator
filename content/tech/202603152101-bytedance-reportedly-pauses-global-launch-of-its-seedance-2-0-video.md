ByteDance has paused plans to launch its new AI video model globally, according to [a report in The Information](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/bytedance-suspends-launch-video-ai-model-copyright-disputes-hollywood).

The Chinese company, best known as TikTok’s parent organization (and now a minority shareholder in [its U.S. spinoff](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/23/heres-whats-you-should-know-about-the-us-tiktok-deal/)), launched Seedance 2.0 in China back in February. Brief videos generated by the model, including a clip featuring Tom Cruise fighting Brad Pitt, soon went viral and [drew intense criticism from Hollywood](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/15/hollywood-isnt-happy-about-the-new-seedance-2-0-video-generator/). 

While one successful screenwriter declared that the footage meant, “It’s likely over for us,” studios quickly sent ByteDance a flurry of cease-and-desist letters, with Disney’s lawyers accusing the company of a “virtual smash-and-grab of Disney’s IP.” ByteDance responded by [promising to introduce stronger safeguards](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/16/bytedance-safegaurds-seedance-ai-copyright-disney-mpa-netflix-paramount-sony-universal.html) for intellectual property.

The company had planned to make Seedance 2.0 available globally in mid-March, The Information said, but it’s delaying those plans as its engineers and lawyers work to avert further legal issues.

ByteDance did not immediately respond to TechCrunch’s request for comment.
