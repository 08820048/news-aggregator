On March 21, 2006, Jack Dorsey [posted a simple message](https://x.com/jack/status/20): “just setting up my twittr”.

That was, of course, the very first post on the site that is still best known as Twitter, even after being renamed X by its new owner Elon Musk (the deal is still being [fought over in court](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/20/elon-musk-misled-twitter-investors-while-trying-to-get-out-of-acquisition-jury-says/)). X then became [part of Musk’s xAI](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/12/the-xai-x-merger-is-a-good-deal-if-youre-betting-on-musks-empire/), which [itself has become part of SpaceX](https://techcrunch.com/2026/02/02/elon-musk-spacex-acquires-xai-data-centers-space-merger/).

Musk [cut the company’s workforce dramatically](https://techcrunch.com/2022/11/03/twitter-layoffs-elon-musk/) and spurred new controversies by incorporating xAI’s chatbot Grok, which [dubbed itself “MechaHitler”](https://techcrunch.com/2025/07/12/xai-and-grok-apologize-for-horrific-behavior/) and was used to [create widespread sexual deepfakes](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/16/california-ag-sends-musks-xai-a-cease-and-desist-order-over-sexual-deepfakes/), including of real women and children.

While X [retains a strong hold on some user groups](https://techcrunch.com/2025/11/22/pews-latest-social-media-report-shows-xs-staying-power-in-the-u-s-despite-competition/), including swaths of the tech industry, it also faces competition from services like Bluesky and Meta’s Threads. One report suggests that Threads recently [overtook X in daily mobile users](https://techcrunch.com/2026/01/18/threads-edges-out-x-in-daily-mobile-users-new-data-shows/). (All of these primarily text-based services are dwarfed by apps like Instagram and TikTok.)

As for Dorsey’s original tweet, the Twitter co-founder later [sold it as an NFT](https://techcrunch.com/2021/08/05/cent-the-platform-that-jack-dorsey-used-to-sell-his-first-tweet-as-an-nft-raises-3m/) for $2.9 million. But its value has reportedly [plummeted](https://cryptoslate.com/the-nft-of-jack-dorseys-first-tweet-originally-purchased-for-2-9m-is-worth-less-than-4-in-todays-market/), with the buyer unable to resell it.
