The Pentagon has officially [designated Anthropic a supply-chain risk](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/05/its-official-the-pentagon-has-labeled-anthropic-a-supply-chain-risk/) after the two failed to agree on how much control the military should have over its AI models, including its use in autonomous weapons and mass domestic surveillance. As Anthropic’s $200 million contract fell apart, the DoD turned to OpenAI instead, which accepted and then watched [ChatGPT uninstalls surge 295%](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/02/chatgpt-uninstalls-surged-by-295-after-dod-deal/). As the stakes keep rising, the question remains: how much unrestricted access should the military have to an AI model? 

Watch as Equity hosts Kirsten Korosec, Anthony Ha, and Sean O’Kane break down what startups should know about chasing federal AI contracts, plus the week’s biggest tech stories, from [Paramount’s Warner Bros. deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/02/paramount-and-hbo-max-to-merge-into-one-streaming-service-after-wbd-deal-closes/) and MyFitnessPal’s [Cal AI acquisition](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/02/myfitnesspal-has-acquired-cal-ai-the-viral-calorie-app-built-by-teens/) to [Pinterest’s $1B AI push](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/03/activist-investor-elliot-takes-a-1b-stake-in-pinterest-betting-on-ai-driven-growth/), [Anduril’s $60B valuation](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/03/anduril-aims-at-60-billion-valuation-in-new-funding-round/), and whether the “[SaaSpocalypse](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/01/saas-in-saas-out-heres-whats-driving-the-saaspocalypse/)” is real.
