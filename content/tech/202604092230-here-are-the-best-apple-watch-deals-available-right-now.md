In September, Apple launched its latest batch of smartwatches, including the [Apple Watch Series 11](https://www.theverge.com/smartwatch-review/778012/apple-watch-series-11-review-hypertension-5g-sleep-score-wearables-smartwatch), the [SE 3](https://www.theverge.com/smartwatch-review/778037/apple-watch-se-3-review-battery-specs-watchos-26-smartwatch-wearable), and the [Ultra 3](https://www.theverge.com/tech/793264/apple-watch-ultra-3-review-satellite-5g-display-battery-wearable). Fortunately, picking up a watch from the latest (or a recent) generation ensures you’re getting a futureproof smartwatch with an updated design, a robust feature set, and plenty of sensors.

While the Apple Watch Series 11 and Ultra 3 introduce a handful of iterative updates, the [new Apple Watch SE 3](https://www.theverge.com/news/772862/apple-watch-se-2025-iphone-event-announcement-specs-price) represents a significant upgrade over its 2022 predecessor. The entry-level watch features the same speedy S10 chip found in Apple’s flagship models, for one, letting you take advantage of Apple’s double tap feature, wrist flick gestures, and on-device Siri. The SE 3 also (finally) adds an always-on display, 5G connectivity, support for fast-charging, and audio playback so you can listen to music or podcasts directly from your wrist.

On the health front, the SE 3 introduces a wrist-temperature sensor for retrospective ovulation tracking, along with breathing disturbance alerts, sleep apnea notifications, and a new Sleep Score that breaks down time spent in each stage. What you won’t find are the Series 11’s EKG functionality, blood oxygen monitoring, or hypertension notifications — those are exclusive to the Series and Ultra models.
