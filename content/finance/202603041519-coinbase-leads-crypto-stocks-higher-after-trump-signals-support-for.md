Dado Ruvic | Reuters

Shares of [Coinbase](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/COIN/) and other cryptocurrency companies surged Wednesday after President Donald Trump threw his weight behind the industry's battle against U.S. banks over yield-bearing stablecoins — adding to momentum the firms were already feeling from [bitcoin](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/BTC.CM=/)'s bounce.

Coinbase was last up more than 12%. Other digital asset firms such as [Strategy](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/MSTR/) and [Circle](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/CRCL/) jumped 9% and nearly 6%, respectively. Meanwhile, shares of [JPMorgan Chase](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/JPM/) and [Bank of America](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/BAC/) fell less than 1%.

"The Genius Act is being threatened and undermined by the Banks, and that is unacceptable," Trump said late Tuesday in his [social media post](https://x.com/erictrump/status/2028976237289304098?s=46). "They need to make a good deal with the Crypto Industry because that's what's in best interest of the American People."

Greenlighting firms to issue dollar-pegged digital tokens that offer interest-like returns has been a sticking point of the Clarity Act, a market structure bill for the crypto industry, in the U.S. Congress.

Crypto companies also got a boost as cryptocurrencies staged a comeback. Bitcoin and [ether](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/ETH.CM=/) advanced 5% and 6% on Wednesday, respectively.
