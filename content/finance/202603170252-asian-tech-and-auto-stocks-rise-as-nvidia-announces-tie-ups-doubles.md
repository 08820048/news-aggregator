Asia-Pacific markets rose Tuesday as auto and tech stocks gained after Nvidia announced robust revenue forecast for its key chips, and partnerships with carmakers from the region.

Memory makers SK Hynix and Samsung Electronics rose over 3% and 4%, respectively. This comes after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang said he expects purchase orders between Blackwell and Vera Rubin chips to reach $1 trillion through 2027 [at Nvidia's](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/NVDA/) annual developer conference on Monday.

Taiwan's TSMC also rose 1%. Meanwhile, Japan's Tokyo Electron added 0.26% while Advantest declined 1.7%.

Shares of automakers Hyundai Motor, [Nissan Motor](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/7201.T-JP/) and Isuzu, as well as China's BYD and Geely rose after [Nvidia](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/NVDA/) announced it was [partnering with these companies](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/16/nvidia-hyundai-byd-nissan-self-driving-tech.html) for its autonomous vehicle development business.

Hyundai Motor advanced 4.74%, while Nissan Motor and Isuzu rose over 2% and 1.66% respectively. BYD added 2.96% while Geely stocks rose 5%.

Investors also assessed Iran war developments, with U.S. President Donald Trump looking to delay his meeting with Chinese President Xi Jinping by "a month or so" due to the Middle East conflict. Trump was expected to travel to China at the end of March.

International benchmark [Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) gained 2.45% to $102.57 per barrel, while the U.S. [West Texas Intermediate](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) rose 2.51% to $95.85 per barrel as of 8:44 p.m. ET. Prices had dropped on Monday.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) added 0.27%. Australia's central bank is expected to raise rates for a second straight time, pushing its key policy rate to 4.1%, the highest since April 2025, according to Reuters estimates.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) added 0.75%, while the Topix jumped more than 1%. South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) rose 2.94%, while the small-cap Kosdaq added 1.53%.

Hong Kong [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) rose 0.94%, while mainland CSI 300 rose 0.28%.

U.S. stock futures were flat after the major averages bounced overnight in light of cooling oil prices.

Futures tied to the [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@DJ.1/) lost 46 points, or 0.1%. [S&P 500 futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@SP.1/) slipped 0.1%, while [Nasdaq 100 futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@ND.1/) declined nearly 0.2%.

Overnight in the U.S., stocks rose while oil prices pulled back as Wall Street tried to recover from another losing week, with investors monitoring the latest developments of the Iran war.

The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) added 387.94 points, or 0.83%, closing at 46,946.41. The [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) rose 1.01% to end at 6,699.38, and the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) gained 1.22% and settled at 22,374.18.

[Meta](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/META/) shares gained more than 2% on a report — which the company has called "speculative" — that it is [planning to lay off more than 20%](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/16/meta-ai-costs-mass-layoffs-20percent-up-premarket.html) of its workforce. Additionally, [Nvidia](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/NVDA/) shares rose more than 1% as its [GTC conference](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/nvidia-gtc-ai-jensen-huang-cpu-gpu.html) kicked off Monday.
