South Korean defense stocks saw massive gains on Tuesday after the country's markets returned from a public holiday, as the Iran war fuels interest in defense names globally.

Heavyweight Hanwha Aerospace, which is South Korea's largest defense manufacturer, saw shares surge nearly 25%, before paring gains to about 20%, while Korea Aerospace Industries gained more than 12%, but cut those to 3.2%.

Shares in air defense systems maker LIG Nex1 soared 30%, while electronic warfare systems manufacturer Victek and anti-aircraft missile components' maker Firstec saw shares rise 30% and 19%, respectively.

Ammunition manufacturer Poongsan rose 12.78%, while Hyundai Rotem, maker of the K2 main battle tank, advanced over 8%.

The sector's gains were in sharp contrast to the wider [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/), which fell 7.24% to lead losses in Asian markets and record its worst day in 19 months.
