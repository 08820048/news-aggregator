Asia-Pacific markets pared gains Tuesday as oil prices rebounded, underscoring lingering uncertainty over the Middle East conflict.

[Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) futures for May rose almost 3% to $102.91 per barrel while the [West Texas Intermediate futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) jumped 3.7% to $91.4 per barrel. The uptick follows a sharp sell-off on Monday, when [Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) fell nearly 11% to around $99 per barrel after [topping $112](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/20/oil-wti-brent-us-weighs-releasing-sanctioned-iranian-crude.html) on Friday.

"Despite the exuberance on Wall Street ... oil is well off its lows after Tehran denied conducting any weekend negotiations with Washington," said José Torres, senior economist at Interactive Brokers, who added that the risk of an extended war remains at the top of the mind for the market.

South Korea's Kospi had surged over 3% before paring some gains to close 2.7% higher at 5,553.92 while the small-cap Kosdaq was last up 2.24%, ending the trading day at 1,121.44.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) rose 1.43% to close at 52,252.28, while the Topix added 2.1% to 3,559.67 after Japan's headline inflation rate eased for a fourth straight month in February as the economy cooled on stabilizing food prices and fuel subsidies.

The consumer price index fell to 1.3% last month, according to data released by Japan's Statistics Bureau Tuesday, marking the lowest since March 2022 and below the central bank's 2% target, down from 1.5% in January.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) rose by 0.16% to end the trading day at 8,379.4.

Hong Kong [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) advanced 2.39%, while the CSI 300 rose 1.28% to 4,474.72.

The gains came after U.S. President Donald Trump said Monday he had [instructed the U.S. military to delay planned strikes](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/23/trump-iran-war-power-plants-energy-infrastructure-middle-east.html) on Iran's power plants and energy facilities for five days, after discussions with Iranian officials.

However, Iranian state media, citing an unnamed senior security official in a Telegram post, disputed Trump's account, denying that any talks had taken place between Washington and Tehran.

"I AM PLEASE TO REPORT THAT THE UNITED STATES OF AMERICA, AND THE COUNTRY OF IRAN, HAVE HAD, OVER THE LAST TWO DAYS, VERY GOOD AND PRODUCTIVE CONVERSATIONS REGARDING A COMPLETE AND TOTAL RESOLUTION OF OUR HOSTILITIES IN THE MIDDLE EAST," [Trump](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/) said Monday in a Truth Social post.

Overnight in the U.S., stocks rallied. The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) jumped 631 points, or 1.38%, to close at 46,208.47. The [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) rose 1.15% and ended at 6,581.00, while the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) gained 1.38% and settled at 21,946.76.

Before Trump's comments, posted on [Truth Social](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/116278232362967212) early Monday, futures pointed to more losses for equity markets under siege from skyrocketing oil prices and uncertainty about the duration of the Iran conflict. But after Trump's comments, Dow futures briefly surged more than 1,000 points.

[Spot gold](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/XAU=/) fell about 1.5% to $4,340.18, while [spot silver](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/Xag=) prices fell nearly 3%.
