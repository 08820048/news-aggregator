[Space](https://www.cnbc.com/space/) stocks soared Wednesday following a report that [Elon Musk's](https://www.cnbc.com/elon-musk/) SpaceX could file to go public as soon as this week.

Satellite designer [AST SpaceMobile](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/ASTS/) and [Rocket Lab](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/RKLB/) both jumped about 10%. Rocket maker [Firefly Aerospace](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/FLY/), which went [public in August](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/08/07/rocket-maker-firefly-aerospace-fly-stock-ipo.html), climbed 16%. [York Space](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/YSS/), an aerospace company that held its IPO in January, rose 5% on the news.

According to [the Information](https://www.theinformation.com/articles/spacex-aims-file-ipo-soon-week), the highly anticipated [stock market debut](#) for SpaceX could raise over $75 billion. CNBC previously reported that it could be the [biggest IPO ever](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/02/spacex-ipo-musk-2026.html), seeking a $1.75 trillion valuation.

SpaceX acquired Musk's xAI last month in a deal valuing the combined company at [$1.25 trillion](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/03/musk-xai-spacex-biggest-merger-ever.html).

The [space sector](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/22/firefly-rocket-lab-intuitive-machines-stock.html), which includes companies focused on national defense, has benefited from excitement surrounding SpaceX, [President Donald Trump's](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/) plans for a "[Golden Dome](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/27/redwire-stock-trump-golden-dome-defense.html)" defense system and soaring demand for [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/ai-effect/) infrastructure.

The rapid buildout of data centers to support AI has been blamed for [rising electricity costs](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/ai-data-centers-electricity-prices-backlash-ratepayer-protection.html). Sending data centers [into space](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/02/musks-xai-needs-spacex-for-money-data-centers-in-space-are-a-dream.html) has been proposed as a solution, but faces numerous barriers, including high costs and limited rocket launch availability.

SpaceX currently operates the Starlink constellation, with [over 9,500 satellites](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/22/why-low-earth-orbit-is-attracting-billions-in-investment.html) in orbit. In January, Musk proposed a larger project that would launch [1 million satellites](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/fcc-chair-amazon-spacex-data-center-space.html), an effort that's been staunchly opposed by scientists for environmental threats.
