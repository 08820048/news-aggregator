Iran's Supreme Leader [Mojtaba Khamenei](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/five-things-to-know-about-irans-new-supreme-leader-mojtaba-khamenei.html) has subscribed to X Premium on his official English-language account, despite the Elon Musk-owned company's policy on sanctioned individuals. 

It comes after the removal of a blue checkmark on the Iranian Supreme Leader's [Farsi-language account](https://x.com/Rahbarenghelab_), after the [profile's verified status](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/iran-supreme-leader-mojtaba-khamenei-x-account-elon-musk.html) had been highlighted by the Tech Transparency Project (TTP) in March. 

Khamenei's [English language X profile](https://x.com/MKhamenei_ir) currently has more followers than his Farsi-language account, with about 175,000, and has been used to disseminate posts regarding the ongoing U.S.-Israel and Iran conflict. According to the profile, the account is located in Iran and was created in March.

Khamenei X's profiles have attracted scrutiny due to the leader falling under the [U.S. Treasury's OFAC sanctions list](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/sm824) since 2019, prohibiting U.S. persons and entities from dealing with him.

Khamenei assumed leadership of Iran after his father, [Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, was killed](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/28/trump-iran-strikes-live-updates.html) in a U.S. strike on Feb. 28, amid the escalating war with the United States and Israel.

Blue checkmarks on X are reserved exclusively for paid [X Premium or Premium+](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/x-premium#tbfeatures) subscribers, and come with a number of perks, such as the ability to write longer posts, upload longer videos and share in ad revenue.

According to the company's [policies](https://help.x.com/en/managing-your-account/about-x-verified-accounts), "You may not purchase X Premium if you are a person with whom X is not permitted to have dealings under US and any other applicable economic sanctions and trade compliance law." It is unclear how that policy is enforced. 

X also often assigns free [grey checkmarks](https://help.x.com/en/using-x/grey-checkmark) to government officials, a feature previously used for [accounts linked to the elder Khamenei](https://x.com/Khamenei_fa). 

However, on Feb 12, TTP released a report that found there were [X accounts in the names of Iranian officials](https://www.techtransparencyproject.org/articles/iranian-officials-get-premium-treatment-on-x-amid-protester-crackdown), agencies and government-run media outlets that had a blue check mark, which indicates they were customers of X's Premium service.

"Because X requires a paid subscription to get premium service, it has likely received revenue from these Iranian individuals and groups — a potential violation of U.S. economic sanctions," TTP's report said. 

[Musk's space exploration company SpaceX](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/02/elon-musk-spacex-xai-ipo.html), which owns X and the artificial intelligence company xAI, did not immediately respond to a request for comment on Mojtaba Khamenei's blue checkmark on the social network. 

The U.S. Treasury, which enforces sanctions against Iran, did not immediately respond to a request for comment.
