[White House](https://www.cnbc.com/white-house/) chief of staff [Susie Wiles](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/07/trump-return-of-soldiers-killed-in-war-in-the-middle-east.html) has been diagnosed with "early stage breast [cancer](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/23/healthy-returns-researchers-move-closer-to-a-universal-cancer-vaccine.html)" but remains committed to her job while undergoing treatment, President [Donald Trump](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/) revealed Monday.

Wiles, 68, "has decided to take on this challenge, IMMEDIATELY, as opposed to waiting," Trump said in a [Truth Social post](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116239674084445278) revealing the diagnosis.

She has an "excellent" prognosis and will be spending "virtually full time at the White House," Trump said in the post.

"Her Strength and her Commitment to continue doing the job she loves, and does so well, while undergoing treatment, tells you everything you need to know about her," he said.

The White House did not immediately respond to CNBC's request for additional information on Wiles.

She appeared next to Trump at the White House on Monday afternoon, during a meeting of the board of the John F. Kennedy Center for the Performing Arts. The board, which has been stocked with Trump's appointees including Wiles, had convened to vote on plans to close the center for renovations starting on July 6, The New York Times [reported](https://www.nytimes.com/live/2026/03/16/us/trump-news).

Trump said at the event that Wiles is dealing with a "minor difficulty" and reiterated that she will be addressing it "immediately."

Wiles, in an X post later Monday, said she received her diagnosis last week, and confirmed that she will continue to serve in the White House.

"Nearly one in eight women in the United States will face this diagnosis. Every day, these women continue to raise their families, go to work, and serve their communities with strength and determination. I now join their ranks," Wiles said in the post.

"I am grateful to have an outstanding team of doctors who detected the cancer early and are guiding my care, and I am encouraged by a very good prognosis," her post said.

Wiles oversaw Trump's winning 2024 presidential campaign and has wielded major influence in his second administration.

While often seen alongside Trump at public events, Wiles rarely speaks on camera or grants on-the-record interviews. However, she participated in a [series of interviews](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/16/trump-susie-wiles-lawsuits-epstein-musk.html) in 2025 with Vanity Fair that revealed strikingly blunt assessments of Trump and his top aides.

She told the outlet, among other things, that Trump has "an alcoholic's personality" and said he has engaged in legal "[retribution](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/11/doj-letitia-james-trump-grand-jury.html)" against his enemies as president.

After the interviews were published in December, Trump said he stood by Wiles and a slew of top administration officials leapt to her defense while attacking Vanity Fair. Wiles herself called the articles a "hit piece."
