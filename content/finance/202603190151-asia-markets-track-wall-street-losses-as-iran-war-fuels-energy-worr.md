Asia-Pacific markets dipped on Thursday, tracking losses on Wall Street that saw the [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) touch a new closing low for the year.

The Federal Reserve held its key policy rate steady at 3.5% to 3.75%, with Chair Jerome Powell watering down rate-cut expectations, saying that inflation [was not coming down as much as ‘hoped.’](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/fed-meeting-today-live-updates.html)

The producer price index — which tracks the change in wholesale prices — rose 0.7% in February, well above the 0.3% that economists polled by Dow Jones had estimated.

Despite that, the U.S. central bank's ["dot plot"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/dot-plot-fed-still-expects-to-cut-rates-once-this-year-despite-spiking-oil-prices-.html) still projects a cut in 2026 and another in 2027, even though the timing is unclear.

The Iran war continues to fuel energy worries. International benchmark [Brent crude futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) rose 3.83% to settle at $107.38 per barrel.

U.S. oil prices were trading at elevated levels as well, with [West Texas Intermediate futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) closing marginally higher at $96.32 per barrel.

Investors in Asia will look toward the Bank of Japan decision, with the bank expected to hold rates at 0.75%.

South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) lost 2.54%, after being the top gainer in the region on Wednesday, while the small-cap Kosdaq saw a smaller loss of 1.69%.

Chip heavyweights Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix saw losses of over 3%.

The South Korean won briefly broke past the 1,500 mark against the dollar earlier in the session, prompting finance minister Koo Yun-cheol to [say on Wednesday that authorities](https://www.chosun.com/english/market-money-en/2026/03/19/TJQSSIEBRBHXPJZIEQE7IKNHSE/) have a "heightened sense of vigilance toward the foreign exchange market," according to South Korean media.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) was down 2.58%, leading losses in Asia, while the broad-based Topix was 2.03% lower.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) was down 1.56%.

Hong Kong's Hang Seng index fell 1.62%, while the mainland Chinese CSI 300 index was 0.89% down.

Overnight in the U.S., the 30-stock Dow lost 1.63%, ending at 46,225.15, reaching a new low this year. The index also closed below its 200-day moving average.

The [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) fell 1.36%, while the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) dropped 1.46%.

_—CNBC's Sean Conlon, Pia Singh and Jeff Cox contributed to this report._
