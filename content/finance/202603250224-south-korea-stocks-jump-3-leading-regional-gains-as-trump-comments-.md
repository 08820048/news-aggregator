South Korea stocks led gains Wednesday as comments from U.S. President Donald Trump pointing to potential talks with Iran lifted sentiment, even as Tehran has denied any direct negotiations with Washington.

Speaking at the Oval Office on Tuesday, Trump said the U.S. and Iran were "in negotiations right now" and suggested Tehran was keen to strike a peace deal, adding he had [stepped back from threats to target](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/trump-iran-war-negotiations.html) Iranian energy infrastructure "based on the fact we're negotiating."

South Korea's Kospi jumped 3%, while the small-cap Kosdaq was 3.18% higher.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) rose 2%.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) gained 2.88%, while the Topix added 2.4%.

Hong Kong's [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) added 1.14%, while the CSI 300 rose 0.67%.

Oil prices were lower in early Asia trading hours.

International benchmark [Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) futures fell around 6% to $98.31 per barrel, while U.S. [West Texas Intermediate futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) were also down 5% at $87.65 per barrel.

U.S. stock futures rose Tuesday night stateside, with [S&P 500 futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@SP.1/) and [Nasdaq 100 futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@ND.1/) rising 0.7% and 0.8%, respectively. [Futures tied to the Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@DJ.1/) gained 318 points, or 0.7%.

Futures were higher after the [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) pulled back, giving back some of the sharp gains seen in the previous session, as crude prices rose again while the Iran war entered its fourth week.

The broad market index lost 0.37% and ended at 6,556.37, while the [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) shed 84.41 points, or 0.18%, and settled at 46,124.06. The [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) dropped 0.84% and closed at 21,761.89.
