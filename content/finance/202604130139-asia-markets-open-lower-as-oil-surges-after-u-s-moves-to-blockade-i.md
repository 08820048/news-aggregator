Asia-Pacific markets opened lower Monday, as investors weigh a U.S. naval blockade on Iran's ports after talks between Washington and Tehran failed to produce an agreement to end the conflict in the Middle East.

The [breakdown of negotiations](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/11/us-iran-talks-set-to-begin-in-islamabad-after-delegations-arrive.html) over the weekend in Islamabad reignited worries that the U.S.-Iran war will last longer than feared, leading to higher oil prices that will continue to strain economies worldwide.

Crude oil prices [surged on Sunday](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/12/oil-prices-iran-war-strait-hormuz-blockade.html) after the talks ended without an agreement and the U.S. moved toward a blockade of Iranian port traffic. The [West Texas Intermediate](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) jumped 7.93% to $104.23 per barrel as of 9:27 p.m. ET. [Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) gained 6.71% to $101.59 per barrel.

U.S. President Donald Trump has reportedly weighed resuming airstrikes on Iran, according to the [Wall Street Journal](https://www.wsj.com/world/middle-east/trump-weighs-limited-strikes-on-iran-after-talks-collapse-71908883). Trump last week agreed to a two-week ceasefire on Tuesday in exchange for Tehran allowing ships to pass through the strait. He had previously threatened to bomb every bridge and power plant in Iran.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) fell 0.72%, while the Topix trimmed slipped 0.20%. South Korea's Kospi was 0.73% lower, while the small-cap Kosdaq reversed early losses to gain 0.42%. In Australia, the [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) was 0.38% lower.

Mainland China's CSI300 index was flat, while Hong Kong's [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) declined 0.71%
