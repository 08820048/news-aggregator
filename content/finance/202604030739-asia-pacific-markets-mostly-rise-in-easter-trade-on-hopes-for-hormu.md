Asia-Pacific markets traded mostly higher Friday, after Iran and Oman were reportedly [drafting a protocol to "monitor transit"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/02/iran-war-oman-hormuz-strait.html) through the [Strait of Hormuz](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/01/trump-address-nation-iran-live-updates.html), raising hopes that the crucial waterway could partially reopen.

Tanker traffic through the key oil-shipping route "should be supervised and coordinated" with the two countries, said Kazem Gharibabadi, Iran's deputy foreign minister of legal and international affairs, according to [Iranian](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/01/iran-war-social-media-meme-propaganda.html) state news agency IRNA.

Oil prices surged Thursday before markets closed for the Good Friday holiday. [U.S. crude futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) jumped almost 12% to trade at $112.06 per barrel, while [global benchmark Brent](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) was up around 8% at $109.24.

[The spot price](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/02/dated-brent-oil-price-actual-cargo-highest-level-2008.html) for current physical cargoes of Brent crude oil soared Thursday to $141.36, the highest level since the 2008 financial crisis, according to S&P Global.

South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) led gains in the region, rising 2.74% and closing at 5,377.3, while the small-cap Kosdaq rose 0.74% to 1,063.75.

South Korean President Lee Jae Myung will meet French President Emmanuel Macron for a summit meeting Friday.

[The Blue House](https://www.president.go.kr/cms/board/boardView.do?MENU_CD=nFSy219D&CONTENTS_CD=vqNUjDNc&BBS_CD=8RWZLYMn&pageNo=1) said in a statement that the two countries are expected to upgrade their relationship to a 'Global Strategic Partnership,' marking the first upgrade in 22 years.

[The Korea Times](https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/foreignaffairs/20260403/lee-macron-hold-summit-as-seoul-eyes-upgrade-in-bilateral-ties) reported that the two sides are expected to discuss expanding cooperation in trade and investment, as well as in sectors such as artificial intelligence, nuclear energy and space.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) was up 1.26% and ended the day at 53,123.49, driven by consumer non-cyclical stocks, and the broad-based Topix was 0.93% higher and closed at 3,645.19, powered by energy stocks.

On Friday, Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama [reportedly said](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-03/japan-s-katayama-voices-concern-over-fx-volatility-after-trump?embedded-checkout=true) that the impact of U.S. President Donald Trump's nationwide address on Wednesday was "quite significant," adding that speculative activity was seen in both crude oil futures and currency markets.

Yields on Japanese government bonds had also hit records, with the 2-year JGB yield reaching 1.39%, its highest level since 1995. The benchmark 10-year JGB bond yield was at 2.399%, its highest since 1999.

The CSI 300 index in mainland China reversed earlier gains, falling 0.85% to 4,440.79.

The Australian and Hong Kong markets were closed for the Easter weekend.

Overnight in the U.S., markets saw a volatile session amid rising oil prices, but the major indexes ended little changed heading into the holiday weekend, with the blue-chip Dow declining 61.07 points, or 0.13%.

The [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) advanced 0.11%, and the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) gained 0.18%.

_— CNBC's Kevin Breuninger, Lisa Kailai Han and Sean Conlon contributed to this report._
