Shares of financial services firms tanked Tuesday after the launch of a new tax planning tool powered by artificial intelligence that promises to do the work "within minutes."

[LPL Financial](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/LPLA/) tumbled nearly 11%, while [Charles Schwab](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/SCHW/) and [Raymond James Financial](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/RJF/) both dropped more than 9% amid fears that AI will disrupt their industry next. [Morgan Stanley](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/MS/) dropped 4%.

Tech platform Altruist [announced the offering](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260210142841/en/Altruist-Introduces-AI-Powered-Tax-Planning-in-Hazel-Helping-Advisors-Deliver-Tax-Strategies-in-Minutes) within its AI platform, Hazel, and said it "helps advisors create fully personalized tax strategies for clients by reading and interpreting their 1040s, paystubs, account statements, meeting notes, emails, and custodial and CRM data, and applying deep tax logic to the analysis."
