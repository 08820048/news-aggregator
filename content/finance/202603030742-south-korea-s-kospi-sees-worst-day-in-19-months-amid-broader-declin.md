South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) saw its worst day in 19 months, weighed down by heavyweights Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix that fell almost 10% and 12%, respectively, on Tuesday as trading resumed after a public holiday.

The index tumbled 7.24% to 5,791.91. Samsung shares plunged after [a report](https://koreajoongangdaily.joins.com/news/2026-03-03/business/industry/Exclusive-Mass-production-at-Samsungs-Taylor-plant-pushed-back-to-next-year/2534681) revealed that mass production at the company's U.S. plant in Taylor, Texas had been pushed back to 2027 from this year.

The country's defense stocks, however, [surged](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/03/south-korea-defense-stocks-soar-hanwha-aerospace-iran-us-israel-war.html), with some names rising over 20%.

Other Asia-Pacific markets tumbled as the conflict in Iran continues to rage on for a fourth day, denting risk sentiment.

Oil prices [extended gains](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/01/crude-oil-futures-iran.html) after Iran reportedly said it had closed the Strait of Hormuz, with U.S. crude futures up 2.16% to $72.78 per barrel, while Brent was up 2.78% to trade at $79.91 per barrel as of 2.38 a.m. ET.

More than 14 million barrels per day transited via the Strait on average last year, accounting for nearly a third of the world's overall seaborne crude exports, according to Kpler data.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) dropped 3.06%, weighed down by consumer cyclicals, to 56,279.1 while the Topix dipped 3.24% to 3,772.17.

Hong Kong's [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) was down 1.25% in its final hour of trading, while mainland China's CSI 300 fell 1.54% to close at 4,655.9.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) dropped 1.34% to finish at 9,077.3, after being one of the few markets on Monday to record a marginal gain.

Overnight in the U.S., the S&P 500 inched up 0.04% after rebounding late in the session. The [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) was higher by 0.36%, coming back from a 1.6% loss.

The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) fell 73.14 points, or 0.15%, settling at 48,904.78. At its lows, the Dow was down nearly 600 points.
