LONDON — European stocks are expected to open sharply higher on Wednesday after the U.S. and Iran agreed to a conditional ceasefire deal.

The U.K.'s [FTSE 100](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.FTSE/) index is seen opening 3% higher, with Germany's [DAX](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.GDAXI/) 5% higher, France's [CAC 40](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.FCHI/) up 4.5% and Italy's [FTSE MIB](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.FTMIB/) 5.3% higher, according to data from IG.

Global markets rallied and oil prices plunged overnight after U.S. President [Donald Trump](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/) said late on Tuesday that he had agreed to suspend [planned attacks on Iranian infrastructure](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/07/iran-war-trump-deadline.html) for two weeks.

The move was "subject to the Islamic Republic of Iran agreeing to the COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING of the Strait of Hormuz," he [wrote on Truth Social.](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116365796713313030)

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said in a post on X on behalf of the country's Supreme National Security Council that Tehran's armed forces will "[cease their defensive operations.](https://x.com/araghchi/status/2041655156215799821/photo/1)"

[Asia-Pacific markets](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/asia-markets-today-trump-iran-war-kospi-nikkei-225-oil-hang-seng-index.html) rallied overnight while [U.S. stock futures](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/07/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) jumped on news of the ceasefire. [Oil prices](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/07/oil-prices-iran-war-trump-deadline-strait-hormuz.html) also plunged below $100 a barrel upon news of the deal, which came after Trump had threatened to order the destruction of Iran's "whole civilization" unless it reopened the Strait of Hormuz.

However, a number Middle Eastern countries continue to report [incoming missiles and drones from Iran](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/iran-missile-attacks-after-us-ceasefire-gulf-air-defenses.html) on Wednesday, triggering air defenses across the Gulf.

Earnings on Wednesday come from [Shell](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/SHEL-GB/), and data releases include German factory orders and EU retail sales figures.
