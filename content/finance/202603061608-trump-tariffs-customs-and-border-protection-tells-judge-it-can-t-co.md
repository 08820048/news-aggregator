U.S. Customs and Border Protection told a Court of International Trade judge on Friday that it is not able to comply with his order to begin refunding reciprocal tariffs imposed last year by President [Donald Trump](https://www.cnbc.com/donald-trump/), which the Supreme Court has ruled are illegal.

[CBP in the same court filing](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.cit.19346/gov.uscourts.cit.19346.31.0_1.pdf) told Judge Richard Eaton that the total amount of so-called IEEPA duties collected as of Tuesday by the agency and estimated duty deposits related to such tariffs "is approximately $166 billion."
