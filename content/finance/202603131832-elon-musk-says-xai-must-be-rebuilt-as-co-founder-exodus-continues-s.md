Less than six weeks after [Elon Musk](https://www.cnbc.com/elon-musk/) merged [SpaceX](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/03/musk-xai-spacex-biggest-merger-ever.html) and [xAI](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/03/28/elon-musk-says-xai-has-acquired-x-in-deal-that-values-social-media-site-at-33-billion.html) in a deal he valued at $1.25 trillion, the world's richest person is [acknowledging](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/2032201568335044978) that his [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/ai-effect/) startup "was not built right first time around, so is being rebuilt from the foundations up."

Musk took to X, which is now owned by SpaceX, to make the comment after a number of xAI's co-founders recently hit the exits. The most recent came this week, when Zihang Dai and Guodong Zhang [reportedly](https://www.businessinsider.com/xai-cofounders-guodong-zhang-zihang-dai-depart-elon-musk-company-2026-3) left the company.

Last month, influential researcher Jimmy Ba [announced](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/10/musks-xai-loses-second-co-founder-in-two-days-as-jimmy-ba-departs.html) his departure in a [post](https://x.com/jimmybajimmyba/status/2021374875793801447) on X, thanking Musk and writing that he was, "Grateful to have helped cofound at the start." That came after Tony Wu [said](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/10/elon-musk-xai-co-founder-tony-wu.html) he was leaving. Toby Pohlen followed them [out the door](https://x.com/TobyPhln) later in February.

The xAI exodus, which leaves Musk with only a pair of people who started the company with him in 2023, comes as SpaceX prepares to [go public](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/11/musk-says-spacex-report-of-2026-ipo-is-accurate.html) sometime this year in what will likely be a record IPO, should it take place.

In merging SpaceX with xAI last month, the reusable rocket company was valued at $1 trillion and the AI part of the business was tagged at $250 billion, according to [documents viewed](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/03/musk-xai-spacex-biggest-merger-ever.html) by CNBC.

Musk previously used xAI to acquire his social network X, formerly Twitter, in another all-stock transaction announced last March.

On Thursday, SpaceX said it hired two programmers from red-hot AI coding startup [Cursor](http://cursor.ai/), Andrew Milich and Jason Ginsberg. The [Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/e5fbc6c2-d5a6-4b97-a105-6a96ea849de5) reported on Friday, citing people familiar with the matter, that Musk has ordered a round of job cuts after seeing the rapid success of coding tools from generative AI rivals [OpenAI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/20/openai-resets-spend-expectations-targets-around-600-billion-by-2030.html) and [Anthropic](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/09/anthropic-was-the-pentagons-choice-for-ai-now-its-banned-and-experts-are-worried.html).

"Many talented people over the past few years were declined an offer or even an interview @xAI," Musk [wrote on X](https://x.com/elonmusk/status/2032341856944865487) early Friday. "My apologies."

Musk added that he and Baris Akis, who is responsible for engineering talent at xAI, "are going through the company interview history and reaching back out to promising candidates."

In addition to losing early talent and falling behind in AI-powered coding, xAI faces a number of controversies surrounding its chatbot and image generator Grok, which is the subject of government [investigations](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/26/elon-musk-grok-eu-explicit-images-investigation.html) in multiple international jurisdictions.

The problems began after Grok enabled users to easily generate non-consensual sexual images, or [deepfake porn](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/02/musk-grok-ai-bot-safeguard-sexualized-images-children.html), by modifying photos or videos of real adults and children.

Under the Trump presidency, xAI's Grok has won [government contracts](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/07/14/anthropic-google-openai-xai-granted-up-to-200-million-from-dod.html) from the Defense Department and [General Services Administration.](https://www.gsa.gov/about-us/newsroom/news-releases/gsa-xai-partner-to-accelerate-federal-ai-adoption-09252025)

Meanwhile, xAI has been spending billions of dollars to stand up power and data infrastructure in and around Memphis, Tennessee, in recent years. The company just [scored](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/elon-musk-xai-permit-for-mississippi-plant-despite-pollution-concerns.html) a permit in the state of Mississippi to set up one of the largest power plants in the region to use natural gas-burning turbines for xAI's data centers.

[Tesla](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/TSLA/), the automaker that Musk counts on for substantially all of his liquid wealth, is working with xAI in a number of ways. The EV maker is integrating Grok into its vehicle infotainment and navigation systems and using Grok models in conjunction with the development of Optimus humanoid robots.

Tesla has also sold hundreds of millions of dollars worth of its [big backup batteries](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/29/tesla-sold-430-million-worth-of-its-megapack-batteries-to-xai-in-2025.html) to xAI for use at its data centers.

Musk and xAI didn't immediately respond to requests for comment.
