Asia-Pacific markets opened mostly higher Friday, though a fragile [two-week ceasefire](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/07/trump-iran-ceasefire-hormuz-strait.html) between the U.S. and Iran keeps investors on tenterhooks with oil prices resuming gains.

The Mideast conflict, which has been going on for more than a month, led to the closure of the [Strait of Hormuz](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/09/iran-war-oil-tanker-strait-hormuz.html), and traffic continues to largely be restricted via the crucial energy waterway despite the ceasefire.

Tehran had said it would reopen the strait as long as all attacks on the country were halted, according to [a statement](https://x.com/araghchi/status/2041655156215799821?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E2041655156215799821%7Ctwgr%5E16e29459fe77117059526fd609f78b92d0705716%7Ctwcon%5Es1_&ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.cnbc.com%2F2026%2F04%2F07%2Ftrump-iran-ceasefire-hormuz-strait.html) from its foreign minister. Media reports said that Israel had also agreed to the ceasefire. That followed U.S. President Donald Trump pausing attacks on Iran on Tuesday.

Trump said Thursday that [Iran](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/08/stock-market-today-live-updates.html) "better stop now" if it was charging fees to [oil](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/09/iran-war-oil-saudi-arabia-east-west-pipeline.html) tankers for allowing them passage through the strait, while Iran's parliamentary speaker, Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf, charged the U.S. on Wednesday of violating the ceasefire agreement.

The [West Texas Intermediate](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) was up 0.79% at $98.66 per barrel as of 9:32 p.m. ET. [Brent crude](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) rose by 0.43% to $96.33 per barrel. WTI had crossed $100 per barrel earlier in the session, as traffic through the Strait of Hormuz remained minimal despite the ceasefire agreement with U.S.

"Iran is doing a very poor job, dishonorable some would say, of allowing Oil to go through the Strait of Hormuz," [Trump said](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/116377109535639790) in a Truth Social post.

South Korea's Kospi advanced 1.68%, while the small-cap Kosdaq was 1.14% higher.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) gained 1.75%, while the Topix was marginally higher. Japanese Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi said Friday that the country plans to release of 20 days' worth of oil reserves from May onwards, Reuters reported. Japan has enough oil for 230 days in its reserves, as of April 6.

Australia's S&P/ASX 200 was 0.31% lower.

China's CSI 300 gained 0.6%. China's factory-gate prices rose for the first time in more than three years, while the consumer price index climbed 1% in March from a year earlier. Hang Seng Index rose 0.88%.

Overnight on Wall Street,  oil prices came off their highs of the day while the S&P 500 traded into the green.

The [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) ended the session at 6,824.66, adding 0.62%, while the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) gained 0.83% to 22,822.42. The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) rose 275.88 points, or 0.58%, and settled at 48,185.80. The 30-stock index turned positive for the year, up 0.25%.
