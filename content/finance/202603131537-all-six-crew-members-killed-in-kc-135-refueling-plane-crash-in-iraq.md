The [U.S. military](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/10/iran-war-trump-hegseth-oil.html) on Friday said that all six crew members of a [KC-135 military refueling plane that crashed](https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/03/13/us-kc135-crash-iraq-iran-threats-shipping-attacks.html) a day earlier while flying over western [Iraq](https://www.cnbc.com/id/10000298) have been confirmed as dead.

"The circumstances of the incident are under investigation," [U.S. Central Command said in a statement.](https://www.centcom.mil/MEDIA/PRESS-RELEASES/Press-Release-View/Article/4434083/all-crew-members-of-us-kc-135-loss-in-iraq-confirmed-deceased/) "However, the loss of the aircraft was not due to hostile fire or friendly fire.

The identities of the crew members are being withheld until 24 hours after their next of kin have been notified, Central Command said.

Earlier, Central Command said two aircraft were involved. The second landed safely, Central Command added.

"The incident occurred in friendly airspace during [Operation Epic Fury](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/02/global-week-ahead-operation-epic-fury-us-israel-iran-strikes-oil-shipping-markets-volatility.html)," Central Command said Thursday, referring to [the war against Iran](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/13/iran-war-strait-of-hormuz-hegseth.html), which led to retaliatory strikes by Tehran across the Middle East.

Getty Images

The Islamic Resistance in Iraq, a group of militias in the country backed by Iran, claimed responsibility for the downing of the U.S. aircraft in a statement posted on its Telegram channel.

This is the fourth reported aircraft loss since the Iran war started, after [three F-15 fighters were shot down](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/02/us-iran-live-updates-trump-oil-gold.html) by friendly fire from Kuwait's air defenses.

The KC-135, which cost [$39.6 million in 1998](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/1529736/kc-135-stratotanker/) according to the U.S. Air Force, is normally used to refuel other aircraft in mid-air.

## U.S. will be 'sorry': Iran security chief

The aircraft loss comes after Trump hinted that the war would not end soon.

"We have unparalleled firepower, unlimited ammunition, and plenty of time," he said in a Truth Social post overnight, before calling on his followers to "watch what happens" to the Iranian regime on Friday.

Meanwhile, Iran's security chief, Ali Larijani, said Tehran would make the U.S. "sorry" for starting the war in Iran.

"Trump says he is looking for a speedy victory. While starting a war is easy, it cannot be won with a few tweets," Larijani said [in a post on X](https://x.com/alilarijani_ir/status/2032128509817037247?ref_src=twsrc%5Egoogle%7Ctwcamp%5Eserp%7Ctwgr%5Etweet) early Friday.

His statement followed remarks by Iran's new supreme leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, that the [Strait of Hormuz](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/strait-of-hormuz-closure-shipping-economy-oil.html) maritime passage [should remain closed](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/strait-of-hormuz-closure-iran-oil-prices-mojtaba-khamenei.html) as a "tool to pressure the enemy."

Despite U.S. President Donald Trump claiming [that "we won" in Iran](https://www.nbcnews.com/world/iran/live-blog/live-updates-iran-war-oil-ship-attacks-hormuz-trump-israel-lebanon-rcna263101) and that the [war will end "very soon,"](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/09/trump-iran-war-end.html) more foreign ships were [struck in the Persian Gulf](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/12/iran-war-persian-gulf-strait-of-hormuz-ships-uae-iraq.html) on Thursday.

Iran also warned that oil prices could climb to $200 a barrel, accusing the U.S. of destabilising regional security, Ebrahim Zolfaqari, spokesperson for Iran's military command, said Wednesday, [according to Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/combatants-mideast-war-trade-more-air-strikes-iran-clamps-down-dissent-2026-03-11/).
