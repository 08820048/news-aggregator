The relief rally comes after tech names suffered a bruising start to 2026. Tech stocks were hit particularly hard [last month](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/27/tech-stocks-iran-war-meta-verdict.html#:~:text=The%20Nasdaq%20slumped%203.23%25%20this,slight%20gain%20for%20the%20week.) amid a broader market selloff driven by concerns about the Iran war.

[Software stocks](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/19/apollo-global-sambur-software-ai-selloff.html) have crumbled over the past few months on fears that [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/06/ai-anthropic-tools-saas-software-stocks-selloff.html) could disrupt software business models.

The wipeout coincided with shares of [Big Tech companies](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/06/ai-sell-off-stocks-amazon-oracle.html) coming under pressure in February as investors questioned when they'll start to see a return on their massive AI spending plans.

[Microsoft](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/MSFT/) shares have been battered more than most tech names due to worries over its [AI strategy](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/31/microsofts-stock-closes-worst-quarter-since-2008-financial-crisis.html). The stock slumped 23% in the first quarter, a steeper drop than any of its tech peers or the Nasdaq, which fell 7% over that stretch.
