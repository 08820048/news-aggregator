South Korea's Kospi hit a fresh record high Thursday, as the region tracked gains on Wall Street and several bourses in Asia resumed trading after the Lunar New Year holiday.

The Kospi index jumped 2.76% to a fresh record high, with index heavyweights Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix up 4.14% and 1.48%, respectively.

The small-cap Kosdaq advanced 0.59%.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) rose 1.1% in early trade.

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) added 0.52%, while the Topix rose 0.39%.

Hong Kong and mainland China markets remain closed for the Lunar New Year break.

Overnight in the U.S., the [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) moved higher, supported by gains in key technology names, as traders weighed the [release of the minutes](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/18/fed-minutes-january-2026.html) from the Federal Reserve's most recent policy meeting.

The broad-based index climbed 0.56% to end at 6,881.31, while the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) added 0.78% to settle at 22,753.63. The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) added 0.26% to close at 49,662.66.
