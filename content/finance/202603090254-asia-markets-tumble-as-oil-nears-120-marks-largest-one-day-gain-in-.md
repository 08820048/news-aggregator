South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) triggered its second circuit breaker in four sessions on Monday, leading a broader regional sell-off as oil prices neared $120 per barrel for the first time since 2022.

The index plunged over 8%, triggering a 20 minute suspension in trading from 10.31 a.m. local time. Heavyweight Samsung Electronics plunged more than 10%, while chip counterpart SK Hynix shed 11.6%.

A circuit breaker was activated last week when the benchmark tumbled more than 12% Wednesday to record its [worst single-day decline](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/04/asia-markets-hang-seng-index-kospi-nikkei-225.html).

[Brent futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@LCO.1/) spiked 27.78% to $119.04, while [U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude futures](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/@CL.1/) jumped 30% to $118.46. The 30% jump in oil prices is the largest one day gain since late 1988, according to LSEG data.

The surge comes after major Middle Eastern oil producers, including Kuwait, Iran and the United Arab Emirates, cut oil production following the [closure of the Strait of Hormuz.](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/08/crude-oil-prices-today-iran-war.html)

Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) tumbled 6.48%, falling below the 53,000 mark for the first time since Feb. 6, while the Topix was down 5.8%**.**

[Softbank Group Corp](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/9984.T-JP/) was among the largest losers on the index, falling over 11%, while chip-related stocks such as [Advantest](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/6857.T-JP/) and [Lasertec](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/6920.T-JP/) was also down over 10% and 9%, respectively.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) fell 4.15%.

Hong Kong [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) also fell 3%, while the CSI 300 on mainland China was down 2%.

U.S. President Donald Trump, however, posted on Truth Social that a gain in "short term oil prices" was a "very small price to pay" for destroying Iran's nuclear threat.

"Only fools would think differently!" Trump added.

U.S. stock futures also tumbled on higher oil prices, with [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) futures down over 800 points or 1.75% lower.

S&P 500 futures were down 1.59%, while Nasdaq-100 futures slid 1.6%.
