[Micron](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/MU/) shares plummeted 10% on Monday, continuing the memory maker's significant [post-earnings sell-off](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/micron-mu-stock-q2-earnings-memory-shortage.html).

The company snapped a six-day slide on Friday with a modest gain, but with Monday's loss, the stock is down 30% since its [blowout earnings report](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/18/micron-mu-q2-earnings-report-2026.html) on March 18.

Other tech names also saw big losses Monday as oil climbed with the Iran war entering a fifth week and President Donald Trump threatening to destroy the country's oil facilities. Neocloud companies [CoreWeave](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/CRWV/) and [Nebius](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/NBIS/) were each down about 8%, while memory makers [SanDisk](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/SNDK/) and [Western Digital](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/WDC/) sank 7% and 9%, respectively.

Micron's strong [earnings report](https://investors.micron.com/news-releases/news-release-details/micron-technology-inc-reports-results-second-quarter-fiscal-2026) for the second quarter was fueled by insatiable demand for [artificial intelligence](https://www.cnbc.com/ai-effect/) chips.

Micron, [SK Hynix](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/HXSCL/) and [Samsung](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/SSU-FF/) are the major memory suppliers for high-performance AI chips from companies like [Nvidia](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/NVDA/). The surge in AI demand has led to a shortage.

After reporting earnings, [CEO Sanjay Mehrotra](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/19/micron-falls-blockbuster-earnings-chips-ai.html) told CNBC's "[Squawk on the Street](https://www.cnbc.com/squawk-on-the-street/)" that key Micron customers only get "half to two-thirds of their requirements" due to the supply crunch.

Micron shares are up 270% from one year ago, but most of those gains have retreated in 2026. The stock is only up about 2% year to date after the recent slide.
