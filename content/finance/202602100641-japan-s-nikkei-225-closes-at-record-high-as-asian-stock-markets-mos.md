Japan's [Nikkei 225](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.N225/) continued its post-election rally and reached new highs on Tuesday, amid gains in the broader Asian markets.

The Japanese market continues to ride the "[Takaichi trade](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/09/japan-election-sanae-takaichi-markets-yen-bonds-nikkei-225-jgb-160-usd-ldp.html)" in the wake of Prime Minister Sanae Takaichi's landslide victory in the Lower House.

The Nikkei 225 rose 2.28% to a new closing high of 57,650.54, while the Topix gained 1.9% to finish at a record of 3,855.28.

Shares of [Softbank Group Corp](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/9984.T-JP/) spiked as much as 11.95% on Tuesday after its subsidiary company [upgraded its full-year forecasts](https://www.softbank.jp/en/corp/ir/documents/presentations/fy2025/q3_earnings_summary/) for its financial year ending March 31, closing at a 10.68% gain. This follows a 6.3% gain on Monday.

South Korea's [Kospi](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.KS11/) rose marginally to 5,301.69, while the small-cap Kosdaq fell into negative territory, dropping 1.1% to 1,115.2.

Hong Kong's [Hang Seng index](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.HSI/) was up 0.39%, and mainland China's CSI 300 climbed 0.14%.

Australia's [S&P/ASX 200](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.AXJO/) ended flat at 8,867.4%.

Overnight in the U.S., tech stocks powered the [S&P 500](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.SPX/) to back-to-back gains, with the broad-based index rising 0.47%.

The [Dow Jones Industrial Average](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.DJI/) was marginally up to a record high of 50,135.87, and the [Nasdaq Composite](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/.IXIC/) jumped 0.9%.
