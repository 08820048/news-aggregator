Shares of [Globalstar](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/GSAT/) jumped more than 15% in extended trading on Wednesday after the Financial Times [reported](https://www.ft.com/content/abace066-fe93-4ff0-8378-d3c3eb49519c) [Amazon](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/AMZN/) is in talks to acquire the satellite communications company, citing people familiar with the matter.

The companies were still negotiating over some of the complexities of a deal following lengthy talks, the Times reported. One of the complicating factors is that [Apple](https://www.cnbc.com/quotes/AAPL/) took a 20% stake in Globalstar in 2024, according to the FT, as part of a [$1.5 billion investment](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/11/01/apple-commits-1point5-billion-to-globalstar-for-iphone-satellite-services.html) to boost the company's constellation and ground infrastructure.

Amazon declined to comment. Representatives from Globalstar didn't immediately respond to a request for comment.

A deal with Globalstar could bolster [Amazon Leo](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/24/amazon-allows-businesses-to-test-leo-satellite-service-starlink-rival.html), the company's nascent internet-from-space service. Amazon has launched about 200 satellites in low Earth orbit [since last April](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/28/amazon-launches-first-kuiper-satellites-in-bid-to-take-on-starlink.html), and it's aiming to roll out commercial service later this year.

The company intends to build out a constellation of roughly [7,700 satellites](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/10/amazon-gets-fcc-approval-to-launch-4500-leo-internet-satellites.html), though it has faced delays in its satellite deployment milestones. Amazon in January [asked](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/30/amazon-asks-fcc-to-extend-satellite-limit-as-it-buys-more-spacex-rides.html) the Federal Communications Commission for more time to meet a deadline that requires it to launch roughly 1,600 satellites by July 2026.

Amazon is vying to compete with SpaceX's [Starlink](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/04/09/amazon-kuiper-launches-first-satellites-takes-on-elon-musks-starlink.html), which has a significant head start with more than 10,000 satellites in orbit and over 9 million users.

[Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-10-30/globalstar-draws-spacex-interest-in-sale-process) reported last October that Globalstar was exploring a potential sale and held early discussions with Elon Musk's SpaceX.
