[Spider-Noir](https://www.ign.com/tv/spider-noir), the upcoming superhero series [starring Nicolas Cage](https://www.ign.com/articles/every-nicolas-cage-comic-book-based-movie-and-tv-show-so-far), debuts on MGM+ on May 25, 2026, and then on Prime Video on May 27, allowing Spider-Noir to qualify for the 78th [Primetime Emmy Awards](https://www.ign.com/tv/primetime-emmy-awards) just ahead of the Academy of Television Arts & Sciences’ May 31st cutoff date for consideration.

While you might think an action-oriented, period superhero show might be considered a drama, the awards site [Gold Derby](https://www.goldderby.com/tv/2026/spider-noir-comedy-emmys-nicolas-cage/) exclusively reports that Spider-Noir will actually campaign in the Comedy category of the Emmys.

As Gold Derby points out, that would pit Spider-Noir against the likes of perennial Emmy nominees Hacks, Only Murders in the Building, and Shrinking, or see Nic Cage in the running for the Outstanding Lead Actor in a Comedy Series against the likes of Martin Short or Jason Segel.

"Nicolas Cage delivers a singular, deeply original take on Ben Reilly, and I’m incredibly excited for audiences to see his performance," co-showrunner and executive producer Oren Uziel told Gold Derby.

"Alongside [an exceptional ensemble cast](https://www.ign.com/articles/nicolas-cages-spider-noir-series-casts-its-robbie-robertson) — including Lamorne Morris, Li Jun Li, Karen Rodriguez, Abraham Popoola, Jack Huston, and Brendan Gleeson — this was a truly unforgettable collaboration, and I hope audiences feel that spirit."

Set in an alternate universe 1930s New York City, Spider-Noir follows gumshoe Ben Reilly (Cage) as his latest case has him cross paths with the city’s biggest gangster, Silvermane (Gleeson), and his super-powered cronies.

Spider-Noir will be available to watch in both [Authentic Black & White](https://www.ign.com/videos/spider-noir-official-black-white-teaser-trailer) and [True-Hue Color](https://www.ign.com/videos/spider-noir-official-true-hue-color-teaser-trailer).

While you await the arrival of Spider-Noir, discover why the show proves [why we still need Sony’s Spider-Man universe](https://www.ign.com/articles/nic-cages-spider-noir-proves-why-we-still-need-sonys-spider-man-universe).

The 78th Primetime Emmy Awards will be broadcast on September 14, 2026.
