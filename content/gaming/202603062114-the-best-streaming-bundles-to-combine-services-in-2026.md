Ever since digital streaming was introduced as an alternative to cable, companies like Disney, Warner Bros, Netflix, and Amazon have been finding new ways to "revolutionize" monthly subscriptions, which has more or less come right back around to the concept of cable. (Only now every "channel" requires its own login and password, and costs at least $10 a month.) As the streaming service war wages on with constant changes, mergers and acquisitions, "bundles" are becoming more common as more streaming services are created, and some of these have gotten creative with including both digital and physical services.

The ability to combine multiple networks and major streaming platforms into a single subscription is becoming simpler than ever (thankfully) and gives better savings over subscribing individually to every service you'd want. Depending on your needs as a subscriber and your interests as a streamer and/or online shopper, the options available should tick many of your boxes. Check out our picks below for the best streaming bundles in 2026 to [help you save on your streaming bill](https://www.ign.com/articles/how-to-cut-down-your-monthly-streaming-subscription-bill).

### Disney+, Hulu, HBO Max Bundle

Includes subscriptions to all three services with ads.

The [Disney+, Hulu, and HBO Max bundle](https://www.ign.com/articles/how-to-get-disney-hulu-max-bundle) launched last year, and was immediately a standout deal with pricing starting at $19.99/month for an ad-supported tier. Because these price increases also affect all of the individual subscriptions, this bundle still leads to around the same amount in savings, and as such is still one of the overall [best deals in streaming](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-streaming-deals).

With access to [Disney+](https://www.ign.com/tech/disney-plus), viewers can stream all the Disney classics as well as new Disney properties and releases, such as Marvel TV shows and movies, [Pixar movies](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-ranking-pixar-movies), and everything in the [Star Wars universe](https://www.ign.com/articles/star-wars-movies-tv-shows-chronological-order).

This bundle also include [Hulu](https://www.ign.com/tech/hulu), which is home to hit original series ([The Bear](https://www.ign.com/articles/emmy-nominations-2024-shogun-the-bear-lead-list-fallout-and-x-men-97-score-big-nods), [Shōgun](https://www.ign.com/tv/shogun)) and documentaries (Minding the Gap, Flee, Enemies of the State), plus its library of movies and licensed shows from networks like FX, ABC, and Fox.

With the addition of HBO Max, subscribers also get access to that arsenal of HBO shows and HBO Max exclusives (The Penguin, [The Last of Us](https://www.ign.com/articles/how-to-watch-the-last-of-us-hbo-episodes), [Game of Thrones](https://www.ign.com/articles/where-to-stream-game-of-thrones)) and movies (which includes [Ghibli films](https://www.ign.com/articles/where-to-stream-studio-ghibli-movies) and and [all things DC](https://www.ign.com/articles/how-to-watch-the-dceu-movies-in-chronological-order)). You'll also get access to shows from HGTV and other Discovery channels since the Discovery+ merger a few years back. Though unfortunately this bundle pricing does not allow for [streaming HBO Max in 4K](https://www.ign.com/articles/how-to-stream-max-in-4k).

If a sports-focused package is more your thing, you can also try the [new Hulu, Disney+, and ESPN Unlimited bundle](https://www.ign.com/articles/disney-plus-launches-new-espn-unlimited-bundle-special-offer-with-disney-plus-and-hulu), which includes access to [live sports](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-streaming-services-for-sports).

### Disney+, Hulu, and ESPN Unlimited Bundle

ESPN Unlimited has now appeared on the scene, and Disney+ was [quick to launch a bundle to accompany it](https://www.ign.com/articles/disney-plus-launches-new-espn-unlimited-bundle-special-offer-with-disney-plus-and-hulu). With ESPN Unlimited (starting at $35.99/month), you'll get access to all of ESPN's linear networks, which host the likes of the NFL, NBA, and more. If you're considering the new service, I'd look into the full [Hulu + Live TV subscription](https://www.ign.com/articles/hulu-plus-live-tv-prices-and-plans), which includes ESPN Unlimited and offers a three-day free trial.

If you're someone who doesn't want to miss a single game, a more comprehensive option is DirecTV Stream. The base price for a DirecTV Stream plan is $79.99/month, but the incredible streaming options for a sports package inclusion starts at $98.99/month for the 'Choice' bundle, and $109.99/month for the 'Ultimate' bundle, all include regional sports networks and on demand streaming options for sports.

Another alternative is [fubo](https://www.ign.com/tech/fubotv), which also includes a free trial for new subscribers. fuboTV and Disney [recently struck a deal to combine services](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/walt-disney-fubo-tv-hulu-merger/), so this alternative may become the best option once that merger finishes.

If you want to test out any of the streaming services on this list before comitting to a bundle, there are a few [streaming service free trials](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-streaming-service-free-trials) you can take advantage of. Paramount+, Amazon Prime Video, Hulu, and DirecTV Stream all offer some sort of free trial for new subscribers.
