[_Clair Obscur: Expedition 33_](https://www.polygon.com/review/563522/clair-obscur-expedition-33-review-ps5-pc-xbox/) has been a [massively](https://www.polygon.com/clair-obscur-expedition-33-free-major-update/) [successful](https://www.polygon.com/clair-obscur-expedition-33-xbox-game-pass-record-2025/) debut for developer [Sandfall Interactive](https://www.polygon.com/clair-obscur-expedition-33-sandfall-developer-studio-copies-sold/). It also hasn't been without controversy. In December, the [Indie Game Awards revoked two of its awards](https://www.polygon.com/clair-obscur-expedition-33-indie-game-awards-goty-rescinded/) for its inclusion of AI-generated assets at launch. (They were quickly patched out.) Now, a French author is claiming the developer is forcing him to stop selling his newly released graphic novel, titled [_L' Académie Clair-Obscur_](https://www.drakoo.fr/bd/drakoo/surpuissant/l__academie_clair-obscur_-_vol_01/9782382330425).

In a [series of posts on X](https://x.com/0liviergay/status/2029908026769957358) on Friday, author Olivier Gay claimed he "received a lawyer's letter" from Sandfall, "which orders me to stop selling my comic published by Drakoo titled Académie Clair-Obscur, supposedly riding on the game's undeniable success" (via machine translation).

According to Gay, he pitched his graphic novel to Drakoo in 2019 and has a contract from March 2024 with the current title of the graphic novel. ([_Clair Obscur: Expedition 33_ was revealed in June 2024](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IDyqGZy78Ng&t=1s).) Though his graphic novel "has nothing to do with \[Sandfall's\] story," Gay is acquiescing because "I neither have the energy nor the money to engage in a legal battle, especially against French studios that I greatly appreciate." Gay states his comic will continue under a different name, and he "hope\[s\] it keeps finding success."

On Monday, Sandfall Interactive responded with a brief [post on X](https://x.com/SandfallGames/status/2031022213437046832). "Hello everyone, we've seen the discussions, thank you for bringing the situation to our attention. We're in contact with the publisher and Olivier to find a fair solution for everyone." When reached for comment, representatives for Sandfall Interactive redirected Polygon to the studio's statement on X.

Gay is right that his book has no similarities to _Expedition 33_ in terms of its story or characters; it follows a group of students at the Clair-Obscur Academy, a magic school. As some users on [X](https://x.com/ONELineZ0ZZ/status/2029953886585299150) have pointed out, a sticking point may be the typography. At a passing glance, there are similarities between the way the titles are presented on the graphic novel and in the game. "If I happen to walk past your comic in a store, I do get the impression of dealing with a product derived from the game," one person said. There are no official _Expedition 33_ tie-in comics at this time, but there is a [sick vinyl collection](https://www.polygon.com/gaming/615761/clair-obscur-expedition-33-soundtrack-ost-vinyl-preorder-date-release/) and an [art book that was mistakenly seized by government officials](https://www.polygon.com/expedition-33-art-book-monolith-set-customs-ancient-artifact/) for looking like an ancient artifact.

[Clair Obscur is a French translation of chiaroscuro](https://gamerant.com/clair-obscur-expedition-33-title-meaning-french-art-style-explained/) from Italian art history, originating in the Renaissance period. It's meant to refer to the juxtaposition between light and dark, which is a visual and thematic fundamental of _Expedition 33_. Sandfall has stated that Clair Obscur is meant to be the franchise name for its debut IP, with game director Guillaume Broche calling _Expedition 33_ "one of the stories that we want to tell in this franchise" in [an interview](https://www.gamespot.com/articles/clair-obscur-is-the-franchise-name-says-sandfall-as-it-stays-coy-on-what-comes-after-expedition-33/1100-6534302/) last summer.
