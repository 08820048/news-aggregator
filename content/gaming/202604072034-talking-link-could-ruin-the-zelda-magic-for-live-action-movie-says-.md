## Maybe Link shouldn't talk in the Zelda movie, says Majora's Mask creator

   ![Link reaches back to unsheathe the Master Sword in The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild 2](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/chorus/uploads/chorus_asset/file/23352688/zelda_botw_2.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Nintendo

For four long decades, [Nintendo](https://www.polygon.com/nintendo/) fans have puzzled over the long-eared enigma that is Link. The hero of the [Legend of Zelda](https://www.polygon.com/game/the-legend-of-zelda/10941/) games is known to be a man (Hylian?) of very few words. One of the few times we've ever heard Link talk was in the cornball 1989 animated series _The Legend of Zelda,_ where his trademark is acting like a douche and saying "[Excuuuuse me, Princess](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qzfXxkHrIBM)!" to Zelda in a snotty tone. Now, the art director of _[Majora's Mask](https://www.polygon.com/2015/2/4/7874951/zelda-majoras-mask-3d-review-legend-new-3ds-xl/),_ Takaya Imamura, is weighing in with his own thoughts on the longstanding debate. And it turns out, he's anti-yapping.

"The moment Link speaks, I can't help but worry a little that the 'Zelda magic' everyone has been nurturing in their hearts might just vanish into thin air," [Imamura wrote on X](https://x.com/ima_1966/status/2040582168888701019), in response to another post asking "Now that [_The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie/) has been out for a few days, how do we feel about the upcoming _Legend of Zelda_ movie?"

In response, a fan asked Imamura how he thought Nintendo might handle Link speaking in the upcoming [_Legend of Zelda_ movie, which is slated to hit theaters in 2027](https://www.polygon.com/movies/548607/zelda-movie-release-date-2027-nintendo/). Imamura [acknowledged](https://x.com/ima_1966/status/2040663319443546157) that the character will "obviously talk," but noted "it seems like \[Link will\] be a taciturn character." (Given how little the recent [_Mario Galaxy Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-review/) seems to care about giving its characters interesting stuff to do, I think it's entirely possible that Link won't speak at all.)

Imamura retired in 2021, but he worked at Nintendo for 32 years, designing the story and characters of _F-Zero_ and many of the supporting characters from the Star Fox series of video games. Not only was he responsible for creating the iconic falling moon in _Majora's Mask_, he also created the legendary weirdo [Tingle](https://www.polygon.com/23698108/tingle-history-legend-of-zelda-tears-of-the-kingdom/), a middle-aged man who dreams of becoming a fairy. Frankly, if he doesn't want to hear Link talk, maybe we ought to respect those wishes.
