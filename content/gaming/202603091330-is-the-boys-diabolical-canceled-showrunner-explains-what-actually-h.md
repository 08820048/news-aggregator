## The Boys: Diabolical isn't technically canceled, showrunner reveals

[_The Boys_](https://www.polygon.com/reviews/24176428/the-boys-season-4-review-new/) returns for its [fifth](https://www.polygon.com/the-boys-season-5-trailer/) and final season on April 8, but the franchise isn't going away anytime soon. There's a third season of the spinoff [_Gen V_](https://www.polygon.com/gen-v-season-2-hamish-linklater-cipher/) in the works, along with the prequel [_Vought Rising_](https://www.polygon.com/sdcc/24207080/the-boys-prequel-vought-rising-announced/) and [_The Boys: Mexico_](https://screenrant.com/the-boys-mexico-spinoff-timeline-crossover-update/). One offshoot of the hit superhero satire that apparently won’t be continuing is the animated anthology is [_The Boys: Diabolical_](https://www.polygon.com/22961941/the-boys-diabolical-episodes-list-grossest/), which franchise-runner Eric Kripe said had been canceled in a [2025 interview](https://www.polygon.com/the-boys-diabolical-season-2-canceled/).

However, in a recent interview, _The Boys: Diabolical_ showrunner Simon Racioppa told Polygon that his series wasn’t technically canceled — there just wasn't ever a plan to make more of it beyond season 1.

“It was always supposed to be a single one-off. It was basically done,” Racioppa said in an interview for season 5 of his other animated superhero series, [_Invincible_](https://www.polygon.com/tv/538050/invincible-season-3-finale-interview-marvel-dc-inspiration/). “We did it in 10 months, just basically as something to give the fans something to watch because the main series was delayed because of the pandemic and nobody knew what was happening.”

    ![A man wearing glasses and a white lab coat holds a vial of Compound V in The Boys: Diabolical](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/diabolical_s1_fg_107_00023407_still021.jpg?q=70&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=1)Image: Prime Video

Released in 2022, _The Boys: Diabolical_ spanned a wide range of tones and animation styles in its eight episodes. [_The Boys_ comic writer](https://www.polygon.com/2019/8/19/20803752/amazons-the-boys-dc-comics-references-superman-batman/) Garth Ennis took another crack at the franchise he started, Seth Rogen and Evan Goldberg paid tribute to the Looney Tunes, and [_Rick and Morty_](https://www.polygon.com/tv/600901/rick-and-morty-season-8-episode-1-dan-harmon-interview/) co-creator Justin Roiland co-wrote an episode. Racioppa’s explanation of the show’s fate paints a different picture than Kripke, who told [The Wrap](https://www.thewrap.com/gen-v-season-2-episode-7-eric-kripke-the-boys-franchise-future-interview/) in October 2025 that he wanted another season of _Diabolical._

"I don’t think there’s going to be a Season 2 of Diabolical," Kripke said. "It’s not for lack of us pushing. I think ultimately the viewer numbers weren’t there to justify a second season, sadly. Although, we love it and Simon \[Racioppa\], the showrunner, would be game to do it, but we haven’t gotten a go ahead on that."

Racioppa is co-showrunner of [_Invincible_](https://www.polygon.com/invincible-season-4-trailer-release-date-thragg/), which kicks off its fourth season on March 18, but he’d still like to return to _The Boys: Diabolical_.

“We have ideas,” he said. “There's certainly lots of space. Like _Invincible_, there's lots of great stories to tell in that world, but there's nothing I can announce about that right now, unfortunately.”
