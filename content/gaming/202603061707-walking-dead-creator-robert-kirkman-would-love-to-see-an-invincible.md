In 2020, Wizards of the Coast changed the trajectory of [_Magic: The Gathering_](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-magic-the-gathering/) when it released a series of new cards set in the world of [The Walking Dead](https://www.polygon.com/game/the-walking-dead/2306/), the franchise based on the long-running comic book series from Robert Kirkman and Tony Moore. Now, in a video interview ahead of the March 18 premiere of [_Invincible_ season 4](https://www.polygon.com/invincible-season-4-trailer-release-date-thragg/) on Prime Video, Kirkman tells Polygon he’d love to see his superhero series get the same treatment.

"I mean, I'm interested,” Kirkman says. “If you know some people at Wizards of the Coast, we can set something.”

_The Walking Dead_ was the first of Magic’s [limited release Secret Lair drops](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-secret-lair-print-to-demand-is-gone-for-good/) to feature entirely original cards rather than reprints of existing ones with alternate art based on popular IP. It set the stage for [Universes Beyond](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-2026-schedule-universes-beyond-preview/), the company’s name for sets tied to highly non-_Magic_ franchises like [_Avatar: The Last Airbender_](https://www.polygon.com/news/527510/avatar-the-last-airbender-magic-mtg-universes-beyond-reveal/) and [_Final Fantasy_](https://www.polygon.com/yuna-hope-of-spira-universes-beyond/). While [some players](https://www.polygon.com/magic-the-gathering-mtg-universes-beyond-ongoing-complaints-mark-rosewater/) don’t like seeing the game become the [cardboard equivalent of _Fortnite_](https://www.polygon.com/22639854/magic-the-gathering-fortnite-street-fighter-secret-lair-release-date-price/), the huge commercial success of these crossovers has led to them [dominating the game’s release schedule](https://www.polygon.com/magic-the-gathering-mtg-2025-review-universes-beyond/).

   ![Negan magic the gathering card walking dead crossover](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/224145_in_1000x1000.jpg?q=70&fit=crop&w=672&dpr=1)

While Wizards of the Coast might have to tone down the gore for an _Invincible_ set, it would otherwise be a natural fit for the game thanks to the franchise's sprawling world of superheroes, terrifying villains, and various alien species. Meanwhile, the company has already struck a deal with Marvel that kicked off with the release of a [Spider-Man set](https://www.polygon.com/are-magic-the-gathering-precons-good-for-beginners/) in 2025 and will continue with [_Marvel Super Heroes_](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-magic-the-gathering-marvel-super-heroes-reveal/) in June 2026.

An _Invincible_ set could use many of the same mechanics emphasized in the Marvel sets. Transform could represent secret identities or literally transforming characters such as Monster Girl, while Sagas could be used for key arcs such as the Invincible War, which would presumably unleash a horde of Invincible tokens when completed. For the space-based aspects of _Invincible_, Magic could borrow rules from [_Edge of Eternities_](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-magic-the-gathering/608804/edge-of-eternities-lore-setting-original-unique/). _Magic_ is adding sets based on [_The Hobbit_](https://www.polygon.com/mtg-magic-the-gathering-magiccon-news-announcements-spoilers/) and [Star Trek](https://www.polygon.com/star-trek/) in 2026, but maybe 2027 will be the year we all build _Invincible_ commander decks.
