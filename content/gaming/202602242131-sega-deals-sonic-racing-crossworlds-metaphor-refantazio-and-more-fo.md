Hopefully, you're ready to spend some money. Because [Sega](https://www.polygon.com/tag/genesis/) is ringing in the year of the horse with big price cuts on a long list of games worth playing. The company's annual Lunar New Year sale runs through March 1, offering deep discounts — up to 70% off — on a plethora of [PC](https://www.polygon.com/pc/), [PlayStation 5](https://www.polygon.com/tag/playstation/), [Xbox Series X](https://www.polygon.com/tag/xbox-series-x/), and [Nintendo Switch 2](https://www.polygon.com/nintendo-switch-2/) games.

Among the titles being on sale at reduced prices are Atlus' fantastic 2024 role-playing game [_Metaphor: ReFantazio_](https://www.polygon.com/game/metaphor-refantazio/444905/), a number of [Persona](https://www.polygon.com/tag/persona/) and [Yakuza](https://www.polygon.com/game/yakuza/11636/) games, and even last year's [_Sonic Racing: CrossWorlds_](https://www.polygon.com/sonic-racing-crossworlds-vs-mario-kart-world-review/), which is on sale for its lowest price yet. For our money, the best deal of the bunch is _Persona Collection_, which includes _Persona 5 Royal_, _Persona 4 Golden_, and _Persona 3 Portable_. This three-pack of games is on sale for $26.99, 70% off its normal $89.99 price tag. Unfortunately, that particular deal is only available for Xbox and Switch 2 players.

Still, the sale is loaded with hundreds of options that appeal to a wide variety of gamers. If you love management simulators, the [Two Point collection](https://store.steampowered.com/bundle/56132/?snr=1_1056_615_yearofthehorsesale_salesectiontabid2) is on sale. If strategy games are your thing, there are a bunch of [Total War games](https://store.steampowered.com/bundle/33761/Total_War_ROME_II__Ultimate_Edition/) waiting for you to take the battlefield. Even _[Football Manager 26](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3551340/Football_Manager_26/)_ is on sale.

[Sega's Lunar New Year sale](https://www.sega.com/deals?utm_campaign=20260223_nasa_sega_lunar_new_year_72hours_ret) runs on Steam and the storefronts for Nintendo, PlayStation, and Xbox through March 1.
