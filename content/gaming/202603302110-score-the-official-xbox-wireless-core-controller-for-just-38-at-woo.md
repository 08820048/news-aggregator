This controller is the same one that comes bundled with the Xbox Series X and Xbox Series S consoles. While it might not offer all the bells and whistles of the Xbox Elite Series 2, it’s still one of the [best Xbox Series X|S controllers](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-xbox-series-controller) you can grab, and it even makes for a [fantastic PC controller](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-pc-controller). From the familiar, comfortable design to the consistently reliable performance, there’s a lot to love, especially at this bargain price point. It even earned a “Great” 8/10 rating in our [Xbox Series X Controller review](https://www.ign.com/articles/xbox-series-x-controller-review) from 2020, with Mike Epstein noting it’s “a positive evolution.”

Xbox Wireless support brings a seamless, low-latency wireless connection to Microsoft’s console, and Bluetooth connectivity is available for phones, tablets, and PC. Plus, you can always opt for a wired USB-C link on the controller. An upgraded, more responsive D-pad, a new Share button, and additional grips are welcome improvements compared to previous models. Simple button mapping with the Xbox app and 3.5mm audio jack to listen on many [top gaming headsets](https://www.ign.com/articles/best-gaming-headset) are other highlight features that round out this versatile controller.
