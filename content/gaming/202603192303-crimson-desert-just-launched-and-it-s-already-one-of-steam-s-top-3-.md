[Crimson Desert](https://www.ign.com/games/crimson-desert) looks set to hit big Steam concurrent player numbers after it broke into the top three most-played games on Valve’s platform just minutes after launch.

Pearl Abyss’ hotly anticipated open world action game had looked set for a huge launch, given it was at the top of the Steam sales chart and had secured 3 million Steam Wishlists ahead of today’s release.

Rhys Elliott, head of market analysis at Alinea Analytics, [said](https://alineaanalytics.substack.com/p/crimson-desert-is-on-track-for-a) earlier this week that Crimson Desert was “on track for a killer launch on Steam.” According to Alinea Analytics estimates, Crimson Desert was approaching 400,000 pre-launch copies sold on Steam, representing gross revenues of over $20 million. “This game could really blow up, if it sticks the landing,” Elliott added.

On Steam, only eternally popular Valve games Counter-Strike 2 and Dota 2 have more players. At the time of this article’s publication, Crimson Desert was on its way to 250,000 concurrent players on Steam. Crimson Desert is also out on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X and S, so the total number of players will be even higher than Steam suggests, but neither Sony nor Microsoft make player numbers public.

The launch comes after [Pearl Abyss, publisher and developer of Crimson Desert, saw its stock price plunge nearly 30% in what was seen as a reaction to review scores](https://www.ign.com/articles/apparently-not-good-enough-for-investors-crimson-desert-publisher-stock-price-plunges-nearly-30-amid-78-metacritic-score).
