The [saga of _MindsEye_](https://www.polygon.com/news/605922/mindseye-ex-rockstar-president-weird-launch-week/), the [glitchy](https://www.polygon.com/gaming/606523/what-is-going-on-with-mindseye/) third-person action game that launched last summer, continues. On Tuesday, publisher IOI Partners and developer Build A Rocket Boy announced "the conclusion of their publishing collaboration for _MindsEye,_" according to a news release. This break-up comes just a couple weeks after Build A Rocket Boy went through a round of layoffs that [co-CEO Mark Gerhard effectively blamed on a conspiracy](https://www.polygon.com/mindseye-layoffs-blamed-on-criminal-activities/).

Once IOI is done transferring publisher status to _MindsEye_ developer Build A Rocket Boy, the studio "will assume sole publishing responsibilities going forward, ensuring continuity for the _MindsEye_ community and all partners."

IOI washing its hands clean of _MindsEye_ also means the Hitman collaboration with _MindsEye_ is now squashed, unfortunate news for the [handful of people who have been playing](https://steamdb.info/app/3265250/charts/) _MindsEye_ in recent days. (Looks like that [major "reset" update](https://www.polygon.com/mindseye-update-7-patch-notes/) didn't move the needle much; _Cyberpunk 2077_, this is not.) "However, BARB plans on working with partners on other projects in the future," the news release states, so there's a chance Build A Rocket Boy could collaborate with other developers and publishers on future content (if it can find any takers).

Where _MindsEye_ goes next is anyone's guess. It had a disastrous launch, leading to [damage control that made the mess](https://www.polygon.com/news/606496/mindseye-twitch-youtube-stream-cancel-glitches/) even worse. BARB is also "moving toward prosecution" against the "criminal activity" wrought upon the studio at the game's launch, [according to Gerhard](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/buildarocketboy_today-is-a-very-difficult-day-for-our-studio-activity-7435031513055784960-2iQD/).

As for IOI, what's next is clear: its James Bond game is coming out on May 27 after it was [delayed from a March launch](https://www.polygon.com/james-bond-game-007-first-light-delayed-may-2026/). "\[_First Light_\] is about a young man looking for purpose that finds destiny," cinematic and narrative director [Martin Emborg told Polygon](https://www.polygon.com/new-james-bond-007-first-light/).
