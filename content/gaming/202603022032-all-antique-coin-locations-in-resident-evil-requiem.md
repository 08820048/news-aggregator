**Antique coins** in [_Resident Evil Requiem_](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem/) are a special currency that you need in order to upgrade Grace's attributes. While some coins are literally just waiting to be picked up in obvious places, others are hidden and even locked in safes. By finding most coins, you ensure Grace will have what it takes to face the challenges along her way.

However, **depending on your difficulty**, **the antique coin locations will change**. In this guide, you'll find the locations of all antique coins in **casual and standard (modern and classic) difficulties**.

To help you collect these premium currencies, here's a list of **every antique coin location in** **_Resident Evil Requiem_**.

\[**Ed. note:** This guide contains minor spoilers for _Resident Evil Requiem_.\]

**Update (March 2):** We've added the two antique coins to the list.

* * *

## All antique coin locations in Resident Evil Requiem

Antique coins are used in _Resident Evil Requiem_ to unlock Grace's upgrades in the Parlor. These are key upgrades that will make the rest of your journey a bit easier, so obtaining all of them is definitely something you want to aim for.

In total, you need **17 coins to unlock every upgrade** in the Parlor, and **we have found 23 antique coins** scattered throughout the Care Center on casual and standard difficulty. Below, we've listed each antique coin's location in chronological order of when you'll find them.

## Antique coin #1 — Nurse Station

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 1](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_1.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

When you first enter the **Nurse Station**, **climb on top of the cart** and look on top of the locker to find this coin.

## Antique coin #2 — Guard Office

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 2](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_2.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

On the **desk** near the door in the **Guard Office**.

## Antique coin #3 — Hallway outside Filing Room (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 3](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_3.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Head west of the Filing Room in the West Wing to find this antique coin **on a small cabinet in the corridor**.

## Antique Coin #4 — Bar & Lounge (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 4](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_4.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

This antique coin is **on the piano keys** in the **Bar & Lounge**.

## Antique coin #5 — Bar & Lounge (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 14](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_13.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Inside the Bar & Lounge, you'll also encounter a **Singer**, a screaming zombie. If you kill them, they'll drop an antique coin. While you can fight her whenever you want, it's best to wait until you can **approach the Bar & Lounge from the Kitchenette**, since she is patrolling the corridor alone.

## Antique coins #6-8 — Bar and Lounge (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 15, 16 and 17](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_14.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Behind the counter in the **Bar and Lounge**, you'll find a **safe**, which contains three antique coins. For more information on how to open the safe, check out our [Bar and Lounge safe](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-re9-bar-lounge-safe-code-location-how-to-open/) guide.

## Antique coin #9 — Parlor (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 5](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_5.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

You find this coin **on the** **roulette table** inside the Parlor.

## Antique coin #10 — Parlor (West Wing)

    ![A montage using two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location where you can obtain an antique coin by shooting at the roulette table](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/rerequem_guide_coins_10_2.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

To obtain this coin, **shoot at the 7, 15, or 29 on the table**. Then, interact with the roulette. If the **ball lands on the exact number you shot** at, an antique coin will appear over the number on the table.

## Antique coin #11 — Parlor (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 6](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_6.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Still inside the Parlor, this antique coin is located **on the counter**.

## Antique coin #12 — Parlor (West Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 7](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_7.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

This coin is **inside a small green vase on the counter** beside the larger vase on the floor in the Parlor.

## Antique coins #13-15 — Examination Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 18,19, and 20](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_18.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Inside the **Examination Room**, you'll find a **safe**, which contains **three antique coins**, on a shelf in the corner of the room. For more information on how to open the safe, check out our [Examination Room safe](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-re9-examination-room-safe-code-how-to-open/) guide.

## Antique coin #16 — Waiting Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 8](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_8.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

To find this antique coin, look for the **small table on the east side of the** **Waiting Room**, in front of the door from where you enter this room for the first time.

## Antique coin #17 and #18 — Waiting Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 9 and 10](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_9.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

In the **southwest corner of the Waiting Room**, you'll find an open safe behind the counter. Inside, you find **two antique coins**.

## Antique coin #19 — Room east of Waiting Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 11](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_10.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

From the **Waiting Room**, follow **the corridor to the east** to find a small table between two sofas. On top of it, this antique coin is waiting.

## Antique coin #20 —Room east of Waiting Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the antique coins number 19](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/rerequem_guide_coins_19.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

In the same room as antique coin #19, you'll find a **small jar on top of the cabinet**, beside the table where you found the previous coin. **Break the jar** to find an antique coin inside.

## Antique coin #21 — Conference Room(East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 12](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_11.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

As you progress in the campaign, you inevitably need to explore the **Conference Room**. Before you enter the larger room, you'll find the antique coin on a **desk**.

## Antique coin #22 — Conference Room (East Wing)

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 13](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_12.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

The second coin you can obtain in the Conference Room **drops from the Singer**. She is blocking your path to a key quest item, so you'll need to deal with her to reach it. Once she's dead, collect the coin from her body.

## Antique coin #23 — Courtyard

    ![A montage of two Resident Evil Requiem images showing the location of the Antique Coin number 21](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/rerequem_guide_coins_21.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Paulo Kawanishi/Polygon | Source images: Capcom

Immediately after you **leave the Care Center**, you gain access to the **courtyard**. Go down the first set of stairs and turn around to find a set of four chairs. **On the southwesternmost chair**, you'll find an antique coin.
