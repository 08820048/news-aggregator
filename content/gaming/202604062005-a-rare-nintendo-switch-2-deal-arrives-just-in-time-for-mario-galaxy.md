Regardless of how you feel about [_The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_](http://polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie/), it’s interesting that Nintendo hasn’t released a new Mario title to coincide with its release (we’re not quite counting the _[Super Mario Wonder](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-bros-wonder-meetup-bellabel-park-switch-2-review/)_ [Nintendo Switch 2 upgrade](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-bros-wonder-meetup-bellabel-park-switch-2-review/)). Still, if you’ve been caught by Mushroom Kingdom Mania and haven’t yet picked up a Switch 2, there is a rare deal on Nintendo’s latest system that could save you a few bucks.

As someone who missed the original releases of both Mario Galaxy games, I’ve been having an absolute blast playing through them on Switch 2 with my son. It’s also nice to be able to enjoy price parity for Switch 2’s physical and digital versions, [at least for now](https://www.polygon.com/physical-vs-digital-switch-2-games-pricing/).

Of course, you’re also getting the Switch 2 console, which is one [we’ve been particularly impressed by](http://polygon.com/nintendo-switch-2/606757/review/). It’s also worth noting that the system is in for a big first full year on the market in 2026. We’ve already been enjoying the hell out of _[Pokémon Pokopia](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-pokopia/), [Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream](https://www.polygon.com/game/tomodachi-life-living-the-dream/548029/)_ is just weeks away, _Final Fantasy 7 Rebirth_ will likely be an incredible technical feat on the console, and there’s plenty more to come. Buy for the Mario classics, stay for _The Duskbloods,_ is what we’re saying.
