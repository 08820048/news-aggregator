Nintendo has released a major new update for [Mario Kart World](https://www.ign.com/games/mario-kart-world), which rebalances items, adjusts recovery times, and adds another battle mode.

Mario Kart World version 1.6.0 is available to download on Nintendo Switch 2 now, with its most prominent addition being Bomb-omb Blast, the battle mode option which returns from Mario Kart 8 Deluxe. As with the standard battle mode option with balloons, the aim of the game here is to damage your opponent and survive to become the last player standing. Except now you're armed with bombs.

More interesting, perhaps, are the more subtle changes that Nintendo continues to make to the Mario Kart World meta, which will undoubtedly shift again following the release of this new patch.

Recovery time — that period of invincibility after you've crashed or spun out in a race — has now been varied between characters, so that lighter characters are safe for less time. Heavier characters now gain more invincibility time, meanwhile, which may make them more attractive to some players.

The bullet bill item has been buffed with faster speed on certain parts of specific tougher courses, and Nintendo states that "it is now easier to follow a shortcut route immediately after" using one. Presumably this is an adjustment to you dropping speed after exiting the bullet bill state, which slows you down further if you're off-road.

The boomerang item has seen a nerf, meanwhile, with cuts to its range and the number of consecutive throws allowed. Nintendo also says it has "adjusted the probability of items that can be obtained from item boxes during races", though frustratingly does not provide further detail on exactly how.

There's no mention in the patch notes to any further tweaks to Mario Kart World's actual world layout, which [Nintendo has adjusted previously to spruce up some of its duller areas](https://www.ign.com/articles/mario-kart-world-update-140-tweaks-track-layouts-adds-custom-item-rules-now-lets-you-see-what-music-is-playing). We'll keep an eye on what fans find may or may not have changed as Nintendo continues to update its Switch 2 launch title. Certainly, the company has been busy — dozens of smaller bug fixes are also included in today's update, which you can read about in the full patch notes below.

## Mario Kart World - Version 1.6.0 (Released 30th March 2026)

### **General**

-   Added Bob-omb Blast as a Battle mode.
    -   You can have up to 10 Bob-ombs on hand at a time.
    -   The distance Bob-ombs can be thrown depends on how long you hold the L Button.
-   Adjusted the performance of Bullet Bill.
    -   Increased their range of lateral movement.
    -   It is now easier to follow a shortcut route immediately after using Bullet Bill.
    -   Increased Bullet Bill’s speed on parts of the Bowser's Castle, Starview Peak, and Rainbow Road courses.
-   Adjusted the performance of the boomerang.
    -   Reduced the boomerang’s range.
    -   Reduced the number of consecutive throws allowed.
-   Adjusted the probability of items that can be obtained from item boxes during races.
-   Adjusted the invincibility time after spinning or crashing during a race so that it varies depending on the character and vehicle.
    -   The heavier the weight, the longer the invincibility time.
-   Made it so that you won’t get crushed by things, such as Thwomps, while spinning or crashing.
-   It is now possible to be hit by lightning and Spiny Shells immediately after being crushed by things, such as Thwomps.
-   Made it so that things like Bob-ombs and Bananas don't bounce on top of the vines of Ivy Piranha Plants.
-   When playing Single Player or 1p during Online Play and Wireless Play, up to two warnings will be displayed when items like Red Shells or Spiny Shells come from behind.
-   Shortened the time until the roulette stops to determine the course in Online Play and Wireless Play.
