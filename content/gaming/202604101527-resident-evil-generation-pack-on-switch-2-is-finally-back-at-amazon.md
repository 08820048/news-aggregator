After a long period of being out of stock online, the Resident Evil Generation Pack on [Nintendo Switch 2](https://www.ign.com/tech/nintendo-switch-2) is available once again at Amazon ([see it here](https://zdcs.link/QqJq8B?object_uuid=06421001-0f25-4f72-bd41-89d2c10704ee&t=article)).

[![Resident Evil Generation Pack - Nintendo Switch 2](https://assets-prd.ignimgs.com/2026/04/10/resident-evil-generation-pack-nintendo-switch-2-1775824024002.jpg?width=220&format=jpg&auto=webp&quality=80)](https://zdcs.link/QqJq8B?object_uuid=06421001-0f25-4f72-bd41-89d2c10704ee&t=article "Resident Evil Generation Pack - Nintendo Switch 2")

### Resident Evil Generation Pack - Nintendo Switch 2

If you’ve had your eyes on this stacked horror collection for Nintendo’s latest handheld, which was released in celebration of Resident Evil’s 30th Anniversary, it’s now available to buy at Amazon for $89.99. You’ll want to act fast to grab it, though, as who knows how much longer it will be available at the retailer.

Alongside the recently released [Resident Evil Requiem](https://www.ign.com/games/resident-evil-requiem) - a game we said, “successfully splices two separate strains of survival horror together into the one highly infectious new mutation” in our [review](https://www.ign.com/articles/resident-evil-requiem-review) - this pack also comes with [Resident Evil 7 biohazard](https://www.ign.com/games/resident-evil-7-biohazard) Gold Edition and [Resident Evil Village](https://www.ign.com/games/resident-evil-village) Gold Edition.

For Resident Evil 7’s Gold Edition, you’ll get the base game alongside the “Not a Hero” chapter and all other DLC. It’s a similar case for Resident Evil Village Gold Edition, which also comes with the Winters’ Expansion and the Trauma Pack to have along with the base game, so there’s plenty to keep you busy.

It’s worth mentioning that all of these games are ones we think very highly of, too. Resident Evil Requiem landed with a 9/10 score in its review this year, while Village [earned an 8/10](https://www.ign.com/articles/resident-evil-village-review) and Resident Evil 7 biohazard [earned a 7.7/10](https://www.ign.com/articles/2017/01/23/resident-evil-7-biohazard-review) in their respective reviews.

We called Village "a genuinely engrossing and increasingly combat-heavy continuation of the Ethan Winters story", while Biohazard is one we said is "a fresh take on the Resident Evil series that fondly recalls the classics even as it takes a bold new direction" when it released back in 2017.

Personally, I’m a very big fan of all three of these Resident Evil games, and being able to take this terrifying trio with you on-the-go to play on Switch 2 is a fun way to experience them. Again, this collection may not be in stock for long at Amazon, so it’s worth it to pick it up now in case it sells out again.
