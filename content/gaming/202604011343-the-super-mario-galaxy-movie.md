Everything you need to know about Nintendo and Illumination's latest team-up

   ![The Super Mario Galaxy Movie – Final Trailer 2-8 screenshot](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/the-super-mario-galaxy-movie-final-trailer-2-8-screenshot.jpeg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Illumination, Nintendo

After the massive success of _The Super Mario Bros. Movie_, Mario, Luigi, Peach and the rest of the gang have returned for a galaxy-spanning adventure. And this time, Yoshi's there, too!

Below, you'll find everything you need to know about _The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_, from our review and Easter egg explainers to interviews with the cast and producers.

    ##### [The Super Mario Galaxy Movie is dazzling, candy-coated chaos](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-review/ "The Super Mario Galaxy Movie is dazzling, candy-coated chaos")
    
       ![Mario and Luigi introduce Yoshi, a small green dinosaur, to Toad, a short guy with a mushroom hat, in The Super Mario Galaxy Movie](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/mario-galaxy-movie-mario-luigi-yoshi-toad.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Illumination/Nintendo
    
    [_The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-trailer-easter-eggs/) is aptly named. Nintendo and Illumination Entertainment’s previous Mario animated film, 2023’s [_The Super Mario Bros. Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/23597125/super-mario-bros-movie-rap-commercial-easter-eggs/), is largely about brothers Mario and Luigi discovering a new home and finding purpose beyond plumbing, exhibiting a twinge of character growth along the way. The sequel is instead focused on expanding the Nintendo Cinematic Universe with a constellation of new characters (many of whom have too little to do in _The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_) and an emptiness that rivals outer space.
    
    Much of what we said about [_The Super Mario Bros. Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/reviews/23669990/super-mario-bros-movie-review-references/) applies to its sequel. Just like its predecessor, _Galaxy_ is stuffed with references to Mario games released over the past 40 years, and it relies on nostalgia and dazzling visuals to carry a paper-thin plot. The sequel is even less focused, yanking in references from [across the Nintendo IP library](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-surprise-cameo-star-fox-mccloud/) to fill the gaps between story beats.

    [Nintendo](https://www.polygon.com/nintendo/) and Illumination’s new animated adventure [_The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-trailer-easter-eggs/) is a massive leap for the franchise. Mario, Peach, and Luigi venture far beyond the Mushroom Kingdom’s confines in the sequel to 2023’s [_The Super Mario Bros. Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/reviews/23669990/super-mario-bros-movie-review-references/), rubbing elbows with a surprising array of Nintendo characters on their journey.
    
    Some of those character cameos are expected: _The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_ [welcomes old-school Super Mario villains like Wart](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-trailer-nintendo-direct-yoshi-actor/) (a mostly forgotten bad guy from _Super Mario Bros. 2_) and ROB (the robot toy that was packaged with the original NES). Other cameos are more surprising, like the [inclusion of Fox McCloud from the Star Fox games](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-surprise-cameo-star-fox-mccloud/), a major crossover for Nintendo, the likes of which are typically [only seen in the Super Smash Bros. games](https://www.polygon.com/e3/2018/6/12/17452068/super-smash-bros-ultimate-fighters-roster-nintendo-switch/).

    ##### [Donald Glover reveals how he landed the role of Yoshi in The Super Mario Galaxy Movie](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-donald-glover-yoshi/ "Donald Glover reveals how he landed the role of Yoshi in The Super Mario Galaxy Movie")
    
       ![mario galaxy movie yoshi baby mario luigi toad](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/mario-galaxy-movie-yoshi-baby-mario-luigi-toad.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Illumination, Nintendo
    
    When Nintendo and Illumination revealed that actor-musician Donald Glover was [stepping into the role of Yoshi](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-trailer-nintendo-direct-yoshi-actor/) for [_The Super Mario Galaxy Movie_](https://www.polygon.com/super-mario-galaxy-movie-surprise-cameo-star-fox-mccloud/), the consensus was that it was a surprisingly good and unexpected casting choice. While [Chris Pratt voicing Mario](https://www.polygon.com/22925474/chris-pratt-mario-voice-movie-trailer/) was initially [controversial](https://www.polygon.com/23396736/mario-movie-chris-pratt-character-reinvention/) and Jack Black voicing Bowser felt spot-on, Glover as a cartoon dinosaur felt like a less obvious but inspired fit.
    
    Even Mario creator Shigeru Miyamoto and Illumination CEO Chris Meledandri had some reservations about Glover as Yoshi, mostly on the actor’s behalf. He did know that he’d really just be saying "Yoshi," right?
