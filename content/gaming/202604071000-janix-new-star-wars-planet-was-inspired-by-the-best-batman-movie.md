## How Batman inspired the best new Star Wars planet since Coruscant

   ![Janix Star Wars Maul](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/hfns8fwxiaao53s.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Lucasfilm

After years of [Star Wars](http://polygon.com/star-wars) focusing on the ever-popular city planet of Coruscant, [_Star Wars: Maul — Shadow Lord_](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars-maul-shadow-lord-review/) is finally taking us somewhere new: [Janix](https://www.starwars.com/databank/janix).

Described as a planet that has “largely escaped the attention of the Empire,” on the surface, Janix is self-governed by its own democratic government, with the help of local law enforcement officers like Captain Brander Lawson. However, as established by the first two episodes of [_Maul: Shadow Lord_](https://www.polygon.com/maul-shadow-lord-master-daki-obi-wan-kenobi/), look closer, and Janix is in fact a hive of scum and villainy, with gangers and crime syndicates thriving under the cover of darkness. It’s the perfect place for Maul to [rebuild his criminal empire](https://www.polygon.com/maul-shadow-lord-rook-cast-shadow-collective-explained/).

For _Shadow Lord’s_ executive producers Athena Yvette Portillo, Matt Michnovetz, and Brad Rau, one of their first instructions from Star Wars Lucasfilm’s president, Dave Filoni, was to ensure that Janix was its own character. To do this, the _Shadow Lord_ team drew on several sources of inspiration, from [Syd Mead](https://www.instagram.com/p/DTfKt59gdyM/) to [Blade Runner](https://www.polygon.com/alien-earth-noah-hawley-blade-runner-synthetics/). However, one particular city stood out from the crowd.

    ![A group of Mandalorians and Zabrak climb a skyscraper in Maul Shadowlord](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/maul_trailer1_still_1.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Image: Lucasfilm/Disney

“We looked at a lot of Gotham through various versions of Batman. _Dark Knight_ was one of our touchstones,” Brad Rau, who also serves as Maul: _Shadow Lord’s_ supervising director tells Polygon.”We wanted to do something that looked very different from Coruscant; that was one of the big things Dave put to us. And I think we did it.”

The comparison to Christopher Nolan’s _Batman: The Dark Knight_ trilogy is quite fitting. Like Nolan’s [Gotham](https://www.polygon.com/tabletop-games/507362/batman-gotham-city-chronicles-rpg-monolith-crowdfunding/), Janix manages to appear gray and industrial during the day while coming alive at night, further enhanced by the paint-like brushstroke aesthetic used to bring _Maul: Shadow Lord_ to life.

Furthermore, each sector of Janix tells a story that reflects how Gotham is portrayed in Nolan’s trilogy. In earlier films like _Batman Begins_, some areas depict Gotham as narrow and filled with slums, featuring dark, industrial zones largely run by gangs. The same can be said for Janix, where Maul’s base of operations appears to be in the lower, most degraded part of the city planet, from which the only way out is to climb up into the light.

    ![An image from Maul: Shadow Lord of the city Janix.](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/1-3.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Image: Lucasfilm

We've described _Maul: Shadow Lord_ as a sci-fi noir crime-thriller, so for it to take inspiration from one of DC Universe’s most tragic cities, where crime is just an everyday part of life, makes a lot of sense.
