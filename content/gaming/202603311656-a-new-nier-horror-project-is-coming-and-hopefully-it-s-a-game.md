Nier: Cosmic Horror could be anything, but fans are hoping it's a follow-up to Nier: Automata

## A new Nier horror project is coming, and hopefully it's not an April Fools' joke

   ![A close-up shot of 2B from Nier: Automata](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/chorus/uploads/chorus_asset/file/23655225/ss_8b29f7e1ce9a8b9313dc3eb50dbe76a4cf94eef9.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: PlatinumGames/Square Enix

[Yoko Taro](https://www.polygon.com/2017/7/11/15953890/yoko-taro-favorite-ps4-games/), haven't your fans suffered enough? Evidently not, otherwise I can't explain why an official _Nier_ account has decided that March 31 was the best time to vaguely announce something called _Nier: Cosmic Horror_, a new "project" that may or may not be a game.

[The teaser is only 21 seconds](https://x.com/nier_jpn/status/2038994733087830493?s=46&t=UK2blJ9a5k-b_Nx2smVP0g), during which we see the series mascot Emil smiling menacingly in the background. As unsettling music plays, the teaser reveals a biomechanical monstrosity that could have been designed by H.R. Giger. "Is hope born from nothingness?" the post reads. "Or is it despair disguised as salvation?"

It's been nearly a decade since [_Nier: Automata_](https://www.polygon.com/game/nier-automata/38754/), the beloved existential action game known for its twists and multiple endings. Since then, protagonist 2B has shown up in all sorts of places, from [_Rainbow Six Siege_](https://www.polygon.com/23460583/rainbow-six-siege-nier-replicant-maverick-skin-yorha-2b-dlc/) to [_Overwatch_](https://www.polygon.com/overwatch-nier-automata-collab-skins-2b-kiriko-vendetta/). Series director Yoko Taro has also seemed open to a new game in the universe, once notoriously claiming that he would [consider doing nearly anything if he got the money for it](https://www.siliconera.com/yoko-taro-says-hed-nier-remake-just-anything-gets-money/). So far, that's mostly resulted in re-releases, collaborations with other games, or tertiary projects, like concerts and [anime](https://www.polygon.com/23368841/nier-automata-anime-trailer-premiere-date-2023/).

The world is yearning for a full-on new Nier game, though, which makes this announcement kind of cruel, as fans are left wondering if they're being trolled or not. In Yoko Taro's defense, [the game developer revealed in 2025](https://kotaku.com/please-stop-cancelling-yoko-taros-games-2000644949) that he's been busy since _Automata,_ except his projects apparently keep getting canceled. At the time, Taro joked he didn't really mind that since he still got paid.

“I don’t view \[the cancellations\] as a negative thing,” Taro said. “I believe that if I’m going to release something weird, I’d be better off not releasing anything at all.”

Whatever _Nier: Cosmic Horror_ turns out to be, a more frightening take on the franchise makes sense. The franchise is an unsettling, constant assault of tragedy and death. One could argue that _Nier: Automata_ is _already_ a psychological horror game for protagonist 2B, who is forced to shut down her emotions as she fulfills gruesome orders.

One reason to be hopeful here is that, every so often, Japanese developers legitimately announce real games on Aprils Fools. Publisher Square Enix did say [_Nier: Automata_ would somehow continue in February 2026](https://www.polygon.com/nier-automata-sequel-ten-million-announcement/). Cross your fingers that this isn't a fake-out.

At this point, [even The Weeknd is waiting](https://x.com/lofi_lover0930/status/2038298627739943331) for a new Nier game.
