Better Call Saw

## The most ruthless rebel in Star Wars is finally getting the spinoff he deserves

   ![Saw Gerrera (Forest Whitaker) in Rogue One: A Star Wars Story](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/saw-gerrera-rogue-one.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Lucasfilm

In celebration of the 10th anniversary of [_Rogue One: A Star Wars Story_](https://www.polygon.com/2016/12/13/13869428/rogue-one-star-wars-story-review/), [Marvel Comics](https://www.polygon.com/marvel/) is rolling out a promising [slate of one-shots](https://www.comicsbeat.com/rogue-one-10th-anniversary/). Hitting the stands from May through September, these single issues will zoom in on some of the era’s most compelling characters: [Cassian Andor](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/598309/cassian-andor-force-user-luck-obi-wan-kenobi-easter-egg/), [Jyn Erso](https://www.polygon.com/2016/12/20/14033436/rogue-one-ending-star-wars-jyn-erso/), Chirrut and Baze, [Darth Vader](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars-master-of-evil-darth-vader-fractured-psyche/), and [Saw Gerrera](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/566318/saw-gerrera-andor-season-2/) in his first-ever leading role in a comic.

As with the rest of the [Star Wars](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/) prequel one-shots, the Saw issue features a cover by artist David Marquez, who previously did a series of covers for the main _Star Wars_ (2015) title, issues #38-55. Written by Marc Bernardin, the author of 2024’s _Mace Windu_ miniseries, and with art by Gabriel Guzman, the issue follows the Onderonian in the years after _The Clone Wars_, 10 years before the events of _Rogue One_ take place.

A decade before he would ultimately give his life to the cause in _Rogue One_, the issue promises to offer a peek into Saw’s complex morality by placing him on a mission behind enemy lines. Seeking out an advantageous bit of information from the heart of Imperial power, Saw will be forced to make difficult decisions in order to achieve his goal.

The revolutionary Saw is well known for his dedication to bringing down the Empire at all costs, even when that put him at odds with the rest of the Rebel Alliance. After suffering the loss of his beloved sister Steela while they fought to free their home world from Separatist influence, his dedication to the cause only sharpened. Adopting the young Jyn Erso when she was tragically separated from her parents as a child, Saw eventually abandoned her for what he felt was the greater good. Jyn, still struggling with Saw's betrayal, she also has a one-shot in this series in which she attempts to free a scared young prisoner of the Empire.

At various turns a terrorist or a hero, Saw’s “by any means necessary” approach has put him at odds with pretty much every faction in the galaxy at one point or another, including, occasionally, himself. [Baffling some _Star Wars_ fans](https://www.reddit.com/r/StarWars/comments/1kqot48/saw_gerrera_is_the_most_baffling_star_wars/) while remaining [a favorite for others](https://www.reddit.com/r/andor/comments/1ohy8gg/some_thoughts_about_saw_gerrera/), the one-shot is sure to deliver more insight into Gerrera’s complex, sometimes paradoxical morality.
