## Seth MacFarlane is making a Dungeon Crawler Carl TV show for Peacock

   ![Carl has a fluffy white cat named Princess Donut on his shoulder as he runs from an explosion on the cover of the graphic novel version of Dungeon Crawler Carl](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/81lstefoezl-_sl1500_.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1200&h=675)Image: Vault Comics

Matt Dinniman’s hit sci-fi book series Dungeon Crawler Carl follows a contestant on an alien reality TV show forced to navigate a dungeon created by stripping Earth of all its resources. [Variety reports](https://variety.com/2026/tv/news/dungeon-crawler-carl-tv-series-peacock-seth-macfarlane-1236705436/) that [Seth MacFarlane](https://www.polygon.com/2016/5/4/11593702/family-guy-seth-macfarlane-fox/) and Dinniman will executive produce a live-action adaptation of the series for Peacock, so you can watch aliens watch Carl compete.

Chris Yost, whose writing credits include [_Thor: Ragnarok_](https://www.polygon.com/2017/10/19/16499348/thor-ragnarok-review-avengers-marvel-2017/) and [_Thor: The Dark World_](https://www.polygon.com/2019/5/2/18525888/avengers-endgame-spoilers-thor-the-dark-world-revisited/), will write the series and serve as an executive producer. He’ll have plenty of material to work with: Dinniman has written seven Dungeon Crawler Carl books and an eighth, [_A Parade of Horribles_](https://www.amazon.com/Parade-Horribles-Dungeon-Crawler-Carl/dp/B0FY26HLTD), will be released on May 12. The series follows Carl, a Coast Guard veteran, as he tries to survive and entertain viewers with the help of his ex-girlfriend’s cat, Princess Donut. That means battling aliens, monsters, and even other human survivors.

    ![Carl wearing a military jacket runs from a group of goblins driving a very spiky tank in Dungeon Crawler Carl](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/81xbhurusbl-_sl1500_.jpg?q=70&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=1)Image: Dandy House

_Dungeon Crawler Carl_ is one of the definitive series in the LitRPG genre, which draws on tropes from tabletop role-playing games and dungeon-crawler video games to portray characters battling monsters, collecting gear, and acquiring new abilities. Initially self-published, the series has now [sold six million copies and been translated into 20 languages](https://www.nytimes.com/2025/12/12/books/review/dungeon-crawler-carl-matt-dinniman.html).

It’s also turning into a media empire. Playmates will be debuting several lines of [Dungeon Crawler Carl toys](https://www.polygon.com/dungeon-crawler-carl-toys-samantha/) at San Diego Comic-Con in July. Renegade Game Studios is [launching a Backerkit campaign](https://www.backerkit.com/c/projects/renegade-game-studios/dungeon-crawler-carl-rpg-unstoppable/launch_party?ref=bk-ec-62798) on April 14 for a TTRPG that will let players create their own dungeon crawlers. The company is also crowdfunding for a deckbuilder that can be played solo or cooperatively, with cards representing gear and abilities that can be upgraded as you play. A 2025 Backerkit campaign for [_Crocodile_](https://www.backerkit.com/c/projects/vault-comics/dungeon-crawler-carl-an-original-graphic-novel-side-story), a graphic novel extension of Dungeon Crawler Carl, raised $2.6 million. A graphic novel adaptation of the first Dungeon Crawler Carl book is [releasing on May 19](https://www.amazon.com/Dungeon-Crawler-Carl-Graphic-Novel/dp/1638493650/).
