[Game of Thrones](https://www.polygon.com/game-of-thrones/) has officially leveled up from hit series to franchise. While [_House of the Dragon_](https://www.polygon.com/house-of-the-dragon/) confirmed there was more life in author George R.R. Martin's fantasy universe after [_Game of Thrones_](https://www.polygon.com/game-of-thrones/2019/5/22/18634730/game-of-thrones-finale-explained-references-callbacks/)' disastrous series finale, [_A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms_](https://www.polygon.com/knight-of-the-seven-kingdoms-finale-analysis-george-rr-martin/) confirmed that universe was large enough to contain stories both big and small. So what's next for the franchise? It looks like we might be getting a movie, and the latest news indicates that the movie could be attached to arguably the best possible screenwriter for the job.

[Page Six](https://pagesix.com/2026/03/03/hollywood/game-of-thrones-movie-in-the-works-after-acclaimed-screenwriter-submits-prequel-script-to-warner-bros/) reports that Beau Willimon (_[House of Cards](https://www.polygon.com/2018/11/6/18067870/house-of-cards-season-6-review/), [Andor](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/562903/andor-episode-1-3-review/)_) recently submitted a script to Warner Bros. for a Game of Thrones prequel movie. [_Variety_](https://variety.com/2026/film/news/game-of-thrones-movie-warner-bros-1236678055/) notes that while the details of Willimon's screenplay are unknown, it's expected to chart the conquest of Westeros by Aegon Targaryen I, which takes place roughly 100 years before _House of the Dragon_ (and 300 years before _Game of Thrones_). This follows a report from [_The Hollywood Reporter_](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/tv/tv-features/george-rr-martin-interview-thrones-winds-dragon-knight-1236473519/) that revealed the studio was developing an adaptation of Aegon's story, either as an HBO series or a "mammoth Dune-sized feature film."

What's notable here, however, is the inclusion of Willimon, who previously helped write some of the best episodes of the critically acclaimed Star Wars series _Andor_. Specifically, he wrote the [Narkina 5 arc](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/23449962/andor-prison-break-kino-loy-episode-10/) in season 1 (three episodes set on a dystopian prison planet) and then returned in season 2 for the [Ghorman massacre](https://www.polygon.com/star-wars/563181/ghorman-andor-season-2/) (another three episodes depicting how the Empire creates a false-flag attack to squash a growing rebellion on a resource-rich planet). In both cases, he delivered what is arguably the most memorable and gripping batch of episodes in each season, while offering an on-the-ground perspective of how fascist governments grinds down the individual — and how that individual can fight back.

Bringing that sensibility to Aegon's conquest will be an interesting challenge. The story could easily be told from the Targaryen perspective as a glorious campaign of domination where dragons soar across Westeros and the Seven Kingdoms bend to Aegon's will (think _House of the Dragon_, but even bigger). Willimon's involvement suggests something different: a story of imperial conquest told from the perspective of the smallfolk.

As we've seen in _A Knight of the Seven Kingdoms_, this franchise is at its best when it tells stories from the ground level, not the towers of King's Landing. If Willimon can combine that sensibility with the epic scale of a Game of Thrones-style dragon set piece, fans may be in for something truly special.
