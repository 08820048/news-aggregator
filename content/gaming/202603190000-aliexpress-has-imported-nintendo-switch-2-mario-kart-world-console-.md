### Nintendo Switch 2 Mario Kart World Console Bundle

The Switch 2 console was released on June 5, 2025. It is backwards compatible with nearly all Switch games as well as the original Joy-Cons and Switch Pro controller, although the new Switch 2 controllers offer more functionality. In his [Nintendo Switch 2 review](https://www.ign.com/articles/nintendo-switch-2-review), Tom Marks sums up the Switch 2 as "a vital but unexciting upgrade to a console I already love."

At this point, several major Switch 2 video games have been released: Mario Kart World, Donkey Kong Bananza, Pokemon Legends: Z-A, and more recently, Kirby Air Riders and [Hyrule Warriors: Age of Imprisonment](https://www.ign.com/articles/hyrule-warriors-age-of-imprisonment-review). Several triple-A ports are also available, including [Cyberpunk 2077](https://www.ign.com/articles/cyberpunk-2077-nintendo-switch-2-review-update) and [Final Fantasy VII Remake Intergrade](https://www.ign.com/articles/final-fantasy-7-remake-intergrade-nintendo-switch-2-review-update), making the Switch 2 a veritable competitor to pricier handheld gaming PCs. The Switch 2 hardware is powerful enough to run these games at a very playable clip, something that was impossible on the original Switch's increasingly outdated hardware. Whereas there was "nothing" to play when the Switch 2 was originally released, there are now plenty of reasons to finally justify an upgrade.
