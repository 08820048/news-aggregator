_If you have new appointments or transitions in your company that belong here, please send the names of appointees, new role and company, and prior role and company to newhires@gamesindustry.biz._

## **Lori Wright and Haiyan Zhang leave Microsoft Gaming**

Lori Wright and Haiyan Zhang have announced their departures from Xbox.

Wright was CVP of partnerships, business development, and marketing for the platform. Her role covered publishing and platform partnerships, content licensing, developer programs, and marketing expansion.

Wright joined Microsoft in 2017 as vice president and general manager of Office 365 marketing. She became CVP in July 2019.

“I leave with overwhelming gratitude for the adventure of a lifetime,” said Wright. “As for what comes next, I’m hoping for a lot of beautiful sunrises and sunsets, and discovering what lies in the space in between.”

Zhang was general manager and partner of Gaming AI. She began her Xbox career in 2013 as innovation director at Xbox Studios, then moved to Microsoft Research in the same role. In 2020, she became chief of staff for Microsoft Gaming.

“After more than a decade at Microsoft, I’ve decided to step into the next phase of my career, which will take me on a new adventure beyond Microsoft,” said Zhang. She will be joining Netflix’s gaming team in an undisclosed role.

## **Steel City Interactive announces three senior hires**

Glen O’Connell, James Shaw, and Shaun White have joined Steel City Interactive in senior director roles.

O’Connell has been appointed as commercial partnerships director, Shaw as senior production director, and White as brand communications director.

O’Connell previously held senior roles in talent relations, licensing, and commercial marketing. He also led athlete, talent, and rights holder partnerships at EA Sports.

Shaw recently led sports platforms across EA Sports’ portfolio, including the EA Sports app and EA Racenet. White worked at EA Sports and Codemasters on the F1 series.

“Their combined experience, honed through years of working at the highest levels of entertainment, sport, and interactive media, will play a vital role in helping us deliver the ultimate boxing experience for millions of fans around the world,” said Steel City Interactive VP of studio Tim Coupe.

## **Magali Huot | Director of games marketing, Netflix**

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/Magali-Huot.jpg)

Magali Huot

Netflix has appointed Magali Huot as director of games marketing for mainstream games.

Before joining Netflix, Huot was SVP of global gaming strategy at advertising agency Dentsu. She joined Dentsu in 2023 as VP of global gaming strategy.

Huot previously worked at Warner Bros Entertainment Group as director of content marketing and at YouTube Gaming as head of games publishers. She then became senior gaming product marketing manager at the video-sharing platform.

## **Ryan Lastimosa | Studio art director, Devoted Studios**

Ryan Lastimosa has joined Devoted Studios as studio art director.

In his new role, Lastimosa will support Devoted’s partners with “high-level art direction, creative strategy, and hands-on collaboration across projects.”

“I am looking forward to making some magic with this exceptional group of talented, world-class game developers,” he said.

Lastimosa has more than two decades of industry experience, having worked on Apex Legends, Call of Duty, and Titanfall.

He was a founding member of Respawn Entertainment and became creative director in 2022. After leaving Respawn a year later, he founded Sentinel Games and served as its CCO.

## **Michael Borras | Vice president of business development, Arc Games**

Michael Borras has joined Arc Games as vice president of business development.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/1739386084833.jpg)

Michael Borras

“Over the coming weeks I’m taking over all new business development and scouting initiatives, focused on partnering with incredible indie developers around the world,” said Borras. “I’ll also orbit discussions around platform relationships, investments and M&A.”

“Much appreciation to Yoon Im and Jason Park for the trust and opportunity to help accelerate Arc Games’ growth.”

Borras was previously head of global business development at Bitwise Academy. He co-founded Rare Earth Games and Iron Mountain Interactive and was CEO of both developers.

Earlier in his career, Borras worked at Rockstar Games for three years in QA & localisation production.

## **New hire and promotions at Raptor PR**

Raptor PR has announced a new hire and two promotions.

Khairat Animashaun has joined as junior account executive, while Joshua Callaghan and James Killick have been promoted to account manager and media relations manager.

Callaghan started at Raptor PR in 2022 as public relations assistant intern, then joined full-time as junior account executive.

Killick started as public relations account manager in July 2025. He previously worked at CLPR Agency as public relations manager.

## 

**Three new execs at ProbablyMonsters**

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/mark%2Cjonathan%2Cdavid.jpg)

Image credit: Left to right; Mark Subotnick, Jonathan Lander, David Reid

ProbablyMonsters has made three new appointments on its leadership team: Jonathan Lander as chief publishing officer, David Reid as chief marketing officer, and Mark Subotnick as chief product officer.

Lander previously served as Bethesda Softworks' senior vice president of global product and publishing operations.

Reid has more than two decades of publishing marketing leadership experience, including senior roles at Behaviour Interactive, CCP Games, Trion Worlds, and Xbox Platform Marketing.

Subotnick was head of studios at ProbablyMonsters. In his new role as CPO, he "ensures creative direction and production discipline stay aligned across the portfolio."

"As we continue building a balanced portfolio of original games, strong product and publishing leadership is essential," said ProbablyMonsters CEO and founder Harold Ryan.

"Our strategy is grounded in focus and discipline, with a long-term lens. Jonathan, David, and Mark bring the experience and judgment needed to help execute at a high level and deliver great games to market with clarity.

"We are committed to building an environment where talented developers can do their best work, and that requires both meaningful support and a high performance bar."

**Timea Edvi | Publishing director, Kasedo Games**

Timea Edvi has joined Kasedo Games as publishing director.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/kase-1.jpg)

Timea Edvi

Edvi has over twenty years of experience in games publishing, platform partnerships, and indie development support.

She was previously head of Square Enix Collective, where she oversaw projects and partnerships such as PowerWash Simulator and Little Goody Two Shoes.

"I'm absolutely thrilled to be joining Kasedo Games at such an exciting time," said Edvi.

"Kasedo has built a strong reputation for publishing thoughtful, system-driven indie strategy titles and working closely with talented development teams.

I'm looking forward to collaborating with our partners and the wider Kalypso Media Group to expand the label's global footprint and bring exceptional new games to players around the world."

## 

**Sixteen Tons appoints Sebastian Nußbaum as CCO**

Sixteen Tons has appointed Sebastian Nußbaum as chief creative officer.

Nußbaum previously served as Wooga's VP of creative, and was game director on June's Journey. He is joining Sixteen Tons' leadership team "to help drive the next phase of growth for its Emergency franchise.

"What Jan Richter and the team have achieved with Emergency over the past months is truly impressive," said Nußbaum. "The dynamic growth, the clarity of the strategic direction, and the strength of the team made it clear to me that something special is happening at Sixteen Tons.

"Emergency holds enormous potential, and I am convinced that my experience can meaningfully contribute to the continued evolution of this brand."

Alison Zeng has joined Microsoft Gaming as strategic partner manager.

## 

**Midwest Game names Adam Orth as COO**

Adam Orth has been named COO at Midwest Games.

Orth's new role will see him "focusing on company operations, partnerships, business development, and building out the Midwest Games network of experienced publishing resources."

He has held director and founder roles at startups and global video game, entertainment, and technology companies, including Sony Santa Monica, Microsoft, Electronic Arts, and LucasArts.

Cisco Maldonado and Charlotte Cook have also joined Midwest as executive advisors to "support key areas of business strategy."

Maldonado previously held senior leadership roles at NetEase Games, Bandai Namco, and DC Comics/Warner Bros, while Cook was global commercial director at Team17.
