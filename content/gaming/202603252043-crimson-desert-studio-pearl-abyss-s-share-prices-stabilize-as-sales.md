The South Korean developer's shares have fully recovered from a 27.4% drop last week

         ![A person faces off against a large furry monster with deer-like antlers in a snowy forest](https://assetsio.gnwcdn.com/crimsondesert.jpg?width=570&quality=85&format=jpg&dpr=3&auto=webp)

Crimson Desert developer Pearl Abyss has seen its share price stabilize after confirming the new action-adventure game has sold three million copies.

When the review embargo lifted last week, revealing a mixed critical reception, share prices [plummeted](https://www.google.com/finance/quote/263750:KOSDAQ?window=5D) by 27.4%.

Since then, however, the developer has responded promptly to feedback, releasing [three patches](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/3321460) between last week's release day and today, the most recent of which rolled out yesterday (March 24).

According to video games industry consultant Dr. Serkan Toto, that – coupled with the sales [milestone](https://x.com/CrimsonDesert_/status/2036385054570717202) – has restored investor confidence, and within a day of announcing the three million sales, share prices have almost fully recovered, bouncing up 27.8% over the course of the day (thanks, [Eurogamer](https://www.eurogamer.net/crimson-deserts-pearl-abyss-regains-investor-confidence-as-it-continues-to-move-beyond-launch-day-woes)).

Crimson Desert launched last Thursday (March 19), dividing critics. In our [critical consensus](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/crimson-desert-critical-consensus), some praised the RPG's combat, while others found its story underbaked and gameplay overwhelming.

Last week, Crimson Desert developer Pearl Abyss confirmed it would [conduct a "comprehensive audit" of its in-game assets following the "unintentional" inclusion of AI-generated art](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/pearl-abyss-to-conduct-comprehensive-audit-after-ai-generated-art-found-in-crimson-desert).
