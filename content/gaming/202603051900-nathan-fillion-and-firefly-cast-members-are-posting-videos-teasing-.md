## Nathan Fillion is teasing a Firefly reunion in cryptic video posts with his former crew

   ![A group shot of cast members of Firefly in costume and in the cockpit of their spaceship in the movie Serenity](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/firefly-cast.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Universal Pictures

It's been 23 years since Fox summarily canceled Joss Whedon's single-season science fiction show _Firefly_ [before even airing all its episodes](https://www.reddit.com/r/firefly/comments/lju5hq/why_was_firefly_really_taken_off_the_air/), and 21 years since the spin-off movie _Serenity_ brought the cast back together for what seemed like a final story. The series earned a [passionate fan base](http://www.fireflyfans.net/) during its short run, and was popular enough to spark a series of [spin-off novels, comics, and graphic novels](https://www.goodreads.com/list/show/202928.Firefly_Serenity_Books). Since then, there have been [convention cast reunions](https://www.polygon.com/2015/10/10/9493867/firefly-reunion-panel-cast-nycc-2015/) and [periodic rumors of a possible series revival](https://www.polygon.com/2020/1/9/21058954/new-firefly-episodes-rumor-fox-tim-minear-joss-whedon/), but nothing more concrete. For the past week, though, _Firefly_ star Nathan Fillion has been teasing something new for the _Firefly_ crew.

Fillion, who played swaggering former rebel leader Mal Reynolds on the show, has been getting _Firefly_ fans' hopes up by [posting cryptic videos on Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/nathanfillion/), where he visits his former castmates at what appear to be their homes or the sets of their current projects. Each of these videos has some reference to the effect of "It's time," or "It's happening," followed by the two _Firefly_ actors sharing a meaningful silence, while nodding, staring off into the distance, or making dramatic eye contact.

So far, Fillion has posted videos with _Firefly_ co-stars Gina Torres (who played Mal's second-in-command Zoë Washburne), Morena Baccarin (Inara Serra), Sean Maher (Dr. Simon Tam), Summer Glau (River Tam), and in the most recent video, Jewel Staite (Kaylee Frye).

Comments on these videos from another Instagram account, "[oncewewerespacemen](https://www.instagram.com/oncewewerespacemen/)" — the official account of a podcast Fillion records with _Firefly_ co-star Alan Tudyk (Hoban "Wash" Washburne) — have promised an announcement to come on Sunday, March 15. The account has also debunked a few fans' guesses about what Fillion is teasing: "Some of you have guessed convention, podcast, or cross-over. You are wrong," the account posted on the Sean Maher video.

So far, the _Firefly_ fandom seems reasonably cautious about these teases. While some may hold out hope for an actual 20-years-later _Firefly_ series continuation, this coy, teasing lead-up on an actor's personal social media account doesn't seem like the way 20th Century Studios would announce a prominent project.

It also might seem hard to imagine a _Firefly_ spin-off proceeding without Whedon, given how much the series centered on his specific writing voice, sense of humor, and narrative quirks. Whedon who disappeared from film and TV in 2021, after being accused of [abusive behavior and sexual misconduct](https://www.vulture.com/2021/05/complete-timeline-joss-whedon-allegations.html) on the sets of _Justice League_, _Buffy the Vampire Slayer_, and other projects, and giving a [disastrous response interview](https://www.vulture.com/article/joss-whedon-allegations.html) in response. He's largely been silent and absent from public life since then. Then again, Hulu is currently moving forward with [a _Buffy The Vampire Slayer_ revival/sequel series](https://www.polygon.com/news/518576/buffy-the-vampire-slayer-reboot-sequel-sarah-michelle-gellar/), reportedly with no involvement from Whedon.

A full _Firefly_ revival would also inevitably be bittersweet for fans, given that two of the series' close-knit ensemble of rebels died in _Serenity_: Tudyk's character Wash and Glass' character, Shepherd Book. Glass himself died in 2016, but Tudyk continues to have an active career, often specializing in dramatically transformative voice roles (for instance, as K-2SO in [_Rogue One: A Star Wars Story_](https://www.polygon.com/2016/12/13/13869428/rogue-one-star-wars-story-review/) and [_Andor_](https://www.polygon.com/2016/12/1/13808670/star-wars-rogue-one-alan-tudyk-firefly-uncharted/), Gary the robot in [James Gunn's _Superman_](https://www.polygon.com/review/611560/superman-james-gunn-dcu-reboot-review/), and a host of roles on [_Harley Quinn_](https://www.polygon.com/reviews/23283048/harley-quinn-season-3-review/)). So it's always possible he could cameo as a new character, possibly in voiceover. But Wash was enough of a fan favorite that continuing the show without him would likely provoke some negativity and disappointment for a nostalgia-driven audience.

It seems far more likely that the _Firefly_ cast is planning some sort of joint project or appearance together — and the fact that it's being set up in such a circuitous fashion, in a way designed to get fans theorizing and hoping, suggests it might wind up being a small enough event that the participants felt just announcing it wouldn't make enough of a splash among their followers.

But I'm ready to be wrong about that. If this series of videos ends with Fillion visiting Whedon, that'll be huge news, and something really surprising to see. In the meantime, it seems like Fillion's followers are intrigued, but already feeling a little defensive about being baited this way. "I hope you all recognize that most of your fan base is currently perimenopausal and hanging on by the threads of our [pretty floral bonnets](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pScjtvw1r7c)," Instagram user Jen Brophy posted. "If this is a tease, we will make \[_Firefly_ cannibal villains\] reavers look like golden retriever puppies."
