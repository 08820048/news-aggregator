Pixelberry Studios, the developer of the hugely-successful episodic Choices mobile series, has allegedly laid off staff.

Though the studio has not made any public statements at the time of writing, [GameDeveloper](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/report-nexon-owned-mobile-studio-pixelberry-lays-off-staff) reports [several](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/emi-moore_opentowork-share-7443357992055545856-vFbK/) [former](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/olivia-pourzia-6479a978_opentowork-activity-7443110549258797057-KJ3C/) [staff](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/isador-pinto-881b2a15b_another-layoff-hit-choices-this-morning-activity-7443038653938810880-J1G3/) have shared news of their redundancies on LinkedIn at the end of last week, with one Pixelberry developer writing that "another layoff hit Choices \[on Friday\]."

It's unclear how many staff have been impacted, or what support they've been offered.

"\[I\]f you ever see another Pixelberry alum come across your desk, know that they are some of the most brilliant, talented, and hard working people you could hire," [wrote](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/emi-moore_opentowork-share-7443357992055545856-vFbK/) senior game writer, Emi Moore, who was also affected by the cuts.

"They will do amazing things and honestly I'm so sad I won't get to come in everyday and see what amazing things we can make anymore."

[Series Entertainment acquired story-driven, episodic mobile game studio in July 2024](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/series-entertainment-acquires-mobile-game-studio-pixelberry-studios). At the time, Series said the acquisition "signifies Series' strategy to build out its studio system to deliver a diverse catalog of different genres that leverage its world class development technology, the Rho Engine", the world’s first "AI-native, multimodal full-stack game creation platform".

While layoffs were not formally announced at the time, that same month [several former Pixelberry devs announced they had banded together to form Candlelight Games](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/former-pixelberry-devs-band-together-to-form-candlelight-games).
