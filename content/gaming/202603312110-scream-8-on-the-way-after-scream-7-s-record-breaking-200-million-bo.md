Scream 8 is reportedly in the works, following the record-breaking box office success of [Scream 7](https://www.ign.com/movies/scream-7).

[Deadline](https://deadline.com/2026/03/scream-8-writers-zuckerman-sisters-1236770273/) said that Spyglass is working on Scream 8 with Poker Face co-showrunners Lilla and Nora Zuckerman writing.

Scream 7 made over $200 million at the global box office, setting a record for the franchise in the process. The first Scream film made $173 million worldwide from theaters back in 1996, unadjusted for inflation.

Nostalgia played a big part in Scream 7’s success. Neve Campbell, who stars as franchise protagonist Sidney Prescott, returned for Scream 7 after skipping Scream 6 over a pay dispute. According to [Variety](https://variety.com/2026/film/box-office/scream-7-rewrites-neve-campbell-salary-melissa-barrera-firing-1236671347/), Campbell secured a nearly $7 million deal for Scream 7, whereas Courteney Cox, who plays reporter and veteran murder spree survivor Gale Weathers, was paid $2 million.

News of Scream 8 comes as little surprise, then. Speaking earlier this month, Scream 7 writer and director Kevin Williamson revealed [Neve Campbell already had a “great” idea for Scream 8](https://www.ign.com/articles/neve-campbell-has-a-great-idea-for-scream-8-and-it-will-be-made-if-scream-7-works-out), which he promised would be made as long as Scream 7 was a success. Well, that didn't take long!
