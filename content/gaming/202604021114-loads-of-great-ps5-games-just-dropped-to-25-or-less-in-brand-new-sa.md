## Best PS5 Games Under £25 at Amazon This Week

Say what you want about Assassin's Creed Valhalla, but that's a whole lot of game time for just £15. I loved my time with it personally, treating it as my own little MMO with content I can nip away at over a long time.

There's plenty to do here, but you might get burnt out if you try to gamerfy it and complete it in a weekend. I'd suggest taking your time and turning off as many of the HUD icons and guidance as possible. Keep things simple, and go where your interests take you.

One of my other top picks right now is the deal on Call of Duty: Black Ops 7, which drops to just £21.99 for the PS4 version. Stop writing that comment, it's a cross-gen game, and includes a free upgrade to the PS5 copy once you pop it into the console. At £22, that's a tremendous drop in price, if I do say so myself.

BO7 hasn’t exactly set the world on fire like CoD usually would ([see everyone flocking back to 2019's Modern Warfare](https://www.ign.com/articles/2019s-call-of-duty-modern-warfare-is-blowing-up-on-steam-thanks-to-a-big-sale-and-positive-word-of-mouth)) but it’s still a mighty good time if you can get a few mates together and rock out on Zombies every once in a while. And trust me, while the campaign is a little lacking, Zombies in BO7 is surprisingly great. I have truly had an awesome time with it since its release. In many ways, I’d say it even betters the pinnacles of Black Ops 3 Zombies, which is no small feat.
