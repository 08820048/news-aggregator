## The secret squad behind Breath of the Wild is even more involved in the next Zelda game

   ![Link crouches down while wearing a blue tunic in key art for Zelda Tears of the Kingdom.](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/chorus/uploads/chorus_asset/file/24652437/Tears_of_the_Kingdom_Guide_and_Walkthrough_Lede.jpg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Nintendo EPD/Nintendo

As rumors swirl that [Nintendo](https://www.polygon.com/nintendo/) is preparing to release [a remake of the classic _The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time_ for the Switch 2](https://www.polygon.com/zelda-ocarina-of-time-remake-rumor-nintendo-switch-2-2026/), Monolith Soft, which collaborated closely with Nintendo's internal teams on both [_Breath of the Wild_](https://www.polygon.com/game/the-legend-of-zelda-breath-of-the-wild/8645/) and [_Tears of the Kingdom_](https://www.polygon.com/legend-zelda-tears-kingdom/), has confirmed that it's still working actively on the Zelda series.

The Xenoblade Chronicles studio, which has been wholly owned by Nintendo since 2007, has [shared a series of Zelda-themed interviews](https://www.monolithsoft.co.jp/interview/dev01/index.html) on its website. Prefacing the interviews, the developer said (via machine translation), "Moving forward, as a member of the team creating the world of 'The Legend of Zelda' with Nintendo, we will continue to develop games that pursue even more 'new surprises and emotions.'" Monolith Soft is currently hiring across several disciplines in both its Tokyo and Kyoto offices.

It's hardly surprising that new Zelda games are in development, or that Monolith Soft is helping out with them. The developer was instrumental in the production of [_Breath of the Wild_](https://www.polygon.com/2017/3/2/14753500/the-legend-of-zelda-breath-of-the-wild-review-nintendo-switch-wii-u/) and [_Tears of the Kingdom_](https://www.polygon.com/reviews/23718684/zelda-tears-of-the-kingdom-review-switch-totk/); both games were conceived on a much larger scale than anything Nintendo's famed but relatively small internal teams had attempted before. As the interviews detail, Monolith Soft — which had also done some work on earlier Zelda games _Skyward Sword_ and _A Link Between Worlds_ — came on board _Breath of the Wild_ after its design and visual direction had been set in stone. But as development continued on the game and into _Tears of the Kingdom_, Monolith Soft and Nintendo collaborated more closely, with Monolith Soft designers and programmers joining the effort.

According to producers from both Monolith Soft and Nintendo, Monolith Soft is taking an even more active role in wherever Zelda is going next. "With each title, \[Monolith Soft's\] involvement in the creative aspects of development has increased, which I find very encouraging," said Nintendo producer Daiki Iwamoto. "The \[Monolith Soft Zelda\] team is still in its developmental stages, and we feel like we're all building the game together, and also building the team together," said Yasuhiro Fujita, a Monolith Soft producer. "I want to be able to take on even more diverse responsibilities."

"We want Monolith Soft to take on more and more core roles as a strong partner in creating Zelda from the ground up," agreed Iwamoto. "Based on the know-how we've cultivated while working together so far, let's further enhance the overall team strength at Monolith Soft and

continue to create unique series titles together in the future."

The [full chat between Iwamoto and Fujita](https://www.monolithsoft.co.jp/interview/dev01/collab-talk.html) is well worth reading; they're frank about the differences between Monolith Soft's tightly organized, high-volume development style and Nintendo's more free form, trial-and-error approach, and how both were needed to get _Breath of the Wild_ out of the door. "_Breath of the Wild_ involved an unprecedented amount of work for both of us, but thanks to the synergistic effect of collaboration between two companies that excel in different styles, we were able to overcome the challenge without compromising on either quality or quantity until the very end," Nintendo's Iwamoto said.

You can't argue with the result, or the brilliant elaboration on it in _Tears of the Kingdom_. Whether the next major Zelda title will be an _Ocarina of Time_ remake or something else, the continuing, ever-closer collaboration between Nintendo and Monolith Soft is reassuring; they're on one hell of a hot streak.
