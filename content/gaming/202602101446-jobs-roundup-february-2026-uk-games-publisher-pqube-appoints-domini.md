## **Dominic Wheatley | Chairman, PQube**

UK games publisher and distributor PQube has appointed industry veteran Dominic Wheatley as chairman.

In his new role, Wheatley will work closely with PQube's leadership team "to support the company's strategic growth and expansion into new markets."

Wheatley recently served as chairman of Blaze Entertainment, known for its Evercade and HyperMegaTech brands.

He was previously CEO of the Catalis Group and chairman of Kuju Entertainment, where he oversaw the expansion of the company's business. This included bringing UK publisher Curve Games into the group.

Wheatley is best known as the co-founder of Eidos, where he oversaw the launch of franchises such as Tomb Raider and Championship Manager.

"PQube has built an impressive reputation for working closely with talented developers and bringing distinctive, high-quality games to audiences around the world," said Wheatley.

"I'm excited to be joining the company at this stage of its journey and look forward to supporting the team as PQube continues to grow its portfolio and global reach."

PQube Founder and CEO Dave Pain added: "Dominic is one of the most accomplished and respected leaders in the global games industry.

"His track record of building iconic publishing businesses, scaling international teams and nurturing long-term creative partnerships speaks for itself. We're delighted to welcome him as chairman, and his experience and insight will be invaluable as PQube continues to grow, explore new markets and expand the reach of our portfolio worldwide."

## **Christie Golden | Principal writer, IO Interactive**

Christie Golden has been named as the principal writer at IO Interactive.

In her new role, Golden "will work closely with creative leadership and development teams" at IO, "contributing to narrative vision, storytelling, worldbuilding, and character development."

Golden is recognised for her work across games, novels, and transmedia projects on franchises including World of Warcraft, Star Wars, Assassin's Creed, and Overwatch.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/christie-golden.jpeg)

Christie Golden

She served as a senior writer for story and franchise development at Blizzard Entertainment for six years.

"Christie is a world-class storyteller with an extraordinary ability to build rich characters and enduring worlds," said IO Interactive CCO and co-owner Christian Elverdam. "Her experience across games, novels, and major global franchises makes her a tremendous addition to our creative teams.

"We're delighted to welcome her to IO Interactive and look forward to the impact her voice and leadership will have on our stories moving forward."

## **Tobias Sjögren | Head of business development, Alinea Analytics**

Former Starbreeze Entertainment CEO Tobias Sjögren has joined Alinea Analytics as head of business development.

In this role, Sjögren will "leverage his deep institutional knowledge to accelerate Alinea's growth trajectory and give \[its\] clients dedicated, executive-grade support that only a seasoned industry leader can offer."

Sjögren stepped down as Starbreeze CEO in 2024, having held the position for three years.

He has also held leadership roles at Paradox Interactive and DICE, as well as executive positions, including CEO of Fox in a Box, VP of mobile games at Stardoll, and CEO of White Wolf Entertainment.

## **Markus Wilding joins Make-A-Wish International's Infinite Wishes Games Industry Advisory Committee**

Industry veteran Markus Wilding has joined Make-A-Wish International's Infinite Wishes Games Industry Advisory Committee.

By joining the committee, Wilding "will help strengthen engagement across global markets and support the initiative's mission to harness the power of gaming to create meaningful impact."

Wilding has over three decades of experience, having held senior PR and communications roles at Take-Two Interactive (including Rockstar Games), Activision, and 2K.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/markus-wilding.jpeg)

Markus Wilding

His most recent role was at Private Division, where he managed its international marketing and communications team for more than seven years.

"At this stage of my career, I feel a responsibility to give back in a meaningful way," said Wilding. "I've followed the work of the Make-A-Wish Foundation for many years and have always been deeply impressed by the impact they create for children and families around the world.

"Becoming an ambassador is an honour, and I’m excited to contribute by leveraging my international network to help amplify their incredible work."

April Stallings, Make-A-Wish International Charitable Gaming and Creators Community Manager, added: "We're thrilled to welcome Markus to the Infinite Wishes Advisory Committee.

His experience across global markets and his passion for supporting the games community make him a fantastic addition as we continue to build momentum for the initiative. Leaders like Markus help us open new doors, inspire collaboration, and ultimately bring more wishes to life for children around the world."

## **Mike Wallen | President and main board director, Testronic**

QA firm Testronic has appointed Mike Wallen as president and main director.

Wallen was previously chief commercial officer at Keywords, where he held a decade-long tenure.

In his new role at Testronic, Wallen will be "responsible for driving \[its\] strategic expansion across the Americas" and to work closely with the global leadership team focusing on partnerships with studios and publishers.

"We are delighted to welcome Mike to Testronic," said Testronic CEO and executive chair Sharon Baylay-Bell. "His depth of experience in the games industry and proven ability to drive growth and operational excellence will be instrumental as we continue to execute on our strategy and globally scale and grow the business.

"Mike is a strong cultural fit and a leader who shares our commitment to providing exceptional QA services to our worldwide studio and publishing partners."

Wallen added: "Testronic has built an impressive reputation in the market, and I've watched Sharon and her team successfully restructure the company across three Centres of Excellence in Europe, the US and Asia over the past year.

"I'm excited to contribute my experience and help the team continue delivering market-leading QA and localisation services to the global games industry."

## **Dorian Kieken | AI director, CD Projekt Red**

Dorian Kieken has joined CD Projekt Red as AI director.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/dorian.jpeg)

Dorian Kieken

In his new role, Kieken will be responsible for the developer's AI vision and strategy as it continues development on its Cyberpunk and Witcher franchises.

Kieken previously worked at BioWare for eight years, starting in 2007 as a producer. He co-founded its Montréal studio and worked as development director and project development director.

Prior to joining BioWare, Kieken worked at Behaviour Interactive, Ubisoft, and DC Studios in design and production roles, and served as an advisor to Insolent Games.

Kieken founded AI startup AI Redefined in 2017, which focused built software for humans and AI "to train, collaborate, and experiment."

## **Emily Pestell | Senior brand communications manager, King**

Emily Pestell has started a new position at King as senior brand communications manager.

Pestell joined King in 2022 as part of its global corporate and internal communications team.

"This is a team that consistently delivers, smart thinking, beautifully executed work, and campaigns that genuinely resonate," said Pestell.

"The standard is high, the ambition is clear, and the output speaks for itself. It's a privilege to join a group that pairs creativity with real commercial impact (and makes it look effortless). New chapter. Same enthusiasm. Slightly bigger playground."

Her first role in the games industry was as a junior PR manager at Nintendo UK, a position she held for over five years.

Pestell also worked at Activision Blizzard and Samsung Electronics in senior PR roles.

## **Pocket Gamer parent company Steel Media announces leadership transitions**

Steel Media CEO Chris James is stepping into the role of chairman, while COO Dave Bradley has been appointed general manager.

James co-founded Pocket Gamer in 2006 and has served as the brand's managing director and editor-in-chief since then.

In his new role as chairman, James will "focus on strategic oversight and international expansion" and "explore new territories and partnerships to support the company's continued global growth."

"After 20 years at the wheel, it does feel like a good opportunity to step back from the front line and take some time to think about the bigger picture and consider the wider landscape of industry opportunities from a different vantage point," said James.

"Of course, this sort of move is only possible if you have a strong senior management team, and I’m super confident that in Dave Bradley, Steel Media has the perfect successor to lead from the front. He's been a key part of the management team that has got us where we are today."

Bradley joined Steel Media in 2016 as operations manager. He was formerly a magazine journalist with nearly three decades of experience across games, technology, film, and TV.

"It's a privilege to step into a leadership role at a company so beloved by the industry," said Bradley. "Following in his footsteps is an exciting challenge, and I’m looking forward to seeing how our websites and conferences can evolve. It’s an honour to be part of a team that is so forward-looking and has developed such an international influence."

## **Guy DeRosa | Business development director, Abstraction**

Guy DeRosa has been appointed business development director at Dutch studio Abstraction.

DeRosa was previously head of business development at Electric Square and Lively (Keywords Studios) for almost six years.

He was also head of talent at Studio Gobo, and head of business development at Skillsearch. DeRosa joined the latter in 2012 as games and interactive manager.

## **Jay Sullivan | CEO, Fandom**

Jay Sullivan has been appointed CEO of media platform Fandom.

Sullivan has two decades of experience in "helping build and launch technology products that have become an integral part of culture and achieved mass scale."

Prior to joining Fandom, Sullivan was general manager of Twitter's consumer and revenue products.

He has held executive positions at Facebook, where he led development of its Reality Labs' AI Assistant, then led its Privacy, Integrity, and System product teams for Messenger and Instagram Direct.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/Sullivan%2C-Jay.jpeg)

Jay Sullivan

Sullivan was also chief product officer at Groupon, and SVP of product then chief operating officer and interim CEO at Mozilla.

"Jay Sullivan's experience working on products with massive reach, and his understanding of the power of community, makes him the perfect leader to take Fandom into the future," said Fandom and Wikipedia founder Jimmy Wales.

"I am looking forward to seeing how the platform continues to grow and thrive, and better serve the Fandom community, under his stewardship."

Sullivan added: "I've always been drawn to building products that millions of people use every day – and that also serve as platforms others can build on.

"Across the entertainment and gaming industry, Fandom is uniquely positioned to operate at a massive scale as both the destination for fan communities and a platform for the industry to connect with those fans. I'm excited to work alongside the Fandom team, our community, and industry partners to continue building this ecosystem together.”

## **Julien Wera | Chief operating officer, Shiro Games**

Julien Wera has joined Shiro Games as chief operating officer.

In this role, Wera will oversee all studio operations in addition marketing, distribution, and publishing over internally developed titles and third-party publishing.

Wera has held various leadership positions across the games industry, including at Ubisoft, Dice, and Paradox Interactive. He also co-founded Moon Rover Games, where he served as CEO.

"As a studio, Shiro has always had a special place in my heart since its very first days," said Wera.

"I have spent countless hours over the years talking with its founders Sebastien Vidal and Nicolas Cannasse about the trials and tribulations of making games and leading game companies."

He added: "I'm very happy to be joining the studio at an exciting time, with Wartales going strong in its 5th year post-launch, new games SpaceCraft and Farever in development with our own teams, Frostrail coming from our partners at FakeFish Oy through our third-party publishing team, and more that hasn't been publicly announced yet."

## **Mervin Lee Kwai | Vice president of operations, NC America**

NC America has hired Mervin Lee Kwai as vice president of operations.

Kwai will help lead the global launch of Aion 2 and "steer the current line-up" of the Korean developer and publisher's legacy titles, including Aion, Blade & Soul, and Lineage 2.

"His MMO publishing expertise strengthens NC America's prospect as it heads into a busy calendar year with launches of Aion 2, Cinder City, Limit Zero Breakers, and Time Breakers," the company said.

![](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/NCA_MervinLeeKwai_headshot_2.png)

Mervin Lee Kwai

Kwai is a 23-year veteran of the North American and European games industry. This includes leadership roles at Amazon Games, Sony Online Entertainment, and Trion Worlds.

"Mervin's particular blend of game industry executive skills and deep understanding of MMORPGs in the modern market make him the perfect candidate for NC America’s strategic push to deliver our MMOs to the global stage," said NC America CEO and NC West/Arenanet CCO Jeonghee 'JJ' Jin.

Kwai added: "Ever since our very first conversations, joining NC America just felt right. I already have a strong relationship with the Throne and Liberty development team and look forward to globally launching NCsoft’s upcoming titles, starting with Aion 2."

## **Joshua Mills | Creative director (Rainbow Six Siege), Ubisoft Montréal**

Joshua Mills has taken over from Alexander Karpazis as creative director on Rainbow Six Siege.

Mills joined Ubisoft Montréal in 2015 as senior level designer on Watch Dogs 2, becoming lead level designer on Assassin's Creed Valhalla and an unannounced project in 2019.

He started working on Rainbow Six Siege in 2022 as associate game director, then game director in 2024.

Mills previously worked at Compulsion Games for four years in level and game design roles.

"Joshua and I have worked together for many years," said Karpazis. "Not only is he a great friend, but he has proven himself to be a strong leader and a champion of Siege values.

"We have been working on this transition of responsibilities for over a year, and I am confident he will lead this game to even greater heights. I will let him introduce himself in his new role below."

Mills added: "It has been an incredible journey to get to this point. I’ve had the honour of representing Rainbow over the past four years and working alongside one of my favourite people: Alexander Karpazis.

"Thank you to all my mentors, friends and of course, my family for supporting me through all the long hours, business trips, and let’s be honest – almost obsession – to my work. I look forward to continuing to put everything I can into Siege and to continue to entertain and enrich others’ lives through the medium that has given me so much."
