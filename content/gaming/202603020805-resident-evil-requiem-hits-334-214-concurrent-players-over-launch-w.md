Number of players on Steam is triple that of Requiem's predecessor, Resident Evil: Village

         ![](https://assetsio.gnwcdn.com/re9_qyrVSI1.jpg?width=570&quality=85&format=jpg&dpr=3&auto=webp)

Image credit: Capcom

Resident Evil: Requiem reached an all-time concurrent peak of 334,214 players during its debut weekend, the biggest in the franchise's history.

According to [SteamDB](https://steamdb.info/app/3764200/charts/), the game reached this peak on Saturday (February 28), a day after its launch.

As noted by our sister site [Eurogamer](https://www.eurogamer.net/resident-evil-requiems-steam-concurrent-peak-is-the-biggest-in-franchise-history), this record is over double that of [Resident Evil 4 Remake](https://steamdb.info/app/2050650/charts/) (168,191) and triple that of Requiem's predecessor [Resident Evil Village](https://steamdb.info/app/1196590/charts/) (106,631).

Looking across the franchise on SteamDB, there's been a gradual increase in players for both RE4 Remake and RE Village since February 15, 2026.

Steam is one of several platforms Requiem launched on, including the Epic Game Store, PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and Nintendo Switch 2.

Requiem has been well received, with critics praising an overall nostalgic return to the franchise's roots. You can read more in [our critical consensus here](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/resident-evil-requiem-critical-consensus-1).

The game has been [a major financial boost for Capcom](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/resident-evil-and-street-fighter-franchises-boost-capcoms-latest-financials), even before launch, increasing [sales of legacy titles](https://www.gamesindustry.biz/resident-evil-requiem-shows-how-sequels-can-drive-sales-of-previous-releases) in the franchise, including Resident Evil 7 and Resident Evil Village.
