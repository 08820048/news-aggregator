While March is off to a strong start for new games already, with [_Pokémon Pokopia_](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-pokopia-review/), [_Slay the Spire 2_](https://www.polygon.com/slay-spire-2-steam-concurrents-record/), and [_Marathon_](https://www.polygon.com/marathon-hardcore-gamers-new-favorite-shooter/) all releasing this past week, none of those are on Xbox Game Pass. Neither is February's [_Resident Evil Requiem_](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem/), one of the year's best games so far (and one that [might end up in the GOTY conversation](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-goty-contender-2026/) by year's end). But you know what is on Game Pass? A bounty of excellent games well worth checking out.

This week's recommendations include a precursor to _Requiem_, an indie hit, and a must-play roguelike deckbuilder.

## Resident Evil 2

Everyone’s talking about _[Resident Evil Requiem](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-review/)_ right now, partly because it’s become the [fastest-selling](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-sales/) game in the series’s history, and mostly because grown-up Leon looks [incredibly hot](https://www.polygon.com/resident-evil-requiem-leon-kennedy-thirst/). Whether you’ve already finished _Requiem_ or you’re holding out for a sale, now’s the perfect time to revisit Capcom’s stellar _Resident Evil 2_ remake on Game Pass. As rookie cop Leon, you’re tasked with escaping Raccoon City police headquarters and uncovering the nefarious secrets of the Umbrella Corporation. _RE2_ is a thrilling, exquisitely paced adventure that you can easily devour in a weekend. And if you’re left wanting more, you can double your pleasure by playing Claire Redfield’s route. —_Jen Glennon_

## Planet of Lana 2: Children of the Leaf

I love me a stylish and cinematic indie puzzle-platformer, and few are as stylish and cinematic as the newly released [_Planet of Lana 2_](https://www.polygon.com/planet-of-lana-2-review/). [Without recognizable language](https://www.polygon.com/planet-of-lana-2-language-interview/), it tells a deeply personal narrative through expressive animation, sweeping music, and standout vocal performances of lines that to our ears sound like gibberish. The mechanics are deeper than the first game's with new ways to tackle puzzles, like using Mui's ability to hypnotize creatures to progress solutions. _Planet of Lana 2_ opens with a recap of the first game (the only told part in a recognizable language), so you don't technically need to have played the [2023 hidden gem](https://www.polygon.com/impressions/606153/planet-of-lana-impressions/) to enjoy its sequel. But, you still should as both games are available on Game Pass. —_Austin Manchester_

## Slay the Spire

_Slay the Spire 2_ just had [one of the biggest game launches of the year](https://www.polygon.com/slay-spire-2-steam-concurrents-record/). But if you're curious to hear what all the hype is about before hopping on the train, the first one's still right there. Mega Crit's 2019 roguelike deckbuilder is arguably _the_ game that kicked off the roguelike deckbuilder trend we're in the middle of right now. Its mix of cards and characters are an immediate draw for anyone who likes crafting strategy. But its the unique fantastical visuals and true depth of run variety that keep people coming back (for years). _Slay the Spire 2_ takes the original's fundamentals and builds on it, but if you want a taste of what to expect, that's what the first game offers. —_Ari Notis_
