Blizzard starts funding the next decade of Overwatch with new Kiriko and Vendetta skins

   ![overwatch x nier automata artwork](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/overwatch-x-nier-automata.jpeg?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Blizzard Entertainment, Square Enix

[_Overwatch_](https://www.polygon.com/overwatch/)'s next big crossover collaboration will bring the characters of [_Nier: Automata_](https://www.polygon.com/2017/3/6/14735646/nier-automata-review-ps4-playstation-4-windows-pc-square-enix-yoko-taro-platinum-games/) to Blizzard Entertainment's hero shooter, featuring Kiriko as 2B, Vendetta as A2, and Wuyang as 9S. Blizzard and Square Enix's new collab goes live on Tuesday, March 10.

Also included as part of the Overwatch x Project YoRHa collection are skins that imagine Lifeweaver as Adam and Mercy as Commander White. Naturally, the choices for characters on both sides of the collaboration aren't too surprising, given the number of skins that fan-favorite characters like Kiriko and Mercy receive, but _Overwatch_ and Nier fans are likely wondering where the Emil-Wrecking Ball or Pascal-Bastion combo is.

At least the Pods get some love: 2B Kiriko and 9S Wuyang are paired with Pod 042 and Pod 153, respectively.

But even those fans clamoring for more Nier representation can't deny the financial impact of bringing beloved android 2B to a video game — something that's been proven by 2B's previous guest appearances in [_Soulcalibur 6_](https://www.polygon.com/game/soulcalibur-6/40309/), [_Fall Guys_](https://www.polygon.com/game/fall-guys-ultimate-knockout/40692/), [_Final Fantasy 14_](https://www.polygon.com/game/final-fantasy-14-a-realm-reborn/3702/), _Rainbow Six Siege_, _Gravity Rush 2_, _Stellar Blade_, _Monster Hunter Frontier Z_, _Naraka: Bladepoint_, _Phantasy Star Online 2_, _Babylon's Fall_, _Granblue Fantasy Versus: Rising_, and _PUBG Battlegrounds_.

In addition to five Legendary skins, the Overwatch x Project YoRHa collab will feature a themed lobby co-designed by _Nier: Automata_ artists Kimihiko Fujisaka and Koda Kazuma, as well as a new dedicated collaboration event hub. By completing event challenges, players will be able to earn additional cosmetic rewards, including name cards, a player title, and a special weapon charm inspired by the collaboration, Blizzard says.

_Overwatch_ has crossed over with video games in the past, with [collabs based on _Street Fighter 6_](https://www.polygon.com/trailer/599851/overwatch-2-street-fighter-6-collab-skins-price/) and [_Persona 5_](https://www.polygon.com/overwatch-2-persona-5-season-18-trailer/). There are also plenty of Warcraft, StarCraft, and Diablo-themed skins for Blizzard's hero shooter. Blizzard has largely focused on anime, pop culture, and toy properties for _Overwatch_, thus far, with recent collabs focusing on [Hello Kitty and friends](https://www.polygon.com/overwatch-sanrio-skins-hello-kitty/), Nerf, and G.I. Joe.

Square Enix recently revealed that [_Nier: Automata_ has sold 10 million copies](https://www.polygon.com/nier-automata-sequel-ten-million-announcement/) since its release in 2017. The publisher also teased a future for the franchise, saying cryptically, "_Nier: Automata_ to be continued…" But the creative force behind _Nier: Automata_ — game designer Yoko Taro — has his hands full with another project: [a new _Neon Genesis Evangelion_ anime series](https://www.polygon.com/new-evangelion-anime-yoko-taro-hideaki-anno-writer/). Hopefully, this incestuous working relationship ultimately results in EVA-themed skins for the oft-overlooked mechanized members of the _Overwatch_ roster.

Ah, who am I kidding? Set aside some _Overwatch_ coins for the inevitable Kiriko Rei Ayanami skin that should arrive when the new Evangelion anime drops.
