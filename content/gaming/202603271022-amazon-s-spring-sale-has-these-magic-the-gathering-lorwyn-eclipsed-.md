[Magic: The Gathering](https://www.ign.com/games/magic-the-gathering-trading-card-game) is in the middle of the Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles set, but the first release on its [2026 roadmap](https://www.ign.com/articles/magic-the-gathering-full-release-schedule-2026), Lorwyn Eclipsed, is still worth picking up - particularly the Commander Decks.

Each one has seen discounts as part of Amazon’s Spring Sale which runs until March 31, but if you and a friend are looking to expand your arsenal of decks, [you can grab a bundle that includes two of each for $120.67](https://zdcs.link/QK8llB?object_uuid=349b35a8-0029-4ac7-9d55-35f1b07357b1&t=article).

### Lorwyn Commander Bundle

## Lorwyn Eclipsed Precons Are Down To $30 Each

This bundle includes two of each deck, so you and your friend can split the fee and get Blight Curse and Dance of the Elements for your own collections.

[Blight Curse](https://zdcs.link/9YR2Xj?object_uuid=349b35a8-0029-4ac7-9d55-35f1b07357b1&t=article) has received a discount on its own, but it’s still almost $40, while [Dance of the Elements](https://zdcs.link/zE8Bm4?object_uuid=349b35a8-0029-4ac7-9d55-35f1b07357b1&t=article) is closer to $30. That means you’re getting just over $140 of value between two copies of each deck for your $120 buy-in.

It doesn’t hurt that both decks are great in their own way, and very distinct from one another. Blight Curse is all about dishing out -1/-1 counters to make your opponents’ creatures weaker and sap their forces with Auntie Ool, or turn them to your side with Reaper, King No More.

Dance of the Elements is a five-color deck that cheats big Elemental beasts into play before sacrificing them, but if you’re smart, you can find ways to keep them around. Its two Commander options are Ashling, the Limitless, or (my preferred choice) Mass of Mysteries.

I should mention, though, that each deck is hardly suitable for beginners. If you're new to Magic, [I'd actually recommend the Turtle Power precon](https://www.ign.com/articles/magic-the-gathering-teenage-mutant-ninja-turtle-power-commander-deck-preview-impressions) as a sort of toolkit instead.
