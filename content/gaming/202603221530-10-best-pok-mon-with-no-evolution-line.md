## The best Pokémon with no evolutions, ranked

   ![bois the heracross with trainer in pokemon z-a](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/image.png?&fit=crop&w=1600&h=900)Image: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

[Evolution](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-scarlet-violet-guide/23461164/special-evolution-items-stones-pawmo-charcadet-moves-times-gender/) has been a staple of Pokémon since the franchise’s beginnings. From raising your little Charmander into a Charizard to using a team of all [different Eeveelutions](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-winds-waves-new-eevee-eeveelution-flying-type-seed-weather-leaks/), evolving Pokémon goes hand-in-hand with the series’ other gameplay pillars, like catching wild Pokémon and battling trainers. Because of that, you’d think every Pokémon would belong to an evolution line, but plenty don’t.

30 years in, dozens of Pokémon are mere one-off Pokémon who don’t evolve. Few things are more fun than pitting Pokémon against one another, so let’s sit back and rank the 10 best one-and-done Pokémon. This ranking is, of course, the product of one Poké Fan’s mind — unlike [Polygon’s ranking of the top 100 Pokémon](https://www.polygon.com/best-pokemon-ranked/) that dared to be democratic and capture the wide-ranging opinions of our team.

A few caveats: No Legendaries or Mythical Pokémon were considered, because their inclusions would be too obvious. No Paradox Pokémon either, because I love them too much and they’d claim nearly every spot. Mega Evolutions don’t count as a disqualifying mark; they’re more of a battle gimmick than wholly new evolution. (If anything, Mega Evolutions enforce the idea that these are one and done Pokémon as only the final ‘mon of an evolution line can Mega Evolve.)

Without further ado, here are the 10 best Pokémon who don’t evolve.

## 10 Klefki (#707)

    ![34-Klefki](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/34-klefki.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Plenty of people would deride Klefki for being an object Pokémon. I am not one of those people — [object Pokémon rock (especially when they’re ghosts)](https://www.polygon.com/object-pokemon-are-the-best-chandelure-klefki-drifloon/). Klefki is adorable; a sentient key ring with a surprised Pikachu face. All it wants to do is help! (And steal keys, depending on the Pokédex entry.) “This Pokémon is constantly collecting keys. Entrust a Klefki with important keys, and the Pokémon will protect them no matter what,” reads its _Pokémon Sword_ Pokédex entry.

## 9 Ditto (#132)

    ![27-Ditto](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/27-ditto.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

While Ditto may not lead your team into battle, it is wildly useful across games. Stick Ditto and another Pokémon in a day care, or just let them out in camp like in _Pokémon Scarlet_ and _Violet_, and let nature take its course. Soon enough, you’ll have an egg or six. Ditto has also taken on a starring role outside of the mainline games in recent years; it’s a weird-ass human-like henchman in the _Detective Pikachu_ film and recently starred in [_Pokémon Pokopia_](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-pokopia-review/), one of the [Switch 2’s best](https://www.polygon.com/best-switch-2-games/) and [best-selling games](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-pokopia-sales-switch-2-bestseller/).

## 8 Falinks (#870)

    ![falinks-polygon](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/falinks-polygon.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

I love Falinks because, like Dugtrio or Exeggcute, it confounds me. One Falinks is actually… six individual beings linked together? _Human Centipede_\-style? Is this a hive mind sitch or a Hydra-like monster? I don’t understand how these things work, but that doesn’t matter; they’re cute and deadly. Falinks' Mega Evolution is a point in its favor here as the six individuals combine into a Megazord-like knight with a lance and shield._Yes_.

## 7 Tauros (#128)

    ![77-Tauros](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/77-tauros.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Tauros is a powerful beast, and it being a non-bug ‘mon residing exclusively in the Safari Zone in the original games gives it a somewhat unique trait. Honestly, though, it ranks on this list because of its [Paldean regional forms](https://serebii.net/pokedex-sv/tauros/), which are just tuff as hell. They’re all an intimidating black and grey, and each one has different typing (pure Fighting, Fighting/Water combo, and Fighting/Fire combo).

## 6 Absol (#359)

    ![46-Absol](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/46-absol.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Absol — so deadly, so beautiful. A highlight of _Ruby_, _Sapphire_, and _Emerald_, Absol wields a cool AF blade on its head that can one-shot foes once Swords Dance ups its attack stat a couple of times. Its _Ruby_ dex entry reads, “Every time Absol appears before people, it is followed by a disaster such as an earthquake or a tidal wave. As a result, it came to be known as the disaster Pokémon.” Frightening stuff!... that was later retconned as Absol appearing to _warn_ of a disaster.

## 5 Tropius (#357)

    ![80-Tropius](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/80-tropius.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Tropius answers the question that’s been on all our minds from the dawn of time: What would a palm tree look like if it transformed into a dinosaur? Like Absol, Tropius is another gen three standout with a striking design, one that would fit right into the oceanic theme of the upcoming [_Pokémon Winds_ and _Waves_](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-wind-waves-gen-10-switch-2-gameplay-trailer-reveal/) (if it makes the Pokédex cut). Tropius isn’t great just in battle, but for meal breaks in between as it grows delicious fruit around its neck, apparently because it just loves fruit so much. What a guy!

## 4 Heracross (#214)

    ![5-Heracross](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/5-heracross.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Heracross may look unassuming at first blush (though [that smile _is_ to die for](https://x.com/Pokemon/status/2022747673019752852)), but the bipedal insect packs a whalloping punch. Its _Pokémon Crystal_ Pokédex entry reads, “With its Herculean powers, it can easily throw around an object that is 100 times its own weight.” Heracross gets bonus points because it might have the best shiny variant of all 1,000+ Pokémon. (A factual statement!) I mean, c’mon: [look at this pink guy](https://www.serebii.net/pokedex-swsh/heracross/). Stinkin’ cute.

## 3 Mimikyu (#778)

    ![6-Mimikyu](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/6-mimikyu.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

[Mimikyu is a fan-favorite](https://www.polygon.com/every-pokemon-is-someones-favorite-data-poll-mimikyu-data-results/), and it’s not surprising why. It’s a creepy, ghostly take on the series’ mascot; Pikachu by the way of Stephen King. Though, it’s not actually trying to pull a Drifloon and kidnap any kids. It’s actually quite lonely, with Mimikyu’s Disguised Form Pokédex entries saying it only disguises itself to get close to people and other ‘mons. Mimikyu has a unique ability in that it won’t take damage from the first hit it receives in battle. Instead, its disguise’s neck will snap, transforming Mimkyu into its revenge-seeking Busted Form.

## 2 Lapras (#131)

    ![lapras-polygon](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/lapras-polygon.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

Lapras possesses an elegance and charm that few others do. (I mean, have you _seen_ friggin’ [Gholdengo or Zubat](https://www.polygon.com/worst-pokemon-least-favorite-bad/)?) Unfortunately, the people of Kanto don’t view Lapras the way you and I do; according to its Pokédex entries, it’s continually hunted close to extinction. Why do such a thing?! All Lapras wants to do is ferry people around and sing when it’s feeling happy. And while I find _Pokémon Sword_ and _Shield_’s Gigantamax gimmick to be one of the franchise’s sillier ones, there’s no denying Lapras’s Gigantamax rocks; the shell on its back transforms into a cruise ship.

## 1 Hawlucha (#701)

    ![hawlucha-polygon](https://static0.polygonimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/hawlucha-polygon.jpg?q=49&fit=crop&w=825&dpr=2)Graphic: Polygon | Source images: Game Freak/Nintendo, The Pokémon Company

[_Pokémon Legends: Z-A_](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-legends-z-a-review/) was the first time I really used Hawlucha, and, damn, do I wish I incorporated the wrestling bird into my teams sooner in earlier games. It’s just too cool to look at it, with its head and face resembling a luchadore wrestler. Hawlucha’s Mega Evolution is [one of the best that _Legends: Z-A_ introduced](https://www.polygon.com/pokemon-z-a-mega-evolutions-ranked/), leaning even further into the lucha libre-influenced design. The best thing about Hawlucha, though, might be its signature move: Flying Press, which acts as a Fighting- and Flying-type attack, making it a flexible, high-damage-dealing move. Its Mega Hawlucha Pokédex entry states “Hawlucha flexes to show off its strength.” Yeah, you flex, Hawlucha. You’ve earned it.
