Fans of AMC's Dark Winds and the novels by Tony and Anne Hillerman's Leaphorn, Chee, & Manuelito novels will be delighted to learn that the latest Humble Book Bundle will get you 30 of these books (a $451 value) for just $18. Furthermore, this bundle supports Trees, Water & People, which strives to improve lives by helping communities to protect, conserve, and manage their natural resources.

[This Humble Bundle is now live](https://www.humblebundle.com/books/tony-anne-hillerman-leaphorn-chee-novels-harpercollins-books?srsltid=AfmBOoqdyy6IVriPnqGZ0gK4MrYQGuxOMv12tLmbnFn6o7LJ0AFHhC8v) and will run until April 3 at 11am PT / 2pm ET, and, much like other bundles, it is designed to let you pay however much you want to support a good cause and get rewarded for doing so.

The Tony and Anne Hillerman's Leaphorn, Chee, & Manuelito Book Bundle has three different tiers, and they are broken down below;

**4 Item Bundle - Pay at least $3 for these 4 items:**

-   Dance Hall of the Dead
-   Rock With Wings
-   Spider Woman's Daughter
-   The Blessing Way

**8 Item Bundle - Pay at least $10 for these 8 items:**

-   All of the Above Books and...
-   Cave of Bones
-   Listening Woman
-   People of Darkness
-   Song of the Lion

**Entire 30 Item Bundle - Pay at least $18 for these 30 items:**

-   All of the Above Books and...
-   A Thief of Time
-   Coyote Waits
-   Finding Moon
-   Hunting Badger
-   Lost Birds
-   Sacred Clowns
-   Shadow of the Solstice
-   Skeleton Man

-   Stargazer
-   Talking God
-   The Dark Wind
-   The Fallen Man
-   The First Eagle
-   The Fly on the Wall
-   The Ghostway
-   The Sacred Bridge
-   The Shape Shifter
-   The Sinister Pig
-   The Tale Teller
-   The Way of the Bear
-   The Wailing Wind

"If you enjoy mystery fiction, atmospheric novels about the American Southwest, or have suddenly found yourself obsessed with the show Dark Winds, you’re going to want to check out this bundle of Tony and Anne Hillerman’s Leaphorn & Chee detective books," the official description reads.

Humble Bundle recommends to start with The Blessing Way, to "get acquainted with Navajo Tribal Police Lieutenant Joe Leaphorn and a homicide investigation that reeks of evil."
